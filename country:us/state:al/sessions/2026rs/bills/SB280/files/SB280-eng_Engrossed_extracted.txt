9 Relating to the Retirement Systems of Alabama; to add
10 Section 36-27-49.4 to the Code of Alabama 1975, to allow
11 participating members of the Teachers' Retirement System and
12 the Employees' Retirement System to purchase certain federal
13 service credit.
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
15 Section 1. Section 36-27-49.4 is added to the Code of
16 Alabama 1975, to read as follows:
17 §36-27-49.4
18 (a)(1) A member of the Teachers' Retirement System or a
19 member of the Employees' Retirement System may receive up to
20 four years of creditable service for federal nonmilitary
21 employment rendered for the United States, provided: (i) the
22 member of the retirement system claiming the credit shall have
23 attained not less than 10 years of contributing membership
24 service credit, exclusive of military service credit, under
25 the retirement system of which he or she is a member; and (ii)
26 the member performs and complies with the conditions
27 prescribed in subdivision (2).
28 (2) Before receiving credit pursuant to subdivision (1)

29 the member shall first:
30 a. Before the date of his or her retirement and for
31 each year of service credit, contribute to his or her
32 respective retirement system the full actuarially determined
33 cost for each year of claimed service as determined by the
34 system's actuary;
35 b. Have the public retirement system of the federal
36 government certify in writing to the applicable state
37 retirement system that the member had credit under the federal
38 retirement system for the service claimed; and
39 c. Claim, purchase, and receive credit for qualified
40 federal service in increments of not less than one year. If
41 the member's total or balance of qualified federal service is
42 less than one year, he or she may claim and purchase credit
43 for the entire time period.
44 (b) Any provision of this section to the contrary
45 notwithstanding, a member may not receive credit for federal
46 employment rendered for the United States if, at the time of
47 retirement, he or she has credit or is entitled to any
48 benefits whatsoever for the same service under any other
49 retirement or pension plan which is wholly or partly funded
50 from public funds. Nothing in this subsection shall be
51 construed to apply to participation in the federal Social
52 Security program. If federal nonmilitary employment service
53 credit is not qualified, any contribution made pursuant to
54 this section by a member shall be refunded to him or her.
