5 A BILL
6 TO BE ENTITLED
7 AN ACT
8
9 Relating to consumer protections; to prohibit certain
10 health and fitness applications from disclosing, transferring,
11 or taking certain other actions with regard to a consumer's
12 biological data or neural data without the consumer's express
13 consent, with exceptions; to authorize the Office of the
14 Attorney General to enforce; and to provide a civil penalty
15 for violations.
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
17 Section 1. For the purposes of this act, the following
18 terms have the following meanings:
19 (1) BIOLOGICAL DATA. Data generated by the
20 technological processing, measurement, or analysis of an
21 individual's biological, genetic, biochemical, physiological,
22 or neural properties, compositions, or activities, or an
23 individual's body or bodily functions, which are used to
24 uniquely identify the specific individual to whom the data
25 pertains. The term does not include a photograph, video or
26 audio recording, or data generated from a photograph or audio
27 recording, unless such data is generated to uniquely identify
28 the specific individual to whom the data pertains.

29 (2) CONSUMER. Any individual who is an Alabama
30 resident.
31 (3) COVERED ENTITY. The owner or operator of a health
32 and fitness application. The term includes any individual or
33 entity that offers, maintains, owns, licenses, or controls
34 software made available to consumers through an app store or
35 website, provided that:
36 a. The software collects, processes, uses, shares,
37 sells, or generates biological data or neural data from a
38 consumer, whether alone or in combination with other personal
39 data; and
40 b. The data is collected for purposes that include
41 health tracking, wellness, sleep, fitness, mental or cognitive
42 performance, mindfulness, or stress.
43 (4) EXPRESS CONSENT. A consumer's acknowledgment or
44 permission, in writing or captured electronically, to a clear,
45 meaningful, and prominent written notice regarding the
46 disclosure or use of the consumer's biological data or neural
47 data.
48 (5) NEURAL DATA. Information that is generated by the
49 measurement of the activity of an individual's central nervous
50 system and that can be processed by or with the assistance of
51 a device.
52 (6) THIRD PARTY. An individual or legal entity that is
53 not a consumer, covered entity, or processor. For the purposes
54 of this subdivision, a processor is an entity that: (i)
55 processes biological data or neural data on behalf of a
56 covered entity; (ii) adheres to the instructions of the

57 covered entity; and (iii) assists the covered entity in

59 Section 2. (a) A covered entity may not do any of the
60 following without the express consent of the consumer:
61 (1) Transfer a consumer's biological data or neural
62 data to a third party.
63 (2) Disclose the consumer's biological data or neural
64 data to a third party for a reason other than fulfillment of
65 the entity's products or services.
66 (3) Use the consumer's biological data or neural data
67 for a purpose other than what is necessary to perform the
68 services or provide the goods reasonably expected by an
69 average consumer who requests those goods or services.
70 (4) Market to a consumer based on the consumer's
71 biological data or neural data.
72 (b) A covered entity that transfers, discloses, or uses
73 a consumer's biological data or neural data for purposes other
74 than those provided in subsection (a), before the transfer,
75 disclosure, or use, shall notify the consumer that the
76 information may be transferred, disclosed, or used for a
77 specified purpose and provide the consumer the opportunity to
78 limit or prevent the transfer, disclosure, or use of the
79 biological data or neural data.

81 authority to enforce this act.

83 action for a violation of any provision of this act, shall
84 issue a notice of violation to the covered entity accused of
