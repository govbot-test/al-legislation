4 SYNOPSIS:
5 This bill would create the Alabama Professional
6 Workforce Protection Act.
7 This bill would establish a state list of
8 professional careers based on advanced education,
9 licensing requirements, and critical workforce need.
10 This bill would also mandate that individuals
11 serving in professional careers are eligible for state
12 workforce development programs, scholarships, loan
13 repayment programs, and initiatives.

15
16 A BILL
17 TO BE ENTITLED
18 AN ACT
19
20 Relating to professional and occupational licensing; to
21 create the Alabama Professional Workforce Protection Act; to
22 provide legislative findings; to establish a state list of
23 professional careers that recognizes certain occupations
24 removed from the federal classification of professional degree
25 programs; to support Alabama workforce development in fields
26 related to professional careers; and to mandate that
27 individuals serving in professional careers are eligibile for
28 state workforce development programs, scholarships, loan

state workforce development programs, scholarships, loan

29 repayment programs, and initiatives.
30 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
31 Section 1. (a) This section shall be known and may be
32 cited as the Alabama Professional Workforce Protection Act.
33 (b) The Legislature finds and declares all of the
34 following:
35 (1) This state relies on a broad array of highly
36 skilled and licensed occupations that require advanced
37 education, internships, clinical rotations, supervised
38 practice, or board certification.
39 (2) Recent federal actions have narrowed the definition
40 of professional degree programs, excluding several occupations
41 long recognized as professional fields.
42 (3) These occupations, including nursing, education,
43 mental health, allied health, public health, engineering,
44 architecture, accounting, and others, are critical to
45 Alabama's health care, educational success, public safety, and
46 economic competitiveness.
47 (4) Alabama faces workforce shortages in many of these
48 areas and has a compelling state interest in recognizing and
49 supporting these professions.
50 (5) This state should affirm the status of these
51 occupations as professional careers to protect workforce
52 pipelines and ensure access to state-level incentives,
53 scholarships, and workforce development programs.
54 (c) For the purposes of state law, state workforce
55 development programs, state-supported scholarships, career
56 incentive programs, and any state initiative referring to
Page 2
incentive programs, and any state initiative referring to

57 professional occupations, the following shall be recognized as
58 professional careers in this state:
59 (1) Nursing including, but not limited to, registered
60 nurses, licensed practical nurses, advanced practice nurses,
61 nurse practitioners, nurse anesthetists, and nurse midwives.
62 (2) Assistants to physicians.
63 (3) Physical therapists and occupational therapists.
64 (4) Speech-language pathologists and audiologists.
65 (5) Social workers including, but not limited to,
66 licensed bachelor social workers, licensed master social
67 workers, and licensed independent clinical social workers.
68 (6) Mental health counselors including, but not limited
69 to, licensed professional counselors, licensed marriage and
70 family therapists, clinical counselors, and school counselors.
71 (7) Teachers, educators, and administrative staff,
72 including preK–12 educators and principals and higher
73 education faculty and administrators.
74 (8) Public health professionals, including MPH and
75 DrPH.
76 (9) Architecture and design professionals including,
77 but not limited to, licensed architects.
78 (10) Accounting professionals including, but not
79 limited to, certified public accountants.
80 (11) Engineering professionals in all major fields.
81 (12) Individuals who work in allied health professions
82 including, but not limited to, respiratory therapy, radiology
83 sciences, laboratory sciences, dental hygiene, and others who
84 require licensing, certification, or accredited professional

86 (13) Any additional occupation determined by the
87 Alabama Department of Workforce, in consultation with the
88 Alabama Commission on Higher Education, to meet the criteria
89 of advanced education, licensing, and critical workforce need.
90 (d) All professional careers listed in subsection (c)
91 are eligible for:
92 (1) State workforce development grants and initiatives
93 referencing professional fields.
94 (2) State-funded scholarship and loan repayment
95 programs available to professional careers.
96 (3) Strategic workforce planning reports and statewide
97 economic development plans that classify occupations by
98 professional category.
99 (e) Nothing in this section shall modify, reduce, or
100 alter the authority or requirements of any state professional
101 or occupational licensing board, agency, or commission.
102 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
