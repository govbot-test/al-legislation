4 SJR___ COMMENDING JOHN R. COOPER FOR EXEMPLARY SERVICE TO
5 THE STATE OF ALABAMA.
6
7 WHEREAS, John R. Cooper has served with distinction
8 as Director of the Alabama Department of Transportation,
9 providing stable and effective leadership for a period
10 unmatched in Alabama's history; and
11 WHEREAS, John R. Cooper is the Department's
12 longest-serving Transportation Director in total and in
13 consecutive years; and
14 WHEREAS, John R. Cooper began his tenure with the
15 Department in January 2011; and
16 WHEREAS, John R. Cooper was honored in February 2026
17 by the American Association of State Highway and
18 Transportation Officials as the longest currently tenured
19 chief executive officer of any state transportation
20 department in the United States; and
21 WHEREAS, during the first 100 years of the Alabama
22 Department of Transportation, the average consecutive tenure
23 of a director was just over three years, making the
24 continuity provided by John R. Cooper's service exceptional
25 and historically significant; and
26 WHEREAS, under Governor Robert Bentley, John R.
27 Cooper guided development of the Alabama Transportation
28 Improvement and Rehabilitation Program (ATRIP), accelerating
Page 1
Improvement and Rehabilitation Program (ATRIP), accelerating
SJR115 Enrolled
29 road and bridge improvements statewide; and
30 WHEREAS, upon assuming office in 2017, Governor Kay
31 Ivey retained John R. Cooper as Transportation Director,
32 reflecting confidence in his leadership and the value of
33 continuity; and
34 WHEREAS, John R. Cooper played a central role in the
35 enactment of the Rebuild Alabama Act to generate new state
36 revenues for transportation purposes; and
37 WHEREAS, his tenure included significant legislation,
38 including authorization for Design-Build project delivery,
39 enactment of legislation related to tolling, and statutory
40 improvements to the department's administrative operations;
41 and
42 WHEREAS, under his leadership, the Alabama Department
43 of Transportation delivered major projects, including
44 improvements to U.S. Highway 280, replacement of the
45 Interstate 59/20 bridges in downtown Birmingham, widening
46 the final stretch of U.S. Highway 431 in Eufaula, congestion
47 relief along Alabama Highway 59 through acquisition of the
48 Foley Beach Express toll bridge and construction of a new
49 bridge over the Intracoastal Waterway, widening portions of
50 Interstates 65, 10, and 565, and initiating construction of
51 the West Alabama Highway; and
52 WHEREAS, his vision, tenacity, and persistence have
53 helped develop the Mobile River Bridge and Bayway, making it
54 the most thoroughly developed mega-project in the United
55 States; and
56 WHEREAS, his leadership has helped advance the
Page 2
WHEREAS, his leadership has helped advance the
SJR115 Enrolled
57 Birmingham Northern Beltline through the initial phases of
58 construction; and
59 WHEREAS, throughout his service, John R. Cooper has
60 demonstrated a strong commitment to public service, fiscal
61 responsibility, and the safety and mobility of Alabama's
62 citizens; now therefore,
63 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
64 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That we hereby commend John R.
65 Cooper for his exemplary and unprecedented service as
66 Transportation Director and express the gratitude of the
67 Legislature and the people of the State of Alabama for his
68 leadership and dedication.
69 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That a copy of this
70 resolution be provided to John R. Cooper as a formal
71 expression of the Legislature's respect and esteem.
