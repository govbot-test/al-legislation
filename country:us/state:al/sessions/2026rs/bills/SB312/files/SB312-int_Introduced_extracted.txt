9 Relating to Marshall County; to amend Sections
10 45-48-142.22, 45-48-142.24, and 45-48-142.26, Code of Alabama
11 1975; to authorize the Marshall County Commission to annex new
12 areas into existing volunteer fire districts; to define
13 "business property" and "dwelling" for the application of a
14 fire service fee; to include recreational vehicles, mobile
15 homes, and tiny homes; to impose a delinquent fee, citation
16 fee, and a penalty for late payments; to authorize the revenue
17 commissioner to collect the fees and penalties; and to require
18 the revenue commissioner to create reports of the assessment,
19 collection, and distribution of the fees.
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
21 Section 1. Sections 45-48-142.22, 45-48-142.24, and
22 45-48-142.26, Code of Alabama 1975, are amended to read as
23 follows:

35 department shall be certified by the Alabama Forestry

105 and without limiting the generality of the foregoing:
106 (1) A building, structure, or improvement assessed for
107 purposes of state and county ad valorem taxation, as Class III
108 single-family owner-occupied residential property.
109 (2) A duplex or an apartment building.
110 (3) Any mobile home or house trailer.
111 (2) The fee shall apply to all dwellings and business
112 properties in the geographic boundaries of the fire district,

properties in the geographic boundaries of the fire district,

113 including, but not limited to, the following:
114 a. Buildings, structures, or other improvements
115 assessed for the purposes of state or county ad valorem
116 taxation as Class III single family owner-occupied or
117 renter-occupied residential property.
118 b. Dwelling units where each unit is separated by
119 structural partitions or walls and the units do not share
120 mechanical systems or utilities. Each unit shall have the fee
121 applied separately.
122 c. Portable or movable buildings, structures, or other
123 improvements that are used as a dwelling and connected to
124 utilities.
125 d. Recreational vehicles placed on owned or rented
126 property which are connected to utilities or furnished with

128 recreational vehicles that are in storage and not connected to

130 the owner's property.
131 e. Mobile homes, tiny homes, and manufactured homes.
132 (3)a. If a dwelling or business property consists of
133 two or more units separately occupied or secured by different
134 owners, renters, or business entities, a separate fee shall be
135 assessed for each separate unit or space located within the
136 building, structure, or other improvement.
137 b. A business entity that operates from a dwelling, and
138 thereby makes the dwelling a business property, shall have the
139 fee apply to the building, structure, or improvement only
140 once.

141 (b)(c) A building, structure, or other improvement
142 shall be classified as a dwelling for the purposes of this
143 subpartarticle notwithstanding either of the following:
144 (1) That it is wholly or partially vacant or
145 uninhabited at any time during the year for which a fire
146 protection service fee with respect thereto is or is to be
147 levied.
148 (2) That it is also used or expected to be used
149 simultaneously for a purpose, whether or not commercial in
150 nature, other than as a dwelling or residence.
151 (c)(d) The fire protection service fee shall not be
152 construed as a tax on property. The fees shall be levied for
153 the purpose of funding fire protection services to dwellings
154 and businesses under this subpart.
155 (d)(e) The fire protection fee for a district may not
156 be increased for a period of five years after approval of the
157 initial fire protection service fee. Any increase in the

160 increasing the fee.
161 (e)(f) If a majority of the votes cast at the election
162 are negative, another election for setting the amount of the
163 fire protection fee shall not be held for two years from the
164 time of the prior election. "

be collected, administered, and enforced as closely as

169 possible at the same time, in the same manner, and under the
170 same requirements and laws as are the ad valorem taxes of the
171 state. In the case of mobile homes, the fee shall be
172 collected, administered, and enforced as closely as possible
173 at the same time, in the same manner, and under the same
174 requirements and laws as the annual registration fee for
175 manufactured homes provided in Section 40-12-255.
176 (2)a. The revenue commissioner shall collect a
177 delinquent fee of twenty-five dollars ($25) from owners of
178 property subject to the fee under Section 45-48-142.24 who
179 have failed to pay their service fee by the time required by
180 the revenue commissioner.
181 b. The revenue commissioner shall issue citations to
182 owners of property covered under Section 45-48-142.24 for
183 failure to pay a fire service fee or a delinquent fee under
184 paragraph a.
185 (3) The proceeds of the collected fees, minus an
186 administration fee not to exceed five percent, shall be paid
187 to the respective volunteer fire districts. The delinquent fee
188 and citation fee imposed under subdivision (2) shall be
189 distributed in the same manner as the fire service fee.
190 (b) Funds paid to the volunteer fire districts shall
191 only be expended for fire protection and emergency services
192 purposes to include the purchase of vehicles and equipment,
193 daily operations, training, supplies, and insurances. Each
194 fire district receiving funds shall maintain financial records
195 in accordance with the Financial and Compliance Guidelines for
196 Volunteer Fire Departments, August 2009, of the Department of
Page 7
Volunteer Fire Departments, August 2009, of the Department of
SB312 INTRODUCED
197 Examiners of Public Accounts.
198 (c) By September 15th of each year, the volunteer fire
199 district receiving fire protection service fees shall file a
200 financial statement with the county commission detailing the
201 receipt and expenditure of all funds generated by this subpart
202 during the previous 12-month period. The filing shall also
203 account for all unspent funds and whether the unspent funds
204 have been obligated. The county commission shall supply the
205 accounting forms to each eligible fire district.
206 (d)(1) The revenue commissioner shall issue monthly
207 fire protection service fee financial reports detailing the
208 assessment, collection, and distribution of the fee. The
209 revenue commissioner shall create separate reports for each
210 fire district.
211 (2) The revenue commissioner shall deliver a copy of
212 each report to the corresponding volunteer fire department,
213 the county commission, and the Marshall County Association of
214 Fire Departments and Rescue Squads. Once delivered, the
215 revenue commissioner shall make each report publicly
216 available."
217 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
218 1, 2026.
