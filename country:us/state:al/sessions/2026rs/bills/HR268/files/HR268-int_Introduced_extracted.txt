5 90TH BIRTHDAY CELEBRATION.
6
7 WHEREAS, with special pleasure, we offer joyful
8 felicitations to Ruther Lee Averett on the occasion of her
9 90th Birthday Celebration on April 2, 2026; and
10 WHEREAS, Ruther Lee Averett was born on April 2,
11 1936, in Cottonton, Alabama, to Governor Hardman and Sallie
12 Bell James Morris; and
13 WHEREAS, she was lovingly raised by her grandparents,
14 Lucious and Marie James, who instilled in her the values of
15 faith, perseverance, and family that have guided her
16 throughout her life; and
17 WHEREAS, in 1954, she married the love of her life,
18 Melvin Averett, and together they shared more than 60 years
19 of marriage, building a strong and loving family rooted in
20 faith and commitment; and
21 WHEREAS, she is the proud and devoted mother of
22 Carolyn, Phyllis (Ricky), Vladimir (Yolonda), Melvin Bernard
23 (Thersa), along with a cherished bonus daughter, Earline
24 Payne; and
25 WHEREAS, she is the loving grandmother of Travis,
26 Brandon (Sylvia), Kristen, Nia, Mya, and Khrislyn, and the
27 proud great-grandmother of Ciniyah, Trent, Bria, Braylen,
28 Brooke, Ava, and Greyson, along with many beloved bonus

29 grandchildren who have been touched by her love; and
30 WHEREAS, she is blessed with living siblings Eddie
31 Morris, Marie Reed, Norman Hardman, and Amzie Hardman; she
32 has survived her beloved family members, Annie Griggs,
33 Carter Hudson, and several aunts and uncles who were raised
34 with her and loved as siblings; and
35 WHEREAS, her educational journey began at The
36 Catholic School, later known as St. Joseph, before attending
37 Mt. Olive School, continuing at Claflin School in Columbus,
38 Georgia, and later Old South Girard High School; and
39 WHEREAS, demonstrating determination and
40 perseverance, she later returned to school in her 40s and
41 proudly earned her GED; and
42 WHEREAS, at an early age, she accepted Jesus Christ
43 as her Lord and Savior and united with New Hope Baptist
44 Church in Cottonton, Alabama; in 1960, she became a faithful
45 member of Pine Hill Baptist Church, where she served as a
46 Sunday School teacher, choir member, and missionary; and
47 WHEREAS, she worked diligently throughout her life at
48 National Linen Mill, Anderson Mill (later Bibb Mill), and
49 Opelika Manufacturing Company (later Lesher Mill), from
50 which she eventually retired after years of dedicated
51 service; and
52 WHEREAS, throughout 90 remarkable years, she has
53 demonstrated strength, faith, love, wisdom, and devotion to
54 family, church, and community; now therefore,
55 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE
