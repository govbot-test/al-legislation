4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under current law, the Governor's Mansion
6 Authority may hire employees, as may be necessary or
7 desirable, and may fix the compensation of such
8 employees. Current law does not specify the status of
9 such employees under the Merit System Act.
10 This bill would create the Sarah Caldwell Crosby
11 Act.
12 This bill would codify current practice by
13 providing that employees of the Governor's Mansion
14 Authority are employees within the unclassified service
15 for purposes of the Merit System Act. This bill would
16 further provide that any employee of the authority as
17 of the effective date of this act shall be entitled to
18 payment for his or her accumulated leave upon
19 separation from service.

24 AN ACT

26 Relating to the Governor's Mansion Authority; to amend
27 Section 41-9-548, Code of Alabama 1975, to provide that
28 Governor's Mansion Authority employees are in the unclassified

29 service of the Merit System Act.
30 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
31 Section 1. This act shall be known as the Sarah
32 Caldwell Crosby Act.
33 Section 2. Section 41-9-548, Code of Alabama 1975, is
34 amended as follows:
35 "§41-9-548
36 (a)(1) All full-time employees of the authority shall
37 be treated as state employees for the purposes of
38 participating in any insurance programs provided for the state
39 employees.
40 (b)(2) The authority shall pay the employer's
41 contributions to any insurance programs out of funds
42 appropriated to it or otherwise available to it for any
43 purpose whatsoever. The authority may deduct the employees'
44 contributions for such programs by means of payroll deductions
45 or otherwise from the compensation paid to the employees.
46 (b)(1) All employees of the authority shall be members
47 of the unclassified service for purposes of Section 36-26-10.
48 (2) Any employee of the authority employed as of the
49 effective date of the act adding this subsection shall be
50 entitled to payment for his or her accumulated leave upon
51 separation from service, and any employee hired after the
52 effective date shall be entitled to such payment only to the
53 extent generally provided to unclassified employees in the
54 Merit System, consistent with generally applicable
55 administrative rules adopted by the State Personnel
56 Department."
