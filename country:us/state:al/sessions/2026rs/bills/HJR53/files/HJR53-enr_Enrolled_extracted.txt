HJR53 Enrolled
1 Enrolled, An Act,
2
3 SEEKING ALMIGHTY GOD'S HAND OF EVER-PRESENT MERCY AND
4 HEALING ON ALABAMA.
5
6 WHEREAS, we acknowledge that our national and state
7 founders trusted in the omnipotent hand of Providence to
8 bless our land and to guide their actions; and
9 WHEREAS, we recognize that these founders established
10 our nation and our state on the Judeo-Christian values
11 outlined in the Bible, including the concept that
12 governments are established by God to protect the rights of
13 the individual and the teachings to "do unto others as we
14 would have them do unto us" and to love our neighbors as
15 ourselves, and that from these ideas, the spirit of
16 Alabama's hospitality flows; and
17 WHEREAS, the framers of the United States
18 Constitution, recognizing free exercise of religion as an
19 unalienable right, secured its protection in the First
20 Amendment to the Constitution, and the framers of the
21 Constitution of Alabama of 2022, also recognizing this
22 right, secured the protection of religious freedom in
23 Article I, Section 3; and
24 WHEREAS, we understand that only a continued
25 commitment and adherence to these values and the laws of
26 Nature and Nature's God, coupled with a dedication to our
27 Constitution, can thwart the forthcoming attacks against our
28 state values; and
Page 1
state values; and
HJR53 Enrolled
29 WHEREAS, we are thankful that Alabama has been
30 blessed by Providence with abundant resources and a
31 prosperity resultant from liberty; and
32 WHEREAS, we are grateful for the periods of economic
33 stability Alabama has enjoyed; and
34 WHEREAS, despite these blessings, our citizens and
35 neighbors still continue to face many challenges; and
36 WHEREAS, our citizens and neighbors continue to have
37 concerns about the security of our national borders and
38 attacks on our institutions and way of life; and
39 WHEREAS, exposing, resisting, and removing corruption
40 from all levels of government is vital to maintaining
41 liberty for all citizens; and
42 WHEREAS, we ask that true justice and the rule of God
43 be established out of Alabama; let people of kingdom
44 understanding now be positioned to rule; let kingdom minded
45 statesmen and stateswomen arise; let Alabama be known across
46 the nation as a land where justice and God's rule are
47 established; let Alabama be a model state to the entire
48 nation in the name of Jesus; and
49 WHEREAS, human trafficking (slavery) was, is, and
50 forever will be an unacceptable evil in our state and nation
51 that destroys the lives of thousands, depriving them of
52 their Creator-endowed rights to Life, Liberty, and the
53 Pursuit of Happiness; and
54 WHEREAS, acts of criminal violence in our communities
55 are and will remain unacceptable; and
56 WHEREAS, the dependence on and the addiction to drugs
Page 2
WHEREAS, the dependence on and the addiction to drugs
HJR53 Enrolled
57 and alcohol will continue to overwhelm our government
58 finances, our health care and mental health systems, and our
59 law enforcement resources, adversely affecting workforce
60 productivity and damaging families; and
61 WHEREAS, building strong and committed families where
62 the father and mother are committed to one another, their
63 children, and their community is foundational to a healthy
64 Alabama; and
65 WHEREAS, the suicidal and infanticidal tendencies
66 bombarding our culture from all directions belie the truth
67 that everyone's life has value because we are created in the
68 image of God; and
69 WHEREAS, Alabama is a pro-life state, and the
70 sanctity of life is recognized and supported in the
71 Constitution of Alabama of 2022 from the moment of
72 conception; this is a foundational principle that must be
73 protected; and
74 WHEREAS, we know that societies that fail to honor
75 all life cannot survive; and
76 WHEREAS, throughout our history, our leaders have
77 called people to seek the Creator's favor and mercy by
78 issuing proclamations like the one issued by John Adams on
79 March 23, 1798:
80 "As the safety and prosperity of nations ultimately
81 and essentially depend on the protection and the blessing of
82 Almighty God; and the national acknowledgment of this truth
83 is not only an indispensable duty which the People owe to
84 Him, but a duty whose natural influence is favorable to the
Page 3
Him, but a duty whose natural influence is favorable to the
HJR53 Enrolled
85 promotion of that Morality and Piety, without which social
86 Happiness cannot exist nor the Blessings of a Free
87 Government be enjoyed; and as this Duty, at all times
88 incumbent, is so especially in seasons of Difficulty or of
89 Danger, when existing or threatening Calamities, the just
90 Judgments of God against prevalent Iniquity, are a loud call
91 to Repentance and Reformation...Under these considerations
92 it has appeared to me that the Duty of imploring the Mercy
93 and Benediction of Heaven on our Country demands, at this
94 time, a special attention from its Inhabitants"; and
95 WHEREAS, we recognize our duty to legislate
96 responsibly but also understand that solving these problems
97 will require the blessing and protection of Almighty God and
98 a unity of purpose within our communities; and
99 WHEREAS, we hold that "...except the Lord keep the
100 city, the watchman waketh but in vain" (Ps. 127:1(b)); now,
101 therefore,
102 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
103 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That the period of September 1
104 through September 30 of each year be recognized as a time of
105 prayer and fasting in Alabama.
106 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That we desire the continued
107 blessing of Almighty God on Alabama, recognizing that He, as
108 Creator and the King of all Glory, holds all authority to
109 both judge and bless nations, states, and peoples.
110 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That we recognize our sins
111 and shortcomings, both individually and as a people, and
112 humbly ask His forgiveness.
Page 4
humbly ask His forgiveness.
HJR53 Enrolled
113 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That we ask the Lord Jesus to
114 remove corruption, criminal violence, and addiction from our
115 state and our nation, restore marriages and strengthen
116 families, and bring about a righteous unity of vision and
117 purpose for all Alabamians.
118 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That we ask the Holy Spirit
119 to move within the halls of government, our classrooms, our
120 businesses, our places of worship, and our homes, bringing
121 peace, hope, love, kindness, goodness, gentleness,
122 faithfulness, temperance, forbearance, and joy.
123 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That we call upon those who
124 are physically able and spiritually inclined to join with us
125 annually during the month of September in a season of prayer
126 and fasting, humbling ourselves before Him, asking His
127 forgiveness, and seeking His blessings, grace, and mercy so
128 that we, our communities, our state, and our nation will be
129 transformed.
Page 5
transformed.
