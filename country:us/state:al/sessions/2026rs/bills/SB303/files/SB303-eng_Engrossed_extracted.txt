7 AN ACT

9 Relating to off-road vehicles; to provide for the
10 operation of off-road vehicles on certain public roads; to
11 provide for the registration and licensure of off-road
12 vehicles; to require the Department of Revenue to adopt rules;
13 to provide a limitation of liability under certain conditions;
14 and to provide a penalty for a violation.
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
16 Section 1. (a) An off-road vehicle, as defined under
17 Section 32-8-2, Code of Alabama 1975, may be operated on a
18 public road subject to the requirements of this section.
19 (b)(1) The Department of Revenue shall establish a
20 certification form for off-road vehicle dealers, as defined
21 under Section 32-8-2, Code of Alabama 1975, to certify that,
22 regardless of the age of the vehicle, the off-road vehicle is
23 installed with all of the following equipment:
24 a. Brakes.
25 b. Head lights, tail lights, brake lights, and turn
26 signal lights.
27 c. A horn meeting the requirements of Section 32-5-213,
28 Code of Alabama 1975.

36 h. A windshield, with or without wipers.
37 i. Tires in compliance with Section 32-5-210, Code of
38 Alabama 1975.
39 (2) If a vehicle is certified by an off-road vehicle
40 dealer as meeting the requirements of subdivision (1), the
41 owner of the vehicle may present the certification to the
42 local licensing official and apply for a tag. Upon meeting the
43 requirements of the foregoing and payment of all applicable
44 fees, the local licensing official shall register the vehicle
45 pursuant to Division 1 of Article 5 of Chapter 12 of Title 40,
46 Code of Alabama 1975, and issue a tag for the vehicle pursuant
47 to Article 2 of Chapter 6 of Title 32, Code of Alabama 1975.
48 The tag shall be securely attached in the same manner as a
49 private passenger vehicle and shall indicate that the off-road
50 vehicle is for restricted use.
51 (3) The off-road vehicle must be covered by a liability
52 insurance policy pursuant to Chapter 7A of Title 32, Code of
53 Alabama 1975.
54 (c) An off-road vehicle may only be operated on a
55 public road by an operator who is at least 16 years of age and
56 who possesses a valid driver license from this state or an

57 equivalent license from another state. The operator shall obey
58 all of the duties applicable to the operator of a private
59 motor vehicle under Chapter 5 and Chapter 5A of Title 32, Code
60 of Alabama 1975, except as to those provisions that by their
61 nature can have no application.
62 (d) The operation of an off-road vehicle under this
63 section is limited to public roads in the unincorporated
64 portions of a county which have a maximum posted speed limit
65 of 45 miles per hour. Nothing in this section authorizes the
66 operation of an off-road vehicle on an interstate highway or
67 within a municipality.
68 (e) No provision of this section shall serve to exempt
69 the owner or operator of an off-road vehicle from being
70 responsible for damage to public roads and rights-of-way as
71 provided in Section 32-5-9, Code of Alabama 1975.
72 (f) The provisions of this section shall not authorize
73 a person to operate an off-road vehicle on private property
74 without the consent of the respective landowner.
75 (g) Notwithstanding any other provision of this section
76 to the contrary, an off-road vehicle that does not otherwise
77 meet the requirements of subsection (b) may be operated to
78 cross from a county road, field, or other area of operation to
79 another, or cross a state highway or other noncounty road, not
80 to include an interstate or national defense highway system,
81 if all of the following conditions are satisfied:
82 (1) The crossing is made at an angle of approximately
83 90 degrees to the direction of the highway and at a place
84 where no obstruction prevents a quick and safe crossing.

104 manner as for a private passenger automobile.

107 contributory negligence, or assumption of the risk.
