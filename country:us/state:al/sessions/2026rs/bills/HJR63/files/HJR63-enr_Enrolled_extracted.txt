3 RECOGNIZING THE DALE COUNTY SCHOOL SYSTEM FOR EARNING A 95
4 OVERALL SCORE ON THE STATE AND FEDERAL ANNUAL REPORT CARDS.
5
6 WHEREAS, it is fitting that we pay tribute to those
7 educational institutions that, through the natural talents
8 and unflagging commitment to academic excellence evidenced
9 by their administration, faculty, staff, and students, have
10 achieved state and federal recognition for excellence; and
11 WHEREAS, one such high-achieving educational system
12 is the Dale County School System, which earned a 95 overall
13 score on the Alabama State and Federal Report Cards, the
14 highest ever earned by a county school system in the history
15 of Alabama; the State and Federal Report Cards are the
16 measurement of the academic achievement and culture of
17 public school systems across the state and nation; and
18 WHEREAS, the dedication to academic excellence
19 demonstrated by all of the fine individuals affiliated with
20 the Dale County School System, including 3,200 students and
21 418 employees, is clearly deserving of our respect,
22 admiration, and commendation; and
23 WHEREAS, the individual schools that have contributed
24 to the success of public education in the communities across
25 Dale County and that are commended for their outstanding
26 achievement are Ariton School, Bridge Academy, Dale County
27 High School, G.W. Long Elementary School, G.W. Long High
28 School, Midland City Elementary School, Newton Elementary
