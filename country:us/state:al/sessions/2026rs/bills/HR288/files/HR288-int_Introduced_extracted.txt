7 WHEREAS, the United States Environmental Protection
8 Agency (EPA) regulates the injection of carbon dioxide into
9 underground wells to prevent the endangering of underground
10 sources of drinking water; and
11 WHEREAS, under this federal regulatory scheme, states
12 may gain the primary enforcement responsibility for these
13 regulations upon adopting an underground injection control
14 program that satisfies the EPA's minimum requirements; and
15 WHEREAS, the Legislature passed Act 2024-325 during
16 the 2024 Regular Session which provided certain regulatory
17 requirements for storing carbon dioxide in underground
18 storage facilities in the state, including authorizing the
19 State Oil and Gas Board to levy fees on carbon dioxide
20 injected for storage; and
21 WHEREAS, there are multiple considerations that must
22 be accounted for when siting these facilities in this state,
23 including Alabama's uniquely appropriate geology to
24 accommodate carbon dioxide injection wells, the economic
25 benefit to the state, the public's consensus, the potential
26 risks of these facilities, and the increasing number of
27 other states beginning to accommodate these wells; and
28 WHEREAS, it is accordingly imperative that an
