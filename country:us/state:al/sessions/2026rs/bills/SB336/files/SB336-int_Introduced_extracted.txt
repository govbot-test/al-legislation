4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law a person must have a license
6 to operate a child-care facility, unless exempted by
7 law.
8 This bill would provide that a child-care
9 facility that would otherwise be exempted is not exempt
10 if the facility is operated on a for-profit basis or is
11 operated on a 24-hour basis.
12 This bill would provide additional requirements
13 for an application to operate a child-care facility on
14 a 24-hour basis.
15 Under existing law, the Department of Human
16 Resources is authorized to adopt rules to regulate
17 child-care facilities.
18 This bill would further provide for the
19 authority to adopt rules by specifying the department
20 has the authority to adopt rules governing the use of
21 video surveillance in the facilities and would
22 establish various data retention requirements.
23 Under existing law, child-care facilities are
24 required to give the department a reasonable
25 opportunity to inspect the child-care facility. The
26 inspection may be performed without prior notice, but
27 must be performed at a reasonable time.
28 This bill would provide that if a law
