4 SYNOPSIS:
5 This bill would require each local board of
6 education and the governing body of each charter school
7 to provide each designated school employee who has
8 access to students during the school day with mobile
9 emergency rapid response systems no later than October
10 1, 2030, and provide training to employees on the
11 systems.
12 This bill would require the State Superintendent
13 of Education to develop a list of approved vendors.
14 This bill would also authorize the use of
15 certain funds for purchasing and using mobile emergency
16 rapid response systems.

29 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
30 Section 1. This act shall be known and may be cited as
31 Alyssa's Law.
32 Section 2. (a) For the purposes of this section, the
33 following terms have the following meanings:
34 (1) EMPLOYEE. An employee of the State Department of
35 Education, any local board of education, or any public charter
36 school who has access to students during the school day. This
37 term includes, but is not limited to, teachers,
38 administrators, administrative staff, custodial staff,
39 maintenance staff, cafeteria staff, nurses, counselors, media
40 specialists, coaches, and bus drivers.
41 (2) GOVERNING BODY. Each local board of education and
42 the governing body of each public charter school.
43 (3) MOBILE EMERGENCY RAPID RESPONSE SYSTEM. A device
44 that shall be wearable or in the personal possession of the
45 individual which, upon activation: (i) immediately connects
46 directly to the locally serving public safety answering point;
47 and (ii) provides accurate location information to the room
48 and floor level inside the facility and to specific areas that
49 are part of school grounds including, but not limited to,
50 parking lots, playgrounds, and athletic fields.
51 (4) PUBLIC SAFETY ANSWERING POINT. The same meaning as
52 Section 11-98-1, Code of Alabama 1975.
53 (b)(1) No later than October 1, 2030, and subject to
54 annual dedicated school safety funds, each governing body
55 shall:
56 a. Adopt a policy designating which employees in the
