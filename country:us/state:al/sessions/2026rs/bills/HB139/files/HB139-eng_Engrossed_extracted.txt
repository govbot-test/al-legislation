7 AN ACT

9 Relating to food enrichment; to amend Section 20-1-71,
10 as amended by Act 2025-387, 2025 Regular Session, and Section
11 20-1-79, as added by Act 2025-387, 2025 Regular Session, Code
12 of Alabama 1975, to add corn masa flour to the corn masa
13 products that must be enriched with folic acid; to further
14 provide for the corn masa products exempt from this
15 requirement; and to make nonsubstantive, technical revisions
16 to update the existing code language to current style.
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
18 Section 1. Section 20-1-71, as amended by Act 2025-387,
19 2025 Regular Session, and Section 20-1-79, as added by Act
20 2025-387, 2025 Regular Session, Code of Alabama 1975, are
21 amended to read as follows:
22 "§20-1-71
23 When used in this article, the following terms have the
24 following meanings, unless the context clearly indicates
25 otherwise:
26 (1) APPROPRIATE FEDERAL AGENCY. The federal agency or
27 department or administrative federal officer charged with the
28 enforcement and administration of the federal Food, Drug and
Page 1
enforcement and administration of the federal Food, Drug and
HB139 Engrossed
29 Cosmetic Act.
30 (2) BOARD. The Alabama State Board of Agriculture and
31 Industries.
32 (3) BREAD. Bread, rolls, or buns of every kind and
33 description made wholly or partly from wheat flour which
34 conforms to the definition and standard of identity of bread
35 set out in 21 C.F.R. Part 136. The term does not include bread
36 containing no wheat flour or breads made from 100 percent
37 whole wheat flour. The term includes breads made from mixtures
38 of white flour and whole wheat flour, known as brown breads.
39 (4) CORN MASA. A dry flour-type product made from corn
40 that has been treated with slaked lime or otherwise undergoes
41 a process known as nixtamalization.
42 (5) CORN MASA FLOUR. A dry flour-type product made from
43 drying corn masa.
44 (5)(6) CORN MASA PRODUCT. Any food product containing
45 made primarily from corn masa or corn masa flour.
46 (6)(7) CORNMEAL. All meal for human consumption made
47 from corn, either white or yellow, whole cornmeal, bolted
48 cornmeal, and degerminated cornmeal as defined in definitions
49 and standards of identity set out in 21 C.F.R. Part 137.
50 (7)(8) ENRICHMENT. As applied to bread, flour, meal, or
51 grits, the addition thereto of vitamins and other ingredients
52 of the nature required by this article. and The terms
53 "enriched bread," "enriched flour," "enriched whole cornmeal,"
54 "enriched bolted meal," "enriched degerminated meal," or and
55 "enriched grits" means mean the appropriate food product,
56 according to the federal standards of identity, which has been
Page 2
according to the federal standards of identity, which has been
HB139 Engrossed
57 enriched to conform to the requirements of this article.
58 (8)(9) FLOUR.
59 a. Flour, white White flour, wheat flour, and plain
60 flour.
61 b. Bromated flour.
62 c. Self-rising flour, self-rising white flour, and
63 self-rising wheat flour.
64 d. Phosphated flour, phosphated white flour, and
65 phosphated wheat flour in the definitions and standards of
66 identity set out in 21 C.F.R. Part 137. The term includes all
67 mixtures of flours with whole wheat or high-extraction flours.
68 The term does not include 100 percent whole wheat flour and
69 specially packaged cake and pancake flour.
70 (9)(10) GRITS. Grits, pearl grits, and degerminated
71 grits made from corn by grinding or cracking as defined in the
72 standards of identity set out in 21 C.F.R. Part 137.
73 (10)(11) PERSON. An individual, corporation,
74 partnership, association, joint stock company, trust, or any
75 unincorporated organization."
76 "§20-1-79
77 (a) No person shall manufacture, mix, compound, sell,
78 trade, or offer for sale or trade for human consumption any
79 corn masa flour or corn masa product in this state unless the
80 following ingredients are contained in each pound thereof
81 except in accordance with the following:
82 (1) For corn Corn masa flour, not less than shall
83 contain 0.7 milligrams of folic acid per pound of flour,
84 within an acceptable industry standard deviation of error.
Page 3
within an acceptable industry standard deviation of error.
HB139 Engrossed
85 (2) For corn Corn masa products, not less than shall
86 contain 0.4 milligrams of folic acid per pound of end product,
87 within an acceptable industry standard deviation of error.
88 (b) This section shall not apply to either any of the
89 following:
90 (1) Corn masa flour or corn masa products made by or
91 supplied to a cottage food production operation.
92 (2) Corn masa flour or corn masa products offered for
93 sale by a grocery store offering conforming products.
94 (3) Snack foods. For the purposes of this subdivision,
95 the term "snack food" means a food product that is intended
96 for immediate consumption, is typically prepackaged in small
97 or single-serving portions, and is primarily consumed between
98 meals, including, but not limited to, chips, crisps, or
99 extruded food products. The term does not include a food
100 product primarily made from corn masa flour or wet corn masa
101 products that is bulk packaged or is commonly used as a
102 primary ingredient in meal preparation, including, but not
103 limited to, tortillas.
104 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
105 1, 2026.
