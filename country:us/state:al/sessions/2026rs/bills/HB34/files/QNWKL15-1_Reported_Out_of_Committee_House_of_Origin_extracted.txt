6 TO BE ENTITLED
7 AN ACT
8
9 Relating to crimes and offenses; to add Section
10 13A-10-9.2 to the Code of Alabama 1975, to establish the crime
11 of giving false information to a law enforcement officer; to
12 add Section 13A-10-3.1 to the Code of Alabama 1975, to
13 establish the crime of refusing give a law enforcement officer
14 information; to provide criminal penalties for a violation; to
15 amend Section 15-5-30, Code of Alabama 1975, to further
16 provide for warrantless investigatory stops; and to repeal
17 Section 13A-9-18.1, Code of Alabama 1975, relating to giving
18 false information to a law enforcement officer.
19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
20 Section 1. Section 13A-10-9.2 is added to the Code of
21 Alabama 1975, to read as follows:
22 §13A-10-9.2
23 (a) A person commits the crime of giving false
24 information to a law enforcement officer if, during the course
25 of a law enforcement officer's official duties, the person
26 knowingly and with intent to deceive gives to the law
27 enforcement officer a false name, address, or date of birth.
28 (b) A violation of this section is a Class A

29 misdemeanor.
30 Section 2. Section 13A-10-3.1 is added to the Code of
31 Alabama 1975, to read as follows:
32 13A-10-3.1
33 (a) A person commits the crime of refusing to give a
34 law enforcement officer information if all of the following
35 occur:
36 (1) A law enforcement officer, acting within his or her
37 official duties, develops a reasonable suspicion that a person
38 has committed, is committing, or is about to commit a felony
39 or misdemeanor and conducts an investigatory stop.
40 (2) The law enforcement officer reasonably identifies
41 himself or herself to the person as a duly sworn law
42 enforcement officer and provides the reason for the stop.
43 (3) The law enforcement officer asks the person for the
44 person's name, address, or date of birth.
45 (b) Nothing in this section shall require a person to
46 give any information that would be deemed self-incriminating.
47 (c) A violation of this section is a Class C
48 misdemeanor.
49 Section 3. Section 15-5-30, Code of Alabama 1975, is
50 amended to read as follows:
51 "§15-5-30
52 A sheriff or other officer acting as sheriff, his
53 deputy or any constable, acting within their respective
54 counties, any marshal, deputy marshal or policeman of any
55 incorporated city or town within the limits of the county or
56 any highway patrolman or state trooperlaw enforcement officer,
Page 2
any highway patrolman or state trooperlaw enforcement officer,

57 acting in the course of his or her official duties, may stop
58 any person abroad in a public place whom hethe officer
59 reasonably suspects is committing, has committed, or is about
60 to commit a felony, misdemeanor, or other public offense and
61 may demand of himthat the person give his or her name,
62 address, date of birth, and an explanation of his or her
63 actions."
64 Section 4. Section 13A-9-18.1, Code of Alabama 1975,
65 relating to giving false information to a law enforcement
66 officer, is repealed.
67 Section 5. This act shall become effective on October
68 1, 2026.
