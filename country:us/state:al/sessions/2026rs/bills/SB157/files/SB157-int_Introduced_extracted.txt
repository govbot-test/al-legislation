4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, a recording made by a
6 body-worn camera or dashboard camera used by a law
7 enforcement agency may only be disclosed to an
8 individual or a personal representative of an
9 individual whose image or voice is the subject of the
10 recording.
11 This bill would provide that a recording made by
12 a body-worn camera or dashboard camera used by a law
13 enforcement agency be considered a public record,
14 making the recording subject to public inspection.
15 This bill would also provide for an appeals
16 process if a law enforcement agency fails to provide a
17 requested recording.

27 recordings; and to provide an appeal process.

29 Section 1. Sections 36-21-210, 36-21-212, and
30 36-21-213, Code of Alabama 1975, are amended to read as
31 follows:
32 "§36-21-210
33 As used in For the purposes of this article, the
34 following terms have the following meanings:
35 (1) BODY-WORN CAMERA. An operational video or digital
36 camera or other electronic device, including a microphone or
37 other mechanism to capture audio, affixed to the uniform or
38 person of law enforcement agency personnel and positioned in a
39 way that allows the camera or device to capture interactions
40 between law enforcement agency personnel and others.
41 (2) CUSTODIAL LAW ENFORCEMENT AGENCY. The law
42 enforcement agency that owns or leases or whose personnel
43 operates the equipment that created the recording at the time
44 the recording was made. If another law enforcement agency
45 takes over the investigation of the recorded incident, that
46 agency becomes the custodial law enforcement agency for the
47 purposes of this article.
48 (3) DASHBOARD CAMERA. A device or system installed or
49 used in a law enforcement agency vehicle that electronically
50 records images or audio of interactions between law
51 enforcement agency personnel and others. This term does not
52 include a body-worn camera.
53 (4) DISCLOSE or DISCLOSURE. To make a recording
54 available for viewing or listening at a time and location
55 chosen by the custodial law enforcement agency. This term does
56 not include the release of a recording.

57 (5) PERSONAL REPRESENTATIVE. A parent, court-appointed
58 guardian, spouse, or attorney of an individual whose image or
59 voice is the subject of the recording. If an individual whose
60 image or voice is the subject of the recording is deceased,
61 the term also means the personal representative of the estate
62 of the deceased individual; the deceased individual's
63 surviving spouse, parent, or adult child; the deceased
64 individual's attorney; or the parent or guardian of a
65 surviving minor child of the deceased.
66 (6)(4) RECORDING. A visual, audio, or visual and audio
67 recording captured by a body-worn camera, a dashboard camera,
68 or any other video or audio recording device operated by or on

70 personnel when carrying out law enforcement responsibilities.
71 This term does not include any video or audio recordings of
72 interviews regarding agency internal investigations or
73 interviews or interrogations of suspects or witnesses.
74 (7) RELEASE. To provide a copy of a recording."
