3 MOURNING THE DEATH AND CELEBRATING THE LIFE OF ELLA
4 COBBS COOK.
5
6 WHEREAS, it is with an overwhelming sense of loss, we
7 mourn the untimely death, yet celebrate the life, of Ella
8 Cobbs Cook of Birmingham, Alabama, whose passing took place
9 on December 13, 2025, at Brown University in Providence,
10 Rhode Island; and
11 WHEREAS, Ella was born on July 18, 2006, to Anna
12 Bishop Cook and Richard Poellnitz Cook in Birmingham; she
13 was the sister of Richard Hooker Poellnitz Cook and Mary
14 Hamner Cook; and
15 WHEREAS, Ella was a sophomore at Brown when she died,
16 concentrating in French and Mathematics-Economics; before
17 college, she graduated from Mountain Brook High School; Ella
18 was an active, lifelong member of Cathedral Church of the
19 Advent in Birmingham; and
20 WHEREAS, Ella was known for a wide array of
21 interests, including spending time with her precious
22 friends, babysitting many children who were special to her,
23 teaching young people in Bible studies, Sunday school, and
24 academic subjects; she had a love for piano, dance, spending
25 time outdoors in Hale County, and traveling anywhere,
26 especially where French is spoken; and
27 WHEREAS, the quality of Ella's personality and soul
28 that most touched others was her Christian faith; she
