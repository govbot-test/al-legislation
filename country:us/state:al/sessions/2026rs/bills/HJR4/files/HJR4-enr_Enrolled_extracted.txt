3 MOURNING THE DEATH AND CELEBRATING THE LIFE OF THE HONORABLE
4 HOWARD SANDERFORD.
5
6 WHEREAS, it is with profound sorrow and deep regret
7 that we record the passing of The Honorable Howard
8 Sanderford on January 7, 2026, at the age of 90; and
9 WHEREAS, an outstanding member of the community,
10 Howard Sanderford selflessly served the state as a military
11 officer, legislator, and public servant; he was known for
12 his commitment to faith, family, and the betterment of his
13 community, and he set a shining example for all who knew
14 him; and
15 WHEREAS, Howard Sanderford was born on October 18,
16 1935, in Meridian, Mississippi; he earned his Bachelor of
17 Science in Accounting from Mississippi State University,
18 where he was active in student government; and
19 WHEREAS, after graduating, he served as an officer in
20 the United States Marine Corps, where he participated in the
21 Beirut, Lebanon, landing of 1958; he was a member of the
22 Marine Corps League, the Military Order of World Wars, the
23 Veterans of Foreign Wars, the American Legion Post 237, and
24 the Military Officers Association of America; and
25 WHEREAS, following his military service, he moved to
26 Alabama and began a business career in the information
27 technology sector, serving as an executive with IBM in
28 Huntsville until his "first retirement" in 1987; that

29 retirement did not last long, as he soon thereafter founded

32 WHEREAS, in 1989, Mr. Sanderford won a special
33 election to represent the 20th District in the Alabama House
34 of Representatives, beginning his storied career as a
35 legislator; within the State House, he was known for his
36 tireless dedication to his constituents and endless energy
37 for public service; he was slow to anger, empathetic, and a
38 role model for young legislators; he retired from the
39 Legislature after 33 years in 2022; and
40 WHEREAS, during his esteemed tenure in the
41 Legislature, Mr. Sanderford served in many capacities,
42 including, but certainly not limited to: Chair of the House
43 Boards, Agencies, and Commissions Committee; Cochair of the
44 Joint Legislative Sunset Committee; Chair of the Alabama
45 Legislative Medal of Honor for Law Enforcement; member of
46 the Alabama Space Authority; executive board member of the
47 Southern Legislative Conference; longtime member of the
48 House Constitution, Campaign, and Elections Committee; and
49 member of the Internal Affairs Committee, the House
50 Technology and Research Committee, and the Madison County
51 Legislation Committee; and
52 WHEREAS, Mr. Sanderford's services to the State of
53 Alabama and its people extends far beyond the titles he held
54 within the State House; he freely dedicated his time to
55 numerous boards, clubs, and organizations, including as past
56 President of the Huntsville Rotary Club; past Chair of the
