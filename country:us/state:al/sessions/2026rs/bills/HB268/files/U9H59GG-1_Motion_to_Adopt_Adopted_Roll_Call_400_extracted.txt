4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, 911 calls in the State of
6 Alabama are handled by public safety answering centers
7 that are directly supervised by local communication
8 districts, which in turn are under the authority of the
9 state 911 Board.
10 This bill would authorize the 911 Board to
11 monitor the dispatch readiness of local communication
12 districts based upon nationally recognized best
13 practices.
14 This bill would provide that the Attorney
15 General, based upon information received that a local
16 district's emergency response is deficient, may request
17 the 911 Board to audit the district's performance.
18 This bill would require the 911 Board to approve
19 and submit a formal report on its audit findings to the
20 Attorney General.
21 This bill would further authorize the 911 Board
22 to prescribe and supervise a remediation plan to the
23 local communication district to improve district
24 emergency response performance.
