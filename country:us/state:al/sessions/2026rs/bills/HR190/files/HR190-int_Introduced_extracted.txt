4 HR____ SUPPORTING THE COMPLETION OF A STATEWIDE WORKFORCE
5 TRANSPORTATION NEEDS ASSESSMENT.
6
7 WHEREAS, a reliable transportation system is
8 essential to workforce participation, economic growth, and
9 quality of life in communities across the State of Alabama;
10 and
11 WHEREAS, transportation remains one of the most
12 significant barriers preventing Alabamians from entering,
13 remaining in, or advancing within the workforce; this is
14 particularly true for low-income individuals, rural
15 residents, older adults, and individuals with disabilities;
16 and
17 WHEREAS, employers across Alabama consistently report
18 difficulty hiring and retaining workers due to
19 transportation gaps that limit access to job sites, training
20 programs, and essential services; and
21 WHEREAS, transit services currently operate in 54
22 counties across Alabama, providing a range of services
23 including rural van transportation, demand-response services
24 for older adults and individuals with disabilities, and
25 fixed-route systems in urban areas; however, these services
26 vary widely in availability, capacity, and funding; and
27 WHEREAS, rural communities in particular face unique
28 transportation challenges due to geographic distance,

32 WHEREAS, in 2018, the Alabama Legislature enacted the
33 Alabama Public Transportation Act, now appearing as Article
34 12, Chapter 23 of Title 41, Code of Alabama 1975; the act
35 created the Alabama Public Transportation Trust Fund, and
36 required that a statewide public transportation needs
37 assessment be completed every five years to identify gaps,
38 priorities, and funding needs related to public
39 transportation; and
40 WHEREAS, despite the statutory requirement, a
41 statewide public transportation needs assessment has never
42 been conducted due to the absence of a dedicated funding
43 stream, leaving policymakers without the data necessary to
44 guide effective transportation and workforce investments;
45 and
46 WHEREAS, Alabama is one of the only states in the
47 nation that does not provide state funding for transit,
48 underscoring the importance of data-driven planning to
49 ensure limited resources are used efficiently and
50 strategically; and
51 WHEREAS, a comprehensive statewide workforce
52 transportation needs assessment would provide critical data
53 on where transportation barriers intersect with workforce
54 shortages, evaluate gaps affecting access to employment and
55 training, and inform future policy and funding decisions to
56 strengthen Alabama's labor force and economic
