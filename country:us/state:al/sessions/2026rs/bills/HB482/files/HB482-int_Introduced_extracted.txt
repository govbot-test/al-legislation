4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, all secondary metals
7 purchase transactions of ferrous and nonferrous metals
8 to which the recycler is a party, and must report the
9 information to the Alabama Criminal Justice Information
10 Center into a database on the day of a purchase
11 transaction.
12 Also under existing law, it is unlawful for a
13 person to damage or destroy certain enumerated types of
14 property commonly subject to recycling by secondary
15 metals recyclers. It is also unlawful to possess or
16 control the property if the property has been stolen.
17 This bill would provide certain minimum periods
18 of incarceration for violations and would also add
19 certain types of copper wire within the list of
20 protected property.
