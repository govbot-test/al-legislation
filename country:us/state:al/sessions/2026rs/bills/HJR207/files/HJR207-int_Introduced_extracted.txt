7 WHEREAS, the Alabama Legislature takes great pride in
8 recognizing outstanding accomplishments by our state's
9 public schools and public school educators; and
10 WHEREAS, at the Alabama Council of Administrators of
11 Special Education (ALA-CASE) spring conference held in
12 Birmingham February 22-26, Houston County Schools employees
13 brought home four awards; and
14 WHEREAS, Dr. Misty Freeman was named Director of the
15 Year, Margaret Valenza and Haden Brown received Teacher of
16 the Year honors, and Sylvia Ketron was named
17 Paraprofessional of the Year; and
18 WHEREAS, in addition, the Houston County School
19 District was recognized at the State Board of Education
20 Meeting in February by the State Board, State Superintendent
21 Dr. Eric Mackey, and Governor Kay Ivey for receiving the Top
22 County System Performance Award; Houston County ranked first
23 out of all 67 counties on its achievement scores for the
24 Alabama Comprehensive Assessment Program (ACAP) summative
25 test; and
26 WHEREAS, the accomplishments of Houston County
27 Schools bring great pride not only to the local community
28 but to the entire State of Alabama; now therefore,
Page 1
but to the entire State of Alabama; now therefore,
HJR207 INTRODUCED
29 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
30 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That Houston County Schools is
31 hereby commended and congratulated on its outstanding
32 performance at the ALA-CASE spring conference, as well as
33 exceptional achievement on the ACAP, and we offer this
34 resolution as a symbol of the high esteem we hold for our
35 state's public schools and the educators who guide them.
