4 HR____ MOURNING THE DEATH AND CELEBRATING THE LIFE OF
5 PHILLIP MAYS.
6
7 WHEREAS, it is with profound sorrow that we note the
8 passing of Phillip Mays of Montgomery, Alabama, on January
9 19, 2026, while we also celebrate his beautiful, fulfilling
10 life; and
11 WHEREAS, his departure leaves a void in the lives of
12 his loved ones, and he will be lovingly remembered by the
13 countless lives he touched; and
14 WHEREAS, Phillip Mays was born on September 25, 1946,
15 in Crawford, Mississippi, to his mother, Joyce Elaine
16 Sydney; he was united in marriage to his best friend and the
17 love of his life, Vera Walls, in 1970; they shared a happy
18 home filled with mutual love and respect; and
19 WHEREAS, Phillip Mays proudly served his country as a
20 Corporal in the United States Marine Corps; he served with
21 distinction in the Vietnam War; and he continued his
22 commitment to service as a member of the National Guard in
23 Clarke County; his dedication, discipline, and sense of duty
24 were evident throughout his life; and
25 WHEREAS, Philip was an accomplished scholar, having
26 graduated from Auburn University and Troy University; he
27 built a meaningful career with the State of Alabama as a
28 Mental Health Psychologist III; he was also a licensed
