4 Relating to elections; to require the judge of probate
5 of each county to conduct a post-election audit after every
6 county and statewide general election to determine the
7 accuracy of the originally reported results of the election.
8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
9 Section 1.(a) This section shall be known and may be
10 cited as the Alabama Post-Election Audit Act.
11 (b)(1) Notwithstanding any other law to the contrary,
12 the judge of probate of each county shall order a
13 post-election audit, after every countywide and statewide
14 general election, of all ballots in one precinct of a
15 countywide or statewide race randomly selected by the
16 canvassing board of each county as further provided in
17 subdivision (2), which appeared on the ballot and which is not
18 subject to a recount. Nothing in this section shall prohibit
19 the judge of probate from ordering the audit of additional
20 precincts and races. The order shall include the name of the
21 precincts and the races to be audited. The order shall
22 authorize the sheriff or other county authority to provide
23 access to the ballot containers along with any other necessary
24 election materials, including electronic data.
25 (2) The canvassing board of each county , at noon on
26 the second Friday after any county and statewide general
27 election, shall randomly select at least one precinct,
28 excluding absentee and provisional ballots, and at least one

31 (3) The judge of probate, at noon on the second Friday
32 after any county and statewide general election, shall select
33 the date, time, and place of meeting to conduct the
34 post-election audit and shall appoint an inspector and the
35 appropriate number of other poll workers required to complete
36 the audit in the same manner as for any other election. The
37 notification and publication of these appointments shall be in
38 the same manner as provided in Section 17-8-2, Code of Alabama
39 1975. Compensation for poll workers shall be the same as
40 provided in Section 17-8-12, Code of Alabama 1975.
41 (4) To begin the audit process, the voted ballot
42 containers subject to the audit shall be delivered, unopened
43 and still sealed in the original containers, to the inspectors
44 in charge of the audit by the custodian of the ballot
45 containers along with any other necessary election materials,
46 including electronic data.
47 (5) The post-election audit shall commence no earlier
48 than 31 days following the statewide general election or the
49 expiration of the time period for filing an election contest,
50 whichever is less, and shall be completed within 30 days of
51 commencement.
52 (6) At the conclusion of the audit, the ballots shall
53 be returned to their original containers and sealed. All other
54 documents and materials related to the audit, including, but
55 not limited to, inspector and poll worker oaths, poll watcher
56 oaths, seals and testing materials for any tabulator used in

79 any general election. The judge of probate shall give notice
80 to the public of the date, time, and location of the audit by
