12 A BILL
13 TO BE ENTITLED
14 AN ACT
15
16 Relating to elections; to require the judge of probate
17 of each county to conduct a post-election audit after every
18 county and statewide general election to determine the
19 accuracy of the originally reported results of the election.
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
21 Section 1.(a) This section shall be known and may be
22 cited as the Alabama Post-Election Audit Act.
23 (b)(1) Notwithstanding any other law to the contrary,
24 the judge of probate of each county shall order a
25 post-election audit, after every countywide and statewide
26 general election, of all ballots in one precinct of a
27 countywide or statewide race randomly selected by the
28 canvassing board of each county as further provided in
Page 1
canvassing board of each county as further provided in
HB95 INTRODUCED
29 subdivision (2), which appeared on the ballot and which is not
30 subject to a recount. Nothing in this section shall prohibit
31 the judge of probate from ordering the audit of additional
32 precincts and races. The order shall include the name of the
33 precincts and the races to be audited. The order shall
34 authorize the sheriff or other county authority to provide
35 access to the ballot containers along with any other necessary
36 election materials, including electronic data.
37 (2) The canvassing board of each county , at noon on
38 the second Friday after any county and statewide general
39 election, shall randomly select at least one precinct,
40 excluding absentee and provisional ballots, and at least one
41 statewide or countywide race to be audited by the judge of
42 probate.
43 (3) The judge of probate, at noon on the second Friday
44 after any county and statewide general election, shall select
45 the date, time, and place of meeting to conduct the
46 post-election audit and shall appoint an inspector and the
47 appropriate number of other poll workers required to complete
48 the audit in the same manner as for any other election. The
49 notification and publication of these appointments shall be in
50 the same manner as provided in Section 17-8-2, Code of Alabama
51 1975. Compensation for poll workers shall be the same as
52 provided in Section 17-8-12, Code of Alabama 1975.
53 (4) To begin the audit process, the voted ballot
54 containers subject to the audit shall be delivered, unopened
55 and still sealed in the original containers, to the inspectors
56 in charge of the audit by the custodian of the ballot
Page 2
in charge of the audit by the custodian of the ballot

57 containers along with any other necessary election materials,
58 including electronic data.
59 (5) The post-election audit shall be conducted and
60 completed by the time election returns required to be sent to
61 the Secretary of State must be certified.
62 (6) At the conclusion of the audit, the ballots shall
63 be returned to their original containers and sealed. All other
64 documents and materials related to the audit, including, but
65 not limited to, inspector and poll worker oaths, poll watcher
66 oaths, seals and testing materials for any tabulator used in
67 the audit, and copies of orders of the probate court related
68 to the audit shall be placed in a separate container labeled
69 "Record of Audit." The inspector and other poll workers shall
70 sign the containers and return them to the appropriate
71 authority for storage with other records related to the
72 audited election. The judge of probate shall report the
73 findings to the Secretary of State, who shall make all results
74 from the post-election audit public by posting the results on
75 the official website of the Secretary of State.
76 (7) The post-election audit report shall include all of
77 the following:
78 a. A description of any problems or discrepancies
79 encountered.
80 b. A description of the likely cause of any problems or
81 discrepancies encountered.
82 c. Recommendations for corrective or remedial actions.
83 (8) The Secretary of State shall report all findings of
84 the post-election audit to the Governor and the Legislature
Page 3
the post-election audit to the Governor and the Legislature
HB95 INTRODUCED
85 within 30 days of receipt of the audits.
86 (9) Poll watchers shall attend and observe the
87 post-election audit in the same manner as for any other
88 election, and appointments shall be made in the same manner as
89 any general election. The judge of probate shall give notice
90 to the public of the date, time, and location of the audit by
91 publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the
92 county or on the county's official website. Accredited members
93 of the media shall be permitted to be present and observe the
94 audit being performed.
95 (10) The judge of probate may conduct the post-election
96 audit by manual tally or by use of ballot counters, provided
97 that at least 30 randomly selected ballots are individually,
98 manually examined and compared to the ballot counter result
99 for each selected ballot.
100 (c) All expenses incurred by any county as a result of
101 the audit shall be reimbursed in full by the Comptroller.
102 (d) The Secretary of State may adopt rules and
103 prescribe forms to implement this section.
104 Section 2. This act shall become effective immediately.
