4 SJR___ RECOGNIZING THE SERIOUSNESS OF THE NATIONAL DEBT AND
5 ITS THREAT TO NATIONAL SECURITY.
6
7 WHEREAS, in October 2025, the outstanding public debt
8 of the U.S. totaled more than $38.5 trillion, with public
9 debt equaling 122.5 percent of gross domestic product and
10 the debt owed per citizen rising to $110,000; and
11 WHEREAS, more than $9.3 trillion of the federal debt
12 is owned by individuals not located in the U.S., including
13 more than $700 billion owned by individuals in China; and
14 WHEREAS, in fiscal year 2025, the U.S. had a deficit
15 of $1.78 trillion after the U.S. Congress failed to pass a
16 balanced budget and federal tax receipts totaled $5.23
17 trillion, short of the federal outlays totaling $7.01
18 trillion; and
19 WHEREAS, the Social Security and Medicare Boards of
20 Trustees project that the Old-Age and Survivors Insurance
21 Trust Fund and the Federal Hospital Insurance Trust Fund
22 will all be depleted in 2033 and 2034; and
23 WHEREAS, improvements in the business climate in
24 populous countries and aging populations around the world
25 will likely contribute to higher global interest rates,
26 which would squeeze all U.S. policy priorities, including
27 defense and foreign policy; and
28 WHEREAS, on September 22, 2011, former Chairman of

45 regular order for budgeting.
