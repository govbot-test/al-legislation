5 RETIREMENT FROM ALABAMA A&M UNIVERSITY.

7 WHEREAS, it is with profound appreciation and deepest
8 admiration that we recognize Dr. Lloyd Walker for 33 years
9 of steadfast service and leadership as a professor, dean,
10 and research director at Alabama A&M University; we also
11 take this opportunity to heartily congratulate him on the
12 momentous occasion of his retirement, effective January 1,
13 2026; and
14 WHEREAS, colleagues describe Dr. Walker's
15 contributions to reshaping the poultry industry as
16 foundational; however, he humbly credits his work with
17 students as remaining central to all his decisions; he
18 believes that real education means teaching students how to
19 use the knowledge that they have earned and learned; and
20 WHEREAS, Dr. Walker, a native of Port Morant in Saint
21 Thomas Parish, Jamaica, received Bachelor's and Master's
22 degrees from Prairie View A&M University in his home
23 country, as well as a Ph.D. from Texas A&M University in
24 College Station, Texas; his early research focused on how to
25 rapidly increase poultry production without the expense of
26 expanding aging facilities; he was a member of the team that
27 helped refine electrical stimulation techniques to
28 accelerate tenderization, moving poultry more efficiently
