8 WHEREAS, The Links, Incorporated, has a rich and
9 documented legacy of promoting and engaging in educational,
10 civic, and intercultural activities that enrich the lives of
11 members of the Black community at large; and
12 WHEREAS, The Links, Incorporated, has five
13 programming facets, Services to Youth, The Arts, National
14 Trends and Services, International Trends and Services, and
15 Health and Human Services, as the cornerstone of its
16 community service; and
17 WHEREAS, The Links, Incorporated, implements programs
18 and initiatives that address the most critical needs of
19 underserved communities; and
20 WHEREAS, the Southern Area of The Links,
21 Incorporated, is comprised of 83 chapters across seven
22 states and the Commonwealth of the Bahamas; and
23 WHEREAS, there are more than 5,000 members of the
24 Southern Area of The Links, Incorporated, who hail from the
25 Deep South states of Mississippi and Alabama; the French
26 traditions and Creole culture of Louisiana; the coastal
27 wonders of North and South Carolina; the southern charm of
28 Georgia; the Emerald Coast and sugar beaches of sunny
Page 1
Georgia; the Emerald Coast and sugar beaches of sunny
HJR171 INTRODUCED
29 Florida; and the sun-drenched tropical beauty of the
30 Bahamas; and
31 WHEREAS, the Southern Area of The Links,
32 Incorporated, is committed to strengthening families and
33 communities through intentional civic engagement and
34 impactful service, and the Southern Area faces persistent
35 food insecurity that disproportionately affects Black,
36 rural, and low-income households located in our communities;
37 and
38 WHEREAS, the Southern Area of The Links,
39 Incorporated, has implemented the Emerald Plate Initiative
40 to enhance food access, justice, and sovereignty throughout
41 the Southeastern United States; and
42 WHEREAS, reliable, nutritious food access is
43 foundational to good health, educational attainment, and
44 economic stability; and
45 WHEREAS, the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance
46 Program (SNAP) is the nation's most effective anti-hunger
47 program, supports local economies, and can be strengthened
48 by state action on eligibility, access, and outreach; and
49 WHEREAS, farmers are central to the South's
50 agricultural heritage, but Black farmers remain
51 underrepresented and face systemic barriers in land
52 retention, market access, and capital; and
53 WHEREAS, In Alabama, 12–13.1 percent of households
54 were food insecure between 2019–2021; over 2.3 million
55 people faced food insecurity in 2021; SNAP supported 756,768
56 individuals in Fiscal Year 2024, bringing $1.734 billion
Page 2
individuals in Fiscal Year 2024, bringing $1.734 billion
HJR171 INTRODUCED
57 into the state; Feeding Alabama network distributes nearly
58 75 million meals annually, supporting 880,000 food-insecure
59 residents; now therefore,
60 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
61 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That the Southern Area of The
62 Links, Incorporated, urges the Legislature of Alabama to do
63 all of the following:
64 (a) Strengthen SNAP access and eligibility by
65 maintaining broad-based eligibility, raising income
66 thresholds, and reducing administrative barriers.
67 (b) Invest in expanding nutrition incentive programs
68 such as Double Bucks, Fresh Access Bucks, or Healthy Bucks
69 that boost fresh produce access and support local farms.
70 (c) Accept Summer EBT funds as a means to reduce
71 childhood hunger, boost the local economy, and improve
72 health and academic outcomes.
73 (d) Support Black farmers by expanding equitable
74 access to land, credit, procurement opportunities, and
75 heirs' property resolution support.
76 (e) Build sustainable food networks, improve
77 infrastructure in rural and underserved areas, and
78 coordinate disaster nutrition programs.
79 (f) Incentivize community and school-based gardens to
80 enhance public health and wellness, benefit the local
81 economy, and provide community and educational
82 opportunities.
83 (g) Pass and fund Healthy School Meals for All
84 legislation to reduce childhood hunger by providing
Page 3
legislation to reduce childhood hunger by providing
HJR171 INTRODUCED
85 breakfast and lunch to all students at no cost, regardless
86 of the student's household income.
87 (h) Establish a Food Security & Equity Task Force to
88 monitor progress and ensure accountability.
89 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the Southern Area of The
90 Links, Incorporated, stands ready to partner with
91 policymakers, agencies, and community organizations to
92 advance a vision of food equity, health, and justice for all
93 residents of Alabama.
