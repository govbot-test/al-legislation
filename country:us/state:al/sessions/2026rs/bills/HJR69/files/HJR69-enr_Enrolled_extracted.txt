1 Enrolled, An Act,

3 DESIGNATING JANUARY AS NATIONAL BLOOD DONOR MONTH.
4
5 WHEREAS, 56 years ago, January was designated as
6 National Blood Donor Month, as an annual observance meant to
7 honor voluntary blood donors and encourage more people to
8 donate blood at a time when blood supplies were historically
9 low; and
10 WHEREAS, LifeSouth Community Blood Centers have been
11 serving Alabama with the collection and distribution of
12 lifesaving blood and blood products to patients in our local
13 hospitals for more than 30 years; and
14 WHEREAS, in our communities, the need for a diverse
15 blood supply is constant, but the supply is not, making
16 volunteer blood donors the foundation for ensuring a safe
17 and stable supply of blood products is available to help
18 meet the medical needs of patients nationwide; and
19 WHEREAS, a blood transfusion occurs in the U.S. every
20 two seconds but only three percent of the eligible
21 population actually donates blood, bringing about chronic
22 blood shortages nationwide which have exposed the
23 vulnerability of our nation's blood supply and revealed its
24 need to be included in emergency preparedness plans; and
25 WHEREAS, O negative is the universal blood type,
26 represented by just six percent of the population and often
27 used in trauma situations, making it the most needed blood
28 type and most likely to be in short supply when there is a

29 blood emergency; and
30 WHEREAS, LifeSouth Community Blood Centers serve
31 Emergency Medical Services in the State of Alabama, allowing
32 EMS to transfuse trauma victims at the scene of an accident
33 before arrival at the hospital, increasing survival rates;
34 and
35 WHEREAS, patients requiring blood transfusions
36 include cancer patients; accident, burn, or trauma victims;
37 newborn babies and their mothers; transplant recipients;
38 surgery patients; chronically transfused patients suffering
39 from sickle cell disease or thalassemia; and many more; now
40 therefore,
41 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
42 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That the month of January is
43 hereby recognized as National Blood Donor Month in the State
44 of Alabama.
45 BE IT FURTHER REESOLVED, That we urge all citizens to
46 celebrate the cause of blood donation and recognize
47 volunteer blood donors for their generous gifts. We further
48 urge all eligible blood donors to donate regularly and
49 encourage businesses and organizations throughout the State
50 of Alabama to sponsor community-based blood drives with
51 LifeSouth Community Blood Centers, among other blood
52 collection institutions, and provide citizens of our state
53 with the opportunity to give.
