4 HJR___ DESIGNATING JANUARY AS NATIONAL BLOOD DONOR MONTH.
5
6 WHEREAS, 56 years ago, January was designated as
7 National Blood Donor Month, as an annual observance meant to
8 honor voluntary blood donors and encourage more people to
9 donate blood at a time when blood supplies were historically
10 low; and
11 WHEREAS, LifeSouth Community Blood Centers have been
12 serving Alabama with the collection and distribution of
13 lifesaving blood and blood products to patients in our local
14 hospitals for more than 30 years; and
15 WHEREAS, in our communities, the need for a diverse
16 blood supply is constant, but the supply is not, making
17 volunteer blood donors the foundation for ensuring a safe
18 and stable supply of blood products is available to help
19 meet the medical needs of patients nationwide; and
20 WHEREAS, a blood transfusion occurs in the U.S. every
21 two seconds but only three percent of the eligible
22 population actually donates blood, bringing about chronic
23 blood shortages nationwide which have exposed the
24 vulnerability of our nation's blood supply and revealed its
25 need to be included in emergency preparedness plans; and
26 WHEREAS, O negative is the universal blood type,
27 represented by just six percent of the population and often
28 used in trauma situations, making it the most needed blood

45 of Alabama.
46 BE IT FURTHER REESOLVED, That we urge all citizens to
47 celebrate the cause of blood donation and recognize
48 volunteer blood donors for their generous gifts. We further

50 encourage businesses and organizations throughout the State
51 of Alabama to sponsor community-based blood drives with
52 LifeSouth Community Blood Centers, among other blood
53 collection institutions, and provide citizens of our state
54 with the opportunity to give.
