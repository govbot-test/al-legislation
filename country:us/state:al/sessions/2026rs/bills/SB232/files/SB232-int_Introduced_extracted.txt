5 This bill would authorize a volunteer fire

10 A BILL
11 TO BE ENTITLED
12 AN ACT
13
14 Relating to volunteer fire departments; to amend
15 Section 9-3-18.1, Code of Alabama 1975; to authorize volunteer
16 fire departments to deposit, invest, or manage funds in a
17 federally insured financial institution.
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
19 Section 1. Section 9-3-18.1, Code of Alabama 1975, is
20 amended to read as follows:
21 "§9-3-18.1
22 (a) For the purposes of this section, the following
23 words have the following meanings:
24 (1) DEPARTMENT. A volunteer fire department certified
25 by the State Forestry Commission and meeting the requirements
26 of Section 9-3-17.
27 (2) FEDERALLY INSURED FINANCIAL INSTITUTION. Any bank
28 or credit union in which the deposits are insured by the
Page 1
or credit union in which the deposits are insured by the
SB232 INTRODUCED
29 Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation or the National Credit
30 Union Administration and the bank or credit union is chartered
31 under state or federal law.
32 (2)(3) SQUAD. A volunteer rescue squad located in
33 Alabama which is a member of the Alabama Association of Rescue
34 Squads and is either administered by a board of directors or
35 by its membership that maintains recorded minutes of its
36 decisions.
37 (3)(4) STATION. The location where a volunteer fire
38 department or a volunteer rescue squad meets, trains, and
39 stores its motorized and nonmotorized firefighting or rescue
40 equipment.
41 (4)(5) VOLUNTEER FIRST RESPONDER. An individual who is
42 a member of a volunteer fire department or a member of a
43 volunteer rescue squad.
44 (b) To provide for the health and safety of volunteer
45 first responders, a department or squad may use funds that are
46 designated as restricted for accounting purposes by the
47 Department of Examiners of Public Accounts to purchase the
48 following:
49 (1) Kitchen equipment for the station, including
50 refrigeration equipment, cooking equipment, microwaves, and
51 other kitchen equipment. All kitchen equipment shall be the
52 property of the department or squad.
53 (2) Food to be stored or refrigerated at the station
54 for on-site consumption by volunteer first responders and
55 electrolyte replacement or sports drinks, water, and similar
56 liquid sustenance in any form for use by volunteer first
Page 2
liquid sustenance in any form for use by volunteer first
SB232 INTRODUCED
57 responders during training or on a fire or rescue call.
58 (c) Any funds expended pursuant to this section shall
59 be accounted for and maintained for a minimum of three years
60 by the department or squad. Detailed records of all
61 expenditures to be maintained shall include, but not be
62 limited to, all of the following:
63 (1) All items purchased in any calendar year.
64 (2) The cost of each item purchased in any calendar
65 year.
66 (3) The location of each item purchased in any calendar
67 year.
68 (4) The total amount expended for each calendar year.
69 (d)(1) Any department or squad may deposit, invest, and
70 manage funds of the department or squad in any federally
71 insured financial institution. The funds may not be commingled
72 with private funds.
73 (2) Every deposit of public funds under subdivision (1)
74 shall comply with all security, reporting, and auditing
75 requirements under local, state, and federal law.
76 (d)(e) A county commission shall not be responsible for
77 the expenditures by or the activities of a volunteer fire
78 department or a volunteer rescue squad pursuant to this
79 section.
80 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
81 1, 2026.
