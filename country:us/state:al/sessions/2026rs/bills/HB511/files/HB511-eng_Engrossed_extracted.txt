6 TO BE ENTITLED
7 AN ACT
8
9 Proposing an amendment to the Constitution of Alabama
10 of 2022, to require local boards of education to adopt
11 policies requiring each public K-12 school to conduct The
12 Pledge of Allegiance to the United States flag each day; to
13 authorize local boards of education to adopt policies allowing
14 student-initiated and student-led prayer to be conducted in
15 public K-12 schools; and to provide for the report and
16 investigation of violations.
17 Section 1. The following amendment to the Constitution
18 of Alabama of 2022, is proposed:
19 PROPOSED AMENDMENT
20 (a) Each local board of education shall adopt a policy
21 requiring each public K-12 school under its jurisdiction, at
22 the beginning of each school day, to conduct The Pledge of
23 Allegiance to the United States flag and give all students the
24 opportunity to voluntarily recite The Pledge of Allegiance to
25 the United States flag.
26 (b) Each local board of education shall adopt a policy
27 requiring each public K-12 school under its jurisdiction to
28 allow a prayer to be conducted, provided that:
Page 1
allow a prayer to be conducted, provided that:
HB511 Engrossed
29 (1) The prayer must be initiated by and led by a
30 student; and
31 (2) No student may be required to participate in a
32 prayer.
33 (c) Any policy adopted by a local board of education
34 pursuant to this section shall ensure that students have the
35 right to opt out of participation in the Pledge of Allegiance
36 or the prayer based on their own beliefs.
37 (d) Violations of this section may be reported to the
38 local superintendent of education. The local superintendent of
39 education shall review the violation.
40 Upon ratification of this constitutional amendment, the
41 Code Commissioner shall number and place this amendment as
42 appropriate in the constitution omitting this instructional
43 paragraph and may make the following nonsubstantive revisions:
44 change capitalization, hierarchy, spelling, and punctuation
45 for purposes of style and uniformity; correct manifest
46 grammatical, clerical, and typographical errors; revise
47 internal or external citations and cross-references; harmonize
48 language; and translate effective dates.
49 END OF PROPOSED AMENDMENT
50 Section 2. An election upon the proposed amendment
51 shall be held in accordance with Sections 284 and 285 of the
52 Constitution of Alabama of 2022, and the election laws of this
53 state. The appropriate election official shall assign a ballot
54 number for the proposed constitutional amendment on the
55 election ballot and shall set forth the following description
56 of the substance or subject matter of the proposed

69 Section 3. The proposed amendment shall become valid as
70 a part of the Constitution of Alabama of 2022, when approved
71 by a majority of the qualified electors voting thereon.
