4 Relating to day care centers; to require the Department
5 of Human Resources to create an anaphylactic response policy;
6 to require all licensed day care centers to adopt and
7 implement the anaphylactic response policy; and to require all
8 licensed day care center employees to complete an anaphylaxis
9 training program.
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
11 Section 1. (a) For the purposes of this section, the
12 term "employee" means an individual who is hired to facilitate
13 the care of children attending day care centers. This
14 definition does not include service staff, such as cooks,
15 janitors, or bus drivers.
16 (b) Section 38-7-22 is added to the Code of Alabama to
17 read as follows:
18 §38-7-22.
19 (a)(1) On or before August 1, 2027, the State
20 Department of Human Resources shall establish an anaphylactic
21 response policy that sets forth guidelines and procedures
22 required for day care centers and trained staff to prevent a
23 child from suffering from anaphylaxis and to be used during a
24 medical emergency resulting from anaphylaxis.
25 (2) On and after January 1, 2028, all day care centers
26 shall implement the anaphylactic response policy developed by
27 the State Department of Human Resources pursuant to this
28 section.

29 (3) On and after January 1, 2028, upon enrollment of a
30 child at a licensed day care center, and annually thereafter,
31 the day care center shall notify the parent or guardian of the
32 anaphylactic response policy in writing.
33 (4) The policy shall be developed in consultation with
34 the Alabama Department of Public Health and the Alabama State
35 Board of Pharmacy and shall be based on the guidance and
36 recommendations provided by the American Academy of Pediatrics
37 relating to the management of food allergies or other
38 allergies.
39 (5) At minimum, the anaphylactic response policy shall
40 require all of the following:
41 a. A procedure and treatment plan, including emergency
42 protocols and responsibilities, for trained employees
43 responding to a child suffering, or reasonably believed to be
44 suffering, from anaphylaxis.
45 b. Appropriate guidelines for each day care center to
46 develop an individual emergency plan for children with an
47 allergy that could result in anaphylaxis. These plans shall be
48 kept by the day care center and followed in the event of an
49 emergency. All employees must be aware of which children have
50 individual emergency plans and where the plans are stored.
51 c. Strategies for reducing the risk of exposure to
52 anaphylactic-causative agents, including food and other
53 allergens.
54 (b) Beginning on or before August 1, 2027, all
55 employees of licensed day care centers must complete an
56 anaphylaxis training program developed by the Alabama

anaphylaxis training program developed by the Alabama

57 Department of Public Health pursuant to Section 22-1-16 and
58 shall complete this program or subsequent online training
59 developed by the Alabama Department of Public Health every two
60 years thereafter. Each day care center shall issue a
61 certificate, as developed by the Alabama Department of Public
62 Health, to each employee who successfully completes the
63 anaphylaxis training program. Each day care center shall keep
64 on file a copy of all issued certificates.
65 (c) Day care centers that opt to acquire and keep stock
66 of nonpatient-specific epinephrine delivery systems on site as
67 authorized entities pursuant to Section 22-1-16 are subject to
68 and must comply with all requirements, immunity provisions,
69 and liability provisions as prescribed by that section.
70 (d) The following persons shall not be liable for any
71 injuries or related damages that result from any act or
72 omission taken pursuant to this section, provided this
73 immunity does not apply to an act or omission constituting
74 willful or wanton conduct:
75 (1) An authorized entity that possesses and makes
76 available epinephrine delivery systems and its employees,
77 agents, and other individuals, pursuant to Section 22-1-16.
78 (2) An individual or entity that conducts the training
79 described in this section, but only to the extent the injuries
80 or related damages arise from the training conducted by the
81 individual or entity. The immunity provided in this subsection
82 does not affect a manufacturer's liability regarding the
83 design, manufacture, and instructions regarding the use of, or
84 training regarding the use of, an epinephrine delivery system.
