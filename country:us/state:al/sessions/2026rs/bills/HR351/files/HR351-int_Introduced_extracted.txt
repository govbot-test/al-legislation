4 HR____ HONORING THE LIFE AND LEGACY OF BARBARA CURRY-STORY.
5
6 WHEREAS, we take great pride and pleasure in honoring
7 the distinguished life and legacy of Barbara Curry-Story, a
8 courageous trailblazer whose strength and perseverance
9 helped shape the course of history in the State of Alabama;
10 and
11 WHEREAS, Barbara Curry-Story, born Barbara Crook on
12 November 24, 1941, in Ohatchee, Alabama, was raised during
13 segregation and overcame significant barriers through
14 determination and a strong commitment to education and
15 opportunity; and
16 WHEREAS, in the fall of 1965, at the height of the
17 Civil Rights Movement and during the final years of Jim
18 Crow, she made history as the first African American student
19 to attend Jacksonville State University,; and
20 WHEREAS, after growing up and attending school in
21 Alabama, she spent five years in New York before returning
22 home as a young single mother determined to build a better
23 future for her child; she enrolled at Jacksonville State
24 University and commuted daily under difficult and often
25 intimidating circumstances, remaining resolute in her
26 pursuit of higher education; and
27 WHEREAS, upon entering Angle Hall for her first
28 class, she was met not with public protest, but with silence
