1 Enrolled, An Act,

3 COMMENDING THE ALABAMA BIRTH EQUITY INITIATIVE FOR ITS
4 EFFORTS TO MEET THE NEEDS OF EXPECTING MOTHERS THROUGHOUT
5 THE STATE.
6
7 WHEREAS, Alabama has one of the highest infant and
8 maternal mortality rates in the United States, and 34
9 percent of Alabama's counties have been classified as
10 maternity health care deserts due to a lack of birthing
11 facilities and prenatal health care providers, which
12 disproportionately affects rural Alabama and Black and
13 Indigenous populations; and
14 WHEREAS, the Alabama Birth Equity Initiative is a
15 Black women-led coalition of reproductive health and mutual
16 aid organizations who have allied to combat the state's
17 maternal mortality crisis by building community-centered
18 solutions, including midwifery training, mobile health, and
19 policy advocacy to ensure safe, dignified, and equitable
20 birthing care; they focus on integrated responses to
21 systemic issues such as medical racism, food insecurity, and
22 violence; and
23 WHEREAS, in its goal to expand midwifery, the
24 initiative is launching a midwifery training pilot program
25 to fund the preceptor training of eight student Certified
26 Practicing Midwives and two Certified Nurse Midwives at the
27 Alabama Birth Center in Huntsville and Oasis Women's Health
28 in Birmingham; the initiative also has plans to advocate for

29 policy changes that will fund rural birth centers and
30 increase availability to prenatal care; in addition, the
31 initiative is planning mobile health units to provide
32 reproductive health care services in rural communities; and,
33 with the knowledge that a safe birth starts long before
34 labor begins, the coalition will address the wraparound
35 issues of stable housing, reliable transportation, 24-hour
36 food pantries, and child care support; the initiative wants
37 women to not just survive childbirth, but to also thrive as
38 mothers; and
39 WHEREAS, Alabama's maternal health crisis is not just
40 about a lack of doctors or closed hospitals; it is about the
41 way policy, poverty, and racism have worked together to make
42 safe birth a privilege instead of a right; now therefore,
43 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
44 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That the Alabama Birth Equity
45 Initiative is highly commended for combining the missions of
46 its member organizations to address the maternal health
47 crisis among Alabama's rural, Black, and Indigenous
48 communities by advocating for policy changes, piloting a
49 midwife program, instituting the concept of mobile health
50 units, and addressing the wraparound issues that are a
51 factor in successful births; we offer this resolution in
52 support of the initiative, its members, and its mission to
53 provide prenatal care and safe birthing centers to Alabama's
54 neglected communities.
