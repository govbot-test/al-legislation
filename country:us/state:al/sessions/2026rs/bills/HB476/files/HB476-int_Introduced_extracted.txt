4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing rule, persons must have a permit
6 issued by the Department of Conservation and Natural
7 Resources to possess protected wild birds or animals.
8 This bill would provide that individuals may
9 care for injured or orphaned wild birds and animals
10 that are not federally protected.
11
12
13 A BILL
14 TO BE ENTITLED
15 AN ACT
16
17 Relating to conservation; to establish the Alabama Good
18 Samaritan Wildlife Rehabilitation Act to authorize individuals
19 to provide care to injured or orphaned wild birds and animals
20 under certain conditions.
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
22 Section 1. (a) This section shall be known and may be
23 cited as the Alabama Good Samaritan Wildlife Rehabilitation
24 Act.
25 (b) Notwithstanding any law to the contrary, an
26 individual may in good faith provide care or treatment to
27 sustain life or reduce disability to any injured, orphaned, or
28 debilitated wild bird or animal that is not federally
Page 1
debilitated wild bird or animal that is not federally
HB476 INTRODUCED
29 protected or otherwise endangered so that the bird or animal
30 may be returned to the wild subject to all of the following:
31 (1) The length of care or treatment of a wild bird or
32 animal may not exceed six months, except the length of care
33 for beavers may not exceed three years.
34 (2) A wild bird or animal shall be returned upon
35 release to the general area from where it was collected.
36 (3) A raccoon, skunk, bat, fox, coyote, or bobcat shall
37 be vaccinated for rabies before being released if a veterinary
38 facility that treats such animals is within five miles of
39 where the animal is housed.
40 (c) Nothing in this section shall be construed to do
41 any of the following:
42 (1) Authorize an individual engaged in wildlife
43 rehabilitation to engage in the practice of veterinary
44 medicine as defined in Section 34-29-61, Code of Alabama 1975.
45 (2) Exempt an individual from the crime of cruelty to
46 animals as provided in Section 13A-11-14, Code of Alabama
47 1975.
48 (3) Authorize an individual to keep or consider a wild
49 bird or animal as a pet.
50 (d) A municipality may adopt ordinances further
51 providing for the care or treatment of wild birds and animals
52 not inconsistent with this section or state or federal law.
53 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
54 1, 2026.
