4 SYNOPSIS:
5 This bill would establish the Alabama Health
6 Command as an independent agency tasked with
7 designating a virtual AI-assisted health platform that
8 may be used in conjunction with rural hospitals,
9 clinics, and health care clinicians that allows
10 consumers in rural areas of the state to connect with a
11 rural health care provider and receive other
12 health-related services, such as wellness education and
13 follow-up assistance, in a virtual encounter.
14 This bill would require the Alabama Health
15 Command to establish standards to ensure patient
16 privacy and ensure the platform does not provide any
17 practice that constitutes the practice of a health care
18 profession.
19 This bill would also exempt rural hospitals and
20 other rural health care providers and state health
21 institutions that contract to use the virtual
22 AI-assisted health platform from competitive bid laws.

67 Section 2. (a) As used in this section, the following
68 terms have the following meanings:
69 (1) AVATAR-BASED VIRTUAL ENCOUNTER SYSTEM. A digital,
70 interactive platform that enables a consumer to participate in
71 a virtual health-related encounter through one or more
72 computer-generated avatars representing an automated
73 interface, a participating health care provider, a user, or a
74 partner institution tethered together within a single, unified
75 visual environment. The system presents the consumer
76 interaction together with identifying information of
77 participating rural or remote health care providers,
78 accreditation indicators, or other authorized entities, and
79 may link or tether avatar interactions to health-related data
80 for the purposes of health management services, navigation,
81 education, referral, and the contextual presentation of
82 health-related information, resources, or promotions relevant
83 to the consumer's needs. The term does not include the
84 diagnosis, treatment, or prescription of medical conditions
