4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Existing law does not authorize, prohibit, or
6 regulate surrogacy.
7 This bill would require that, if a child is born
8 to a surrogate in this state, the probate court in that
9 county shall confirm the identity of the intended
10 parent or parents and ensure that the child is
11 relinquished directly to an intended parent.
12 This bill would require intended parents to
13 provide a certified copy of the identification order to
14 the hospital, birthing center, or other facility where
15 the child is born, and would prohibit a child born via
16 surrogacy from being relinquished to the custody of any
17 individual who is not an intended parent identified by
18 the probate court.
19 This bill would provide that, if an intended
20 parent does not physically appear in this state to
21 retrieve the child, the State Department of Human
22 Resources shall temporarily assume custody of the child
23 until such time that an intended parent provides a
24 certified copy of the identification order and
25 physically retrieves the child.
26 This bill would allow the department to adopt
27 rules.

45 Section 1. (a) If any child is born in this state to a

51 parent or parents.
