4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Existing law establishes a standard procedure
6 for requesting public records.
7 This bill would require the custodian of public
8 records to redact certain information that could be
9 used to initiate a financial transaction such as bank
10 account numbers and routing numbers in any requested
11 public record.
12 This bill would prohibit persons from using the
13 names and addresses of persons in public records held
14 by local boards of education, or from selling or
15 offering to a third party the use of the names and
16 addresses of persons in public records held by local
17 boards of education, for the purpose of soliciting
18 those persons to purchase goods or services.
19 This bill would authorize the Attorney General
20 to impose civil penalties for violations of this
21 prohibition.
22 This bill would authorize the custodian of a
23 public record held by a local board of education who
24 reasonably believes the record may be used in violation
25 of this prohibition to require a written certification
26 from the requesting person.
27 This bill would also authorize the State Board
28 of Education to adopt rules to implement this bill.

33 AN ACT

35 Relating to public records requests; to require
36 redaction from requested public records information that could
37 be used to initiate a financial transaction; to prohibit the
38 use of public records provided by a local board of education,
39 which contain lists of names and addresses of individuals or
40 entities, for the purpose of soliciting those individuals or
41 entities; to authorize the Attorney General to impose civil
42 penalties for violations; and to authorize the State Board of
43 Education to adopt rules.
44 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
45 Section 1. When any records relating to a public
46 contract for goods or services are requested pursuant to
47 Article 3 of Chapter 12 of Title 36, Code of Alabama 1975, the
48 public officer having custody of the records shall redact
49 account numbers, tax identification numbers, routing numbers,
50 employee identification numbers, and similar information that
51 may be used to initiate a financial transaction.
52 Section 2. (a) No person shall knowingly sell, give, or
53 receive any list of names and addresses contained in or
54 derived from public records requested from a local board of
55 education for the purpose of selling or offering for sale any
56 property or service to those individuals or entities listed
