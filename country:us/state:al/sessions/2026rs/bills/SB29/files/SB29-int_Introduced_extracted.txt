4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, a member of the State Board
6 of Chiropractic Examiners must be a graduate of a
7 chiropractic school that required actual attendance in
8 the school to graduate.
9 This bill would remove the requirement that, for
10 purposes of board member qualification, the school that
11 a board member graduated from must have required actual
12 attendance to graduate.
13 Under existing law, applicants for a license to
14 practice chiropractic in the state must be a graduate
15 of a chiropractic school that teaches only attendance
16 courses and requires a four-year standard college
17 course.
18 This bill would remove the requirements that
19 such schools teach only attendance courses and require
20 a four-year standard college course.
21 Under existing law, certain applicants for
22 licensure must have a bachelor's degree or an academic
23 graduate degree and have completed a standardized test
24 commensurate with the graduate degree.
25 This bill would remove the requirement that
26 applicants must hold a bachelor's or other academic
27 graduate degree to qualify for licensure.
