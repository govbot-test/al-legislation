3 Relating to Escambia County; to amend Section
4 45-27-234, Code of Alabama 1975, to further provide for the
5 qualifications of the sheriff.
6 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
7 Section 1. Section 45-27-234, Code of Alabama 1975, is
8 amended to read as follows:
9 "§45-27-234
10 (a) On or after May 18, 2020Beginning June 1, 2026, a
11 person an individual qualifying for election as Sheriff of
12 Escambia County or any person individual appointed to serve as
13 a sheriff shall meet all of the following minimum
14 qualifications, in addition to any other qualifications
15 required by law:
16 (1) The person individual is a citizen of the United
17 States.
18 (2) The person individual has been a resident of the
19 county for at least one year immediately prior to the
20 qualification date.
21 (3) The person individual has the qualifications of an
22 elector pursuant to state and federal law and the person has
23 been registered to vote in the county at least one year
24 immediately prior to qualifying.
25 (4) The person individual has been awarded a high
26 school diploma or a GED equivalent.
27 (5) The person individual is 25 years of age or older
28 prior to qualifying.

29 (6) The person individual has three or more years of
30 full-time prior service as a law enforcement officer having
31 the power of arrest, which service is certified by the Alabama
32 Peace Officers' Standards and Training Commission, and the
33 person has not been retired or separated from full-time active
34 law enforcement certified through the Alabama Peace Officers'
35 Standards and Training Commission for more than two
36 consecutive years one year from the date of qualifying, or in
37 the event the person individual has been retired or separated
38 from active law enforcement for more than two consecutive
39 yearsone year, the person individual has obtained a
40 recertification of full-time active law enforcement status as
41 a law enforcement officer from the Alabama Peace Officers'
42 Standards and Training Commission prior to the date of
43 qualifying. In addition, if the individual has ever retired
44 from active law enforcement, he or she has three or more years
45 of full-time post-retirement law enforcement service prior to
46 the date of qualifying.
47 (7) The person individual has never been convicted of a
48 felony criminal offense in this state or convicted of a
49 criminal offense in another jurisdiction where a conviction of
50 the offense would be considered a felony offense in this
51 state.
52 (b) Upon election, the sheriff of the county shall
53 attend annually a minimum of 12 hours of executive level
54 continuing education approved by the Alabama Sheriff'sSheriffs
55 Association, National Sheriff'sSheriffs' Association, or any
56 other recognized executive level continuing education program
