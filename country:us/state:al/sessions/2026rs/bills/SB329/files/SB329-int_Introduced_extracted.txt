4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Existing law authorizes the issuance of
6 distinctive motor vehicle license plates.
7 This bill would authorize the issuance of a
8 distinctive license plate decal to individuals who live
9 with invisible disabilities and to the primary
10 caregivers of those individuals.
11
12
13 A BILL
14 TO BE ENTITLED
15 AN ACT
16
17 Relating to motor vehicle registration and license
18 plates; to authorize the issuance of a distinctive license
19 plate decal to individuals who have certain medical conditions
20 and to the primary caregivers of those individuals.
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
22 Section 1. (a) For purposes of this section, the term
23 "invisible disability" means a physical or mental condition
24 that may interfere with an individual's ability to communicate
25 with a first responder, including:
26 (1) A communication impediment;
27 (2) Hearing loss;
28 (3) Blindness or a visual impairment;
Page 1
(3) Blindness or a visual impairment;
SB329 INTRODUCED
29 (4) Autism spectrum disorder;
30 (5) Traumatic brain injury;
31 (6) Schizophrenia;
32 (7) Alzheimer's disease or dementia;
33 (8) Post traumatic stress disorder;
34 (9) Epilepsy;
35 (10) A developmental disability;
36 (11) Down syndrome;
37 (12) Diabetes;
38 (13) A heart condition; or
39 (14) A cognitive disability.
40 (b) Notwithstanding Sections 32-6-64, 32-6-65, 32-6-67,
41 and 32-6-68, Code of Alabama 1975, an owner of a motor vehicle
42 who is a resident of Alabama and who has been diagnosed with
43 an invisible disability or an individual who is the primary
44 caretaker of an individual who has been diagnosed with an
45 invisible disability shall be issued a license plate of the
46 individual's choosing and a "Invisible Disability" license
47 plate decal upon:
48 (1) Application to the judge of probate, license
49 commissioner, or other issuing official;
50 (2) Compliance with motor vehicle registration and
51 licensing laws;
52 (3) Payment of regular fees required by law for license
53 plates for private passenger or pleasure motor vehicles;
54 (4) Payment of any additional fees required by law for
55 a distinctive license plate, should the individual seek to be
56 issued a distinctive license plate; and
Page 2
issued a distinctive license plate; and
SB329 INTRODUCED
57 (5) Presentation of a diagnosis from a physician
58 licensed to practice medicine in this state as proof of
59 eligibility.
60 (c) The validity of the "Invisible Disability" license
61 plate decals shall coincide with the reissuance of the
62 registrant's license plate as provided in Section 32-6-62,
63 Code of Alabama 1975.
64 (d) Payment of required license fees and taxes for the
65 years during which a new plate is not issued shall be
66 evidenced as provided in Section 32-6-63, Code of Alabama
67 1975.
68 (e) Kulturecity, Incorporated shall be responsible for
69 the design of the license plate decal subject to approval by
70 the Commissioner of Revenue and compliance with all laws and
71 rules.
72 (f) The "Invisible Disability" decal provided by this
73 section shall not qualify the individual to be parked in
74 special access parking as provided in Sections 40-12-300 and
75 32-6-231, Code of Alabama 1975, unless the individual has been
76 issued a disability distinctive special access plate or
77 placard as provided for in those sections.
78 (g) The Department of Revenue may adopt rules to
79 implement this section.
80 Section 2. This act shall become effective on January
81 1, 2027.
