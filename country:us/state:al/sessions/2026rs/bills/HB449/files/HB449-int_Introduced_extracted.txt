4 SYNOPSIS:
6 amendment proposed in House Bill xxx of the 2026 Regular
7 Session to authorize a state lottery by creating the
8 Alabama Lottery Corporation as a public corporation
9 tasked with operating and managing the lottery.
10 This bill would establish the Alabama Lottery
11 Trust Fund, which would be used by the Legislature each
12 year to provide supplemental appropriations for only the
13 following purposes: (i) to address problem gambling
14 additions and treatment; (ii) to establish a
15 postsecondary scholarship program for public two-year
16 community and technical colleges and four-year public
17 institutions of higher education; (iii) to provide
18 cost-of-living raises and other benefits for active
19 public education employees and bonuses for retired
20 education employees; (iv) to award grants to hospitals to
21 assist in their operation and prevent their closure; and
22 (v) to provide tax credits to eligible residents of the
23 state as determined by the Legislature.
24 This bill would establish the Alabama Lottery
25 Corporation Board of Directors to supervise the state
26 lottery and would provide for the appointment and duties
27 of the board.
28 This bill would provide that the lottery

29 corporation would be under the day-to-day supervision of
30 a chief executive officer who would be the president of
31 the corporation, and would provide for his or her
32 appointment and duties.
33 This bill would impose lottery advertising
34 restrictions on the corporation to limit advertisements
35 with a strong appeal to minors.
36 This bill would provide criminal penalties for
37 sales of lottery tickets to minors, underage purchase of
38 lottery tickets, making of counterfeit lottery tickets,
39 skimming of lottery proceeds, and theft of a lottery
40 prize by fraud.
41 This bill would require lottery tickets to be
42 printed onto paper, plastic, or other tangible material,
43 with exceptions.
44 This bill would amend various gambling-related
45 offenses to increase the penalties and provide heightened
46 criminal penalties for a second or subsequent conviction
47 under the gambling laws of the state.
48 This bill would also provide a procedure for
49 owners and operators of business establishments to be
50 held criminally liable for allowing unlawful gambling
51 activities to occur on the business premises.

90 §41-30-1. Accountability of the Alabama Lottery
91 Corporation.
92 The Legislature recognizes that the operations of a state
93 lottery are unique activities for state government and that a
94 corporate structure will best enable the lottery to be managed
95 in an entrepreneurial and business-like manner. It is the intent
96 of the Legislature that the Alabama Lottery Corporation shall be
97 accountable to the Governor, the Legislature, and the people of
98 the State of Alabama through a system of audits, reports, and
99 disclosures as required by this chapter.

229 §41-30-5. Inspection of records.
230 All records of the corporation shall be deemed public
231 records and subject to public inspection pursuant to the Article
232 3 of Chapter 12 of Title 36, unless any of the following apply:
233 (1) The record relates to or was provided by a
234 confidential source or informant and relates to lottery
235 security, applicant, vendor, or retailer qualifications or
236 conduct.

238 or of a vendor.

240 operations of the lottery or its lottery retailers or the record

242 the security of the lottery or its lottery retailers, including,

244 investigative techniques, or internal security information.

20 education employees; (iv) to award grants to hospitals to
21 assist in their operation and prevent their closure; and
22 (v) to provide tax credits to eligible residents of the
23 state as determined by the Legislature.

27 of the board.
