4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, the Administrative Office of
6 Courts may direct state appropriated funds to pay for
7 the judicial education of any justice, judge, or
8 court-supportive personnel.
9 This bill would allow the Administrative Office
10 of Courts to create a nonprofit entity to collect and
11 use nonpublic funds for judicial education.
12 This bill would allow the Administrative Office
13 of Courts to hold nonpublic funds in accounts outside
14 the State Treasury which would be audited or reviewed
15 annually.
16 This bill would allow the Administrative Office
17 of Courts and other state entities to transfer state
18 funds to the nonprofit entity for judicial education
19 purposes and would allow the nonprofit entity to be
20 housed at the Administrative Office of Courts and
21 staffed by the employees of the Administrative Office
22 of Courts.
23 This bill would be remedial and curative.
24 This bill would also make nonsubstantive,
25 technical revisions to update the existing code
26 language to current style.
