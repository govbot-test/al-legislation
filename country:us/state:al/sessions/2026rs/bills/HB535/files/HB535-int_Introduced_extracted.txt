4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, a person may petition to
6 have records of a criminal conviction expunged if
7 certain requirements are first satisfied. The court may
8 hold a hearing on the petition, and is only required to
9 hold a hearing if there is an objection from the
10 prosecution or a victim of the offense.
11 This bill would authorize a person whose
12 conviction was overturned or vacated to petition a
13 court for the expungement of the conviction.
14 This bill would authorize a person whose offense
15 was pardoned to petition a court for the expungement of
16 the offense.
17 This bill would require a hearing on the
18 expungement petition prior to the dismissal of the
19 petition.
20 Also under existing law, a filing fee of $500 is
21 required for any petition for expungement.
22 This bill would waive the filing fee and
23 associated court costs if the petition for expungement
24 is for an arrest record following a finding of not
25 guilty, or if a petition seeks the expungement of an
26 overturned or vacated conviction.
