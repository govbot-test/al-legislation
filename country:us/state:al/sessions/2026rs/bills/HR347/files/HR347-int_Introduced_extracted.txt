4 HR____ CELEBRATING SIDNEY MAE BROWN BURKS ON THE OCCASION OF
5 HER 100TH BIRTHDAY CELEBRATION.
6
7 WHEREAS, heartiest felicitations are proudly
8 presented to Sidney Mae Brown Burks on the occasion of her
9 100th Birthday Celebration; and
10 WHEREAS, Sidney Mae Brown Burks was born on April 16,
11 1926, in Talladega County, Alabama, to Robert and Sarah
12 Brown; she was raised during an era marked by significant
13 challenges and change in American history, and from an early
14 age developed the strength, resilience, and faith that would
15 define her life; and
16 WHEREAS, she spent her formative years in Alpine,
17 Alabama, and later lived in Youngstown, Ohio; Huntsville,
18 Alabama; and finally, Mobile, Alabama, where she ultimately
19 settled and built her life, family, and legacy; and
20 WHEREAS, Mrs. Burks pursued her education with
21 dedication, graduating from Talladega County Training High
22 School before matriculating to Alabama A&M University; and
23 WHEREAS, following college, she began her
24 professional career serving as a teacher for three years at
25 Dawes Union School in Mobile; she later worked as a clerk at
26 Brookley Air Force Base for several years, contributing her
27 talents during an important period in the nation's history;
28 and
Page 1
and
HR347 INTRODUCED
29 WHEREAS, an accomplished entrepreneur, Mrs. Burks,
30 alongside her husband, owned and operated several successful
31 businesses; together, they managed gas stations,
32 coin-operated laundromats, a taxi company, convenience
33 stores, and rental properties; through hard work,
34 determination, and business acumen, they created
35 opportunities not only for their family but also for their
36 community; and
37 WHEREAS, she was blessed to marry the love of her
38 life, William L. Burks, Jr., on September 2, 1947; they
39 remained devoted to one another for nearly 60 years until
40 his passing in April 2007; they built a strong and loving
41 family and raised four children, Joycelyn Burks Love, Mona
42 Burks Spencer, William L. Burks III, and Fredrick Burks;
43 Mrs. Burks' legacy continues through the lives of her eight
44 grandchildren and one great-grandchild, all of whom have
45 been shaped by her love, guidance, and example; and
46 WHEREAS, a woman of deep faith and commitment, Mrs.
47 Burks has been a devoted member of Mt. Olive Baptist Church
48 #1 since 1948; her lifelong involvement in her church
49 reflects her unwavering spiritual foundation and dedication
50 to service; and
51 WHEREAS, she is an active and respected member of
52 several distinguished organizations, including Alpha Kappa
53 Alpha Sorority, Incorporated; The Links, Incorporated; E.T.
54 Belsaw Women's Federated Club; Alabama A&M University Alumni
55 Association; the Lady Midnight Mystics; and Jack and Jill of
56 America; through these affiliations, she has contributed to
Page 2
America; through these affiliations, she has contributed to
HR347 INTRODUCED
57 civic engagement, community development, and the uplifting
58 of others; and
59 WHEREAS, as she celebrates 100 years, Sidney Mae
60 Brown Burks exemplifies perseverance, faith, leadership, and
61 service; her life stands as a testament to the power of
62 dedication to family, community, and purpose, and her
63 enduring legacy continues to inspire all who know her; now
64 therefore,
65 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE
66 LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, That Sidney Mae Brown Burks is
67 honored on the occasion of her 100th Birthday, and this
68 resolution is offered in highest tribute, along with sincere
69 best wishes for her continued well-being.
