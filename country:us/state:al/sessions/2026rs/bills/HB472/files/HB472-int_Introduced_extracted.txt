4 SYNOPSIS:
5 State ethics law requires a candidate to file a
6 statement of economic interests with the State Ethics
7 Commission for the previous year within five days after
8 the individual qualifies as a candidate, but does not
9 require an additional statement if a statement is
10 already on file with the commission. However, the law
11 does require a candidate who has already filed a
12 statement to provide proof to the commission of the
13 previous filing.
14 This bill would eliminate the requirement that a
15 candidate who has already filed a statement of economic
16 interests notify the State Ethics Commission of this
17 fact upon qualifying for candidacy.
