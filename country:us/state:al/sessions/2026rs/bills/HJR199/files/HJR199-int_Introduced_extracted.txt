5 SERVICE TO THE RESIDENTS OF MOBILE AND THE MOBILE POLICE
6 DEPARTMENT.
7
8 WHEREAS, with great pride and special pleasure, we
9 highly honor Captain Ted Johnson for his outstanding
10 service, leadership, and dedication to the residents of
11 Mobile and the Mobile Police Department; and
12 WHEREAS, a native of Mobile, Captain Johnson
13 graduated from W.P. Davidson High School before beginning a
14 distinguished career in public safety; he joined the Mobile
15 Police Department in 2006 and was first assigned to the
16 First Precinct as a patrol officer, where he quickly
17 demonstrated a commitment to professionalism and community
18 engagement; and
19 WHEREAS, over the years, he has served in several
20 important roles including motor officer in the Traffic
21 Safety Unit and supervisor in the Second Precinct, where he
22 led felony suspect apprehension efforts, general
23 investigations, and patrol operations; and
24 WHEREAS, Captain Johnson rose through the ranks and
25 was promoted to lieutenant; in that role, he served as the
26 unit commander for the Cyber Intelligence Unit, the
27 Real-Time Information Center, and the Crime Scene Unit;
28 these positions required advanced leadership, investigative
Page 1

29 expertise, and strategic coordination; and
30 WHEREAS, throughout his career, Captain Johnson has
31 been recognized with numerous honors, including the
32 Distinguished Service Award, multiple Excellent Police Duty
33 Awards, Chief's Unit Awards, Chief's Commendations,
34 Life-Saving Awards, and Officer of the Month recognitions;
35 and
36 WHEREAS, Captain Johnson is also deeply committed to
37 education and mentorship; he holds two Bachelor of Arts
38 degrees in Criminal Justice and Russian Language, as well as
39 a Master's Degree in Public Administration from the
40 University of South Alabama, where he currently serves as a
41 part-time faculty member, helping educate and inspire the
42 next generation of leaders; and
43 WHEREAS, beyond his official duties, Captain Johnson
44 has taken a proactive and compassionate approach to
45 addressing homelessness in the Tillman's Corner area in
46 Mobile County; he personally meets with individuals
47 experiencing homelessness to explain local laws and connect
48 them with resources such as identification services, housing
49 assistance, employment opportunities, veteran support, and
50 health care; and
51 WHEREAS, he has also coordinated partnerships with
52 community organizations such as One Meal Mobile, which now
53 uses the precinct as a staging area for weekly meals; as
54 well as First Step Initiative, which assists individuals in
55 obtaining identification, housing, and employment; and
56 WHEREAS, in addition to these outreach efforts,
