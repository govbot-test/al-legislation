4 SYNOPSIS:
5 This bill would establish the Bill of Rights in
6 the Classroom Act.
7 This bill would require the State Department of
8 Education to publish a standardized version of the Bill
9 of Rights for use by local boards of education.
10 This bill would require the display of the Bill
11 of Rights in public school classrooms and on the
12 website of each local board of education.
13 This bill would authorize parents to report
14 noncompliance with this act to the local superintendent
15 of education.
16 This bill would authorize declaratory and
17 injunctive relief if the local board of education fails
18 to cure the alleged violation.
19 This bill would also permit courts to award
20 reasonable attorney fees and costs to a prevailing
21 plaintiff.

37 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
38 Section 1. This act shall be known and may be cited as
39 the Bill of Rights in the Classroom Act.
40 Section 2. The Legislature finds and declares the
41 following:
42 (1) The first 10 amendments to the United States
43 Constitution, commonly known as the Bill of Rights, enshrine
44 fundamental protections of individual liberty and limit the
45 powers of government.
46 (2) Civic literacy is essential to the preservation of
47 constitutional self government.
48 (3) Displaying the Bill of Rights in public school
49 classrooms serves an educational purpose and reinforces shared
50 civic principles.
51 Section 3. For purposes of this act, the following
52 terms have the following meanings:
53 (1) BILL OF RIGHTS. The first 10 amendments to the
54 Constitution of the United States as ratified in 1791.
55 (2) CLASSROOM. Any room in which public school students
56 receive regular instructional services.

63 Section 4. (a) On or before January 1, 2027, the
64 department shall publish a standardized version of the Bill of
65 Rights for use by local boards of education, which shall
66 include the full and accurate text of the Bill of Rights.
67 (b) The text shall consist solely of the official
68 language of the Bill of Rights without additional commentary,
69 interpretation, or partisan material.
70 (c) The department shall make the standardized text
71 available in printable electronic format at no cost to local
72 boards of education.
73 Section 5. Before the 2027-2028 school year, each local
74 board of education shall post the text of the standardized
75 version of the Bill of Rights, as published by the department
76 pursuant to Section 4, in a prominent location on its official
77 website.
78 Section 6. (a) Beginning with the 2027-2028 school
79 year, each local board of education shall display the
80 standardized version of the Bill of Rights, pursuant to
81 Section 4, on a poster or framed document in all public school
82 classrooms.
