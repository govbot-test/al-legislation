4 HJR___ OBSERVING MILITARY APPRECIATION DAY AND HONORING
5 MEMBERS OF THE 99TH AIR REFUELING SQUADRON AND THE 117TH AIR
6 REFUELING WING.
7
8 WHEREAS, the Alabama Legislature proudly observes
9 Military Appreciation Day each year to recognize and honor
10 the extraordinary service and sacrifice of the men and women
11 who serve in the United States Armed Forces, including those
12 on active duty, in the National Guard and Reserve, and those
13 who have previously served the nation as veterans; and
14 WHEREAS, the people of Alabama maintain a deep and
15 enduring relationship with the United States military, as
16 the state is home to military installations including
17 Redstone Arsenal, Anniston Army Depot, Sumpter Smith Joint
18 National Guard Base, Maxwell Air Force Base, Dannelly Field,
19 Fort Rucker, the United States Coast Guard Aviation Training
20 Center, Fort McClellan Training Center, the Alabama National
21 Guard Joint Force Headquarters, and numerous additional
22 military units and organizations that contribute to our
23 national security; and
24 WHEREAS, Alabama is home to nearly 400,000 veterans,
25 13,500 active-duty service members, 26,700 National Guard
26 and Reserve members, and 50,000 military spouses and
27 dependents, all of whom contribute to the strength and
28 resilience of our communities and our nation; and
Page 1
resilience of our communities and our nation; and
HJR259 INTRODUCED
29 WHEREAS, these brave individuals serve around the
30 world defending freedom, preserving peace, and protecting
31 the security of the United States and its allies; and
32 WHEREAS, on March 12, 2026, members of the 99th Air
33 Refueling Squadron tragically lost their lives during combat
34 operations in service to the United States, demonstrating
35 the ultimate sacrifice in defense of freedom and the safety
36 of our nation; and
37 WHEREAS, the fallen airmen, Major John Alex Klinner,
38 Captain Ariana Savino, and Technical Sergeant Ashley Pruitt,
39 served their nation with honor, courage, and devotion to
40 duty, embodying the highest traditions of the United States
41 Air Force; and
42 WHEREAS, the members of the Legislature join the
43 people of this state in honoring the courage, commitment,
44 and sacrifice of these fallen service members and extend our
45 deepest condolences to their families, loved ones, fellow
46 airmen, and the entire 99th Air Refueling Squadron; and
47 WHEREAS, the members of the 99th Air Refueling
48 Squadron serve as a fully integrated force with the 117th
49 Air Refueling Wing at Sumpter Smith Joint National Guard
50 Base in Birmingham, Alabama, working hand-in-hand with
51 Alabama National Guard personnel as part of a unified
52 mission, and the "Vulcan Refuelers" are regarded as an
53 enduring part of Alabama's military family; now therefore,
54 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
55 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That the Legislature hereby
56 recognizes March 19, 2026, as Military Appreciation Day and
Page 2
recognizes March 19, 2026, as Military Appreciation Day and
HJR259 INTRODUCED
57 expresses its profound gratitude to all members of the
58 United States Armed Forces, past and present, for their
59 honorable service to our nation.
60 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the Alabama Legislature
61 solemnly honors the memory and sacrifice of Major John Alex
62 Klinner, Captain Ariana Savino, and Technical Sergeant
63 Ashley Pruitt of the 99th Air Refueling Squadron, who lost
64 their lives on March 12, 2026, recognizing their bravery and
65 devotion to duty as an enduring example of service above
66 self.
67 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the Legislature extends
68 its heartfelt sympathies and prayers to the families of
69 these fallen airmen, the members of the 99th Air Refueling
70 Squadron, the members of the 117th Air Refueling Wing, and
71 the entire United States Air Force community.
72 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That a copy of this
73 resolution be delivered to the command leadership of the
74 99th Air Refueling Squadron at Sumpter Smith Joint National
75 Guard Base, the Alabama National Guard, and the families of
76 the fallen airmen as a token of the Legislature's highest
77 respect and appreciation.
