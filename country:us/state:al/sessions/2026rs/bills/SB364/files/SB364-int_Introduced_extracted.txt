17 AN ACT

19 Relating to public education; to require instruction in
20 violence prevention, conflict resolution, and mediation before
21 graduation from high school; and to require the State
22 Department of Education to develop standards for a course or
23 program encompassing that instruction.
24 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
25 Section 1. Section 16-40-13 is added to the Code of
26 Alabama 1975, to read as follows:
27 §16-40-13
28 (a) Beginning with the 2027-2028 school year, each
Page 1
(a) Beginning with the 2027-2028 school year, each
SB364 INTRODUCED
29 public school student shall complete a course or program in
30 violence prevention, conflict resolution, and mediation before
31 graduation from high school. The instruction shall be provided
32 to students in grades six through twelve and shall include a
33 discussion of or instruction in all of the following:
34 (1) Responsible decision-making.
35 (2) The causes and effects of school violence and
36 harassment, cultural diversity, and nonviolent methods for
37 resolving conflict, including peer mediation.
38 (3) The consequences of violent behavior.
39 (4) The causes of violent reactions to conflict.
40 (5) Nonviolent conflict resolution techniques.
41 (6) The relationship between drugs, alcohol, and
42 violence.
43 (7) The implementation of voluntary peer mediation
44 programs and the integration of conflict resolution models in
45 classrooms.
46 (b) Before June 30, 2027, the State Department of
47 Education shall prepare and make available to all local boards
48 of education instructional materials, models, and curricula
49 that may be used as guidelines for development of a violence
50 prevention, conflict resolution, and mediation course or
51 program. Each local board of education shall determine the
52 appropriate curriculum for satisfying the requirements of this
53 section. The State Department of Education shall assist in
54 training teachers to provide effective instruction in violence
55 prevention, conflict resolution, and mediation.
56 (c) The State Board of Education shall adopt rules as

57 necessary to implement this section.
58 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
