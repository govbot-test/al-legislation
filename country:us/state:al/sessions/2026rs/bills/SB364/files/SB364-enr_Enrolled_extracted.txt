4 Relating to public education; to require instruction in
5 violence prevention, conflict resolution, and mediation before
6 graduation from high school; and to require the State
7 Department of Education to develop standards for a course or
8 program encompassing that instruction.
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
10 Section 1. Section 16-40-13 is added to the Code of
11 Alabama 1975, to read as follows:
12 §16-40-13
13 (a) Beginning with the 2027-2028 school year, each
14 public school student shall complete a course or program in
15 violence prevention, conflict resolution, and mediation before
16 graduation from high school. The instruction shall be provided
17 to students in grades six through twelve and shall include a
18 discussion of or instruction in all of the following:
19 (1) Responsible decision-making.
20 (2) The causes and effects of school violence and
21 harassment, cultural diversity, and nonviolent methods for
22 resolving conflict, including peer mediation.
23 (3) The consequences of violent behavior.
24 (4) The causes of violent reactions to conflict.
25 (5) Nonviolent conflict resolution techniques.
26 (6) The relationship between drugs, alcohol, and
27 violence.
28 (7) The implementation of voluntary peer mediation

29 programs and the integration of conflict resolution models in
30 classrooms.
31 (b) Before June 30, 2027, the State Department of
32 Education shall prepare and make available to all local boards
33 of education instructional materials, models, and curricula
34 that may be used as guidelines for development of a violence
35 prevention, conflict resolution, and mediation course or
36 program. Each local board of education shall determine the
37 appropriate curriculum for satisfying the requirements of this
38 section. The State Department of Education shall assist in
39 training teachers to provide effective instruction in violence
40 prevention, conflict resolution, and mediation.
41 (c) The State Board of Education shall adopt rules as
42 necessary to implement this section.
