5 A BILL
6 TO BE ENTITLED
7 AN ACT
8
9 Relating to paroles; to provide that an inmate may
10 attend his or her parole hearing remotely and provide
11 parameters for remote parole hearings.
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
13 Section 1. (a)(1) The Board of Pardons and Paroles
14 shall allow an inmate to participate in his or her parole
15 hearing via telephone, video conference, or any other similar
16 communications system that allows the inmate to interact with
17 the members of the Board of Pardons and Paroles. The board
18 shall give the victim, victim's family, and others
19 representing the victim the option to see or hear the inmate's
20 statement.
21 (2) The board shall allow any other individual
22 participating in a parole hearing, including, but not limited
23 to, the victim, the victim's family, a crime victim advocate,
24 a law enforcement officer, or a prosecutor, to participate in
25 a parole hearing via telephone, video conference, or any other
26 similar communications system.
27 (3) An inmate may waive his or her rights provided
28 under this subsection.

29 (b) Unless the victim or the victim's family chooses
30 otherwise, the board shall not allow an inmate to see, hear,
31 or otherwise interact with victims, victims' families, or
32 crime victim advocates who address the board or attend the
33 hearing.
34 (c) If the communications system fails or is in any way
35 interrupted during a hearing, the board shall suspend the
36 hearing until communication is reestablished.
37 (d) The board shall only provide access to the hearing
38 to individuals permitted to speak pursuant to rules
39 established by the board. Individuals authorized to speak may
40 speak by telephone, video conference, or any other similar
41 communications system. The board shall use video conferencing
42 when that communications system is available; otherwise, the
43 board may choose any other authorized communication method.
44 (e) The Department of Corrections shall procure a
45 telephone, video conference, or other communications system to
46 allow each inmate to participate in his or her parole hearing
47 via the approved telephone, video conference, or any other
48 similar communications system. The communications system shall
49 allow the inmate to interact with the members of the board at
50 the parole hearing. The department shall cooperate with the
51 board in the implementation of this act.
52 Section 2. This act shall become effective on March 1,
53 2027.
