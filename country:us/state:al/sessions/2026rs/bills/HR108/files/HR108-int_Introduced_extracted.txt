6 WHEREAS, it is with profound sorrow that we note the
7 death of Dr. Nathan Lee Hodges of Ozark, Alabama, on
8 February 2, 2026, at the age of 83; and while we mourn his
9 loss, we also celebrate his abundant life with thanksgiving;
10 and
11 WHEREAS, his departure leaves a void in the lives of
12 his loving family, many friends, and the communities he
13 served in Alabama, New Mexico, Texas, Kentucky, and North
14 Carolina, where he was highly regarded as an educator,
15 hospital administrator, and man of profound faith; and
16 WHEREAS, Dr. Hodges was born on December 7, 1942, in
17 Donalsonville, Georgia, to Roy Lee and Mae Fannie Chandler
18 Hodges; following his high school graduation, Nathan
19 embarked on his lifetime of learning at Gulf Coast Community
20 College and Emory University's Candler School of Theology;
21 he was called to pastor for six years before returning to
22 his education and graduating from Troy State University with
23 double Bachelor's degrees in Psychology and History and then
24 a Master's degree in Educational Administration, which he
25 earned while serving as principal at Midland City Elementary
26 School; and
27 WHEREAS, he served for many years in various roles in
28 education before attending Auburn University, earning a

30 the beginning of an extraordinary career in secondary
31 education; following his own advice to "just keep moving;
32 just keep learning," Dr. Hodges earned a degree in Hospital
33 Administration from the University of North Carolina at
34 Chapel Hill; he ultimately returned to his lifelong calling

37 WHEREAS, Nathan Hodges believed in balance and found
38 peace and tranquility as a father, grandfather, husband, and
39 friend; he earned his solo pilot's license and enjoyed the

41 for the outdoors and caring for animals of all kinds; his
