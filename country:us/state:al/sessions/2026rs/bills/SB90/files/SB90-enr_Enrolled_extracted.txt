4 Relating to seafood dealer licenses; to amend Section
5 9-12-125, as last amended by Act 2025-445, 2025 Regular
6 Session, Code of Alabama 1975, to further provide for the
7 license fee paid by certain applicants; and to provide that
8 this act is retroactive and curative.
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
10 Section 1. Section 9-12-125, as last amended by Act
11 2025-445, 2025 Regular Session, Code of Alabama 1975, is
12 amended to read as follows:
13 "§9-12-125
14 (a)(1)a. Any person who engages in the selling,
15 brokering, trading, bartering, or processing of any fresh or
16 frozen seafood, whether on a consignment basis or otherwise,
17 is a seafood dealer and shall purchase a seafood dealer
18 license for a fee of four hundred sixty-five dollars ($465)
19 for Alabama residents domiciled for a period of more than one
20 continuous year immediately preceding the date of issuance and
21 seven hundred thirty dollars ($730) for nonresidents, except
22 for residents of states which charge Alabama residents in
23 excess of seven hundred thirty dollars ($730) for the
24 activity, in which case the fee shall be the amount the other
25 state charges.
26 b. Notwithstanding paragraph a., a state resident who
27 possesses a valid license or permit issued pursuant to Section
28 9-12-82, 9-12-87, 9-12-93, 9-12-113, or 9-12-124 shall
Page 1
9-12-82, 9-12-87, 9-12-93, 9-12-113, or 9-12-124 shall
SB90 Enrolled
29 purchase a seafood dealer license for a fee of two hundred
30 sixty-five dollars ($265).
31 (2) To obtain a license, all entities other than
32 brokers shall have and present proof of a business license
33 from the location of the business, a tax identification
34 number, and the appropriate seafood processing health permit.
35 (3) A license is not required by nonresident seafood
36 dealers buying from or selling to a licensed Alabama seafood
37 dealer or a licensed commercial fishermen fisherman when
38 selling their catch to a licensed Alabama seafood dealer nor
39 is a license required by restaurants where the seafood is
40 cooked and sold for consumption on or off the restaurant's
41 premises. Restaurants shall not purchase seafood from any
42 person that is not licensed to sell seafood in this state.
43 (4) If a licensee owns or operates more than one place
44 of business, an additional license shall be purchased for each
45 separate place of business, providing the location of each. A
46 vehicle used solely for transporting seafood to or from an
47 Alabama seafood dealer is not considered a place of business.
48 Each vehicle from which seafood is sold to or purchased from
49 any person, other than an Alabama seafood dealer, is a place
50 of business and shall be licensed under this section. A
51 seafood dealer shall purchase a license for each such vehicle
52 for a fee of three hundred thirty-three dollars ($333) per
53 license and the operator of the vehicle shall have the
54 original license in his or her possession when selling or
55 buying seafood from that vehicle. Seafood dealers may purchase
56 seafood only from commercial fishermen validly licensed in
Page 2
seafood only from commercial fishermen validly licensed in
SB90 Enrolled
57 Alabama, Alabama seafood dealers, and any nonresident seller
58 who is validly licensed to sell seafood under the laws of that
59 state.
60 (5) It shall be unlawful for any person to sell,
61 broker, trade, barter, or process seafood as provided for in
62 this section without first purchasing a seafood dealer
63 license. Any person violating this section, upon conviction,
64 shall be guilty of a Class A misdemeanor, with a minimum
65 mandatory fine of one thousand dollars ($1,000) for a first
66 offense, two thousand five hundred dollars ($2,500) for a
67 second offense within three years of the date of the first
68 conviction, and five thousand dollars ($5,000) and a mandatory
69 jail sentence of 10 to 30 days for a third and any subsequent
70 offense within three years of the date of the first
71 conviction.
72 (b) The Department of Conservation and Natural
73 Resources shall annually remit two hundred dollars ($200) of
74 each seafood dealer license fee collected to the nonprofit
75 corporation Sweet Grown Alabama for the promotion of this
76 state's seafood products."
77 Section 2. This act is retroactive and curative and
78 shall apply to any license issued pursuant to Section
79 9-12-125, Code of Alabama 1975, on or after October 1, 2025.
80 Section 3. This act shall become effective immediately.
