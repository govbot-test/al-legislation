5 RETIREMENT FROM THE BIRMINGHAM POLICE DEPARTMENT.
6
7 WHEREAS, it is with profound appreciation and deepest
8 admiration that we recognize Birmingham Police Lieutenant
9 Rebeca Herrera for more than 20 years of steadfast service
10 and leadership in various roles within the Birmingham Police
11 Department; we also take this opportunity to heartily
12 congratulate her on the momentous occasion of her
13 retirement, effective January 23, 2026; and
14 WHEREAS, Lieutenant Herrera, who holds a Bachelor of
15 Science in Criminal Justice from The University of Alabama
16 at Birmingham, began training at the Birmingham Police
17 Academy on November 29, 2004; she served as a South Precinct
18 Officer for two years beginning in April 2005; and
19 WHEREAS, she then worked a variety of assignments,
20 including as a South Precinct Task Force Officer from 2007
21 until 2009 and a Vice/Narcotics Unit Detective for six years
22 beginning in 2009; and
23 WHEREAS, Lieutenant Herrera and her husband, Charles
24 Newfield, made history in October 2015, when both were
25 honored in a special ceremony at police headquarters;
26 surrounded by family, friends, and coworkers, the couple
27 officially received their sergeant's stripes together; it
28 was the first time in BPD history that a married couple was
