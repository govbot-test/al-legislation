5 DISEASE AWARENESS MONTH.
6
7 WHEREAS, more than one in seven individuals in the
8 United States, totaling approximately 37 million Americans,
9 are estimated to have chronic kidney disease (CKD), and
10 approximately 90 percent of those individuals are unaware
11 that they have CKD; and
12 WHEREAS, while the two most common causes of kidney
13 disease are hypertension and heart disease, the third
14 leading cause of kidney disease is glomerulonephritis, often
15 caused by rare, immune-mediated kidney diseases such as IgA
16 nephropathy (IgAN) and Apolipoprotein L1 (APOL1) mediated
17 kidney disease gene; and
18 WHEREAS, patients with glomerular diseases frequently
19 advance to more costly and more debilitating later stages of
20 kidney disease, including end-stage renal disease (ESRD),
21 more quickly than patients with CKD in general, with
22 estimates suggesting that rare kidney diseases make up
23 5-to-10 percent of CKD patients but account for
24 approximately 25 percent of patients receiving treatment
25 kidney failure; and
26 WHEREAS, 90 percent of patients with CKD stages 1-3
27 are undiagnosed, when diagnosed is often diagnosed in late
28 stages of the disease when irreversible damage to the
Page 1
stages of the disease when irreversible damage to the

29 kidneys has already occurred; and
30 WHEREAS, it is imperative to improve diagnosis and
31 treatment of CKD through community-based programs that
32 address awareness, diagnosis, and treatment of chronic
33 kidney disease; now therefore,
34 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE
35 LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, That the members of this body
36 recognize March 2026 as Chronic Kidney Disease Awareness
37 Month in Alabama in order to raise awareness of the need for
38 research, screening programs, and access to care for
39 individuals who suffer from chronic kidney disease.
