4 SYNOPSIS:
5 This bill would establish the Automotive Supply
6 Chain Resilience and Expansion Act that creates a
7 strategic framework to support the automotive industry
8 in response to federal tariff disruptions, by offering
9 targeted relief, supply chain incentives, and export
10 assistance to protect jobs and maintain the sector's
11 global competitiveness.

29 Section 2. It is the intent of the Legislature to
30 provide strategic support to the automotive manufacturing
31 sector in response to disruptions caused by federal tariffs
32 and global trade instability.
33 Section 3. For the purpose of this act, the following
34 terms and phrases have the following meanings:
35 (1) DEPARTMENT. The Alabama Department of Commerce.
36 (2) ELIGIBLE ENTITY. Any Alabama manufacturer or
37 supplier operating in the state.
38 (3) TARIFF-RELATED DISRUPTION. Increased costs, reduced
39 revenues, or supply interruptions resulting directly or
40 indirectly from federal tariffs or foreign retaliatory
41 measures.
42 Section 4. (a) The Automotive Tariff Relief Grant
43 Program and the Automotive Tariff Relief Grant Fund are
44 established and shall be administered by the department.
45 (b) Grant funds may be used for:
46 (1) Cost mitigation;
47 (2) Inventory diversification;
48 (3) Workforce retention; or
49 (4) Capital improvement.
50 (c) Grants shall be awarded to eligible entities that
51 demonstrate hardship due to tariff-related disruptions.
52 (d) The aggregate amount of grants awarded pursuant to
53 this section shall not exceed ten million dollars
54 ($10,000,000) in a calendar year.
55 (e) The department shall establish rules for
56 application procedures and documentation requirements.

57 Section 5. (a) A refundable income tax credit, known as
58 the Supply Chain Relocation and Expansion Tax Credit, may be
59 claimed by eligible entities that relocate critical supply
60 operations, expand domestic sourcing within the state, or
61 invest in technology or equipment that mitigates reliance on
62 tariff-affected imports.
63 (b) The amount of any income tax credit shall not
64 exceed 30 percent of qualifying investment expenditures.
65 (c) The income tax credit authorized by this act is
66 limited to an aggregate amount for all taxpayers of ten
67 million dollars ($10,000,000) annually.
68 (d) The income tax credit is nontransferable and may be
69 carried forward for up to five years.
70 (e)(1) The income tax credit allowed under this section
71 shall be effective beginning January 1, 2027, for the 2027 tax
72 year, and shall continue through the 2029 tax year, except for
73 carry-forward as provided in subsection (d).
74 (2) Notwithstanding the other provisions of this
75 subsection, a business shall not claim this income tax credit
76 for more than three tax years.
77 (f) The Alabama Department of Revenue may adopt rules
78 for the implementation and administration of this section.
