3 ESTABLISHING THE ALABAMA SCHOOLS S.T.E.M. PROGRAMS STUDY
4 COMMISSION.
5
6 WHEREAS, the education of youth is one of the
7 fundamental powers and responsibilities reserved to the
8 states; and
9 WHEREAS, Alabama has found much success in
10 subject-specific state programs that amplify the ability of
11 the state's public and private schools to educate our youth
12 in important subject matters; and
13 WHEREAS, the expanding and evolving economy has
14 continued to increase the importance of the subjects of
15 science, technology, engineering, and mathematics, known
16 together as S.T.E.M. subjects; and
17 WHEREAS, it is incumbent upon Alabama to ensure its
18 youth are prepared with the necessary knowledge and skills
19 to contribute to our future, including, but not limited to,
20 the ability to become productive members of the workforce of
21 the future; and
22 WHEREAS, before any policies or programs are
23 established or revised, it is prudent to conduct an
24 inventory or survey of existing state programs in public and
25 private schools which assist with the teaching of S.T.E.M.
26 subjects; now therefore,
27 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
28 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That there is created the Alabama
