4 HJR___ ESTABLISHING THE ALABAMA SCHOOLS S.T.E.M. PROGRAMS
5 STUDY COMMISSION.
6
7 WHEREAS, the education of youth is one of the
8 fundamental powers and responsibilities reserved to the
9 states; and
10 WHEREAS, Alabama has found much success in
11 subject-specific state programs that amplify the ability of
12 the state's public and private schools to educate our youth
13 in important subject matters; and
14 WHEREAS, the expanding and evolving economy has
15 continued to increase the importance of the subjects of
16 science, technology, engineering, and mathematics, known
17 together as S.T.E.M. subjects; and
18 WHEREAS, it is incumbent upon Alabama to ensure its
19 youth are prepared with the necessary knowledge and skills
20 to contribute to our future, including, but not limited to,
21 the ability to become productive members of the workforce of
22 the future; and
23 WHEREAS, before any policies or programs are
24 established or revised, it is prudent to conduct an
25 inventory or survey of existing state programs in public and
26 private schools which assist with the teaching of S.T.E.M.
27 subjects; now therefore,
Page 1
subjects; now therefore,
HJR275 INTRODUCED
28 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
29 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That there is created the Alabama
30 Schools S.T.E.M. Programs Study Commission to analyze
31 existing state educational programs and policies relating to
32 S.T.E.M. subjects and to recommend changes in program
33 structures, authorizations, and financial support for those
34 programs.
35 (a) The membership of the study commission shall
36 consist of all of the following:
37 (1) The Speaker of the House of Representatives, or
38 his or her designee.
39 (2) The President Pro Tempore of the Senate, or his
40 or her designee.
41 (3) The Speaker Pro Tempore of the House of
42 Representatives, or his or her designee.
43 (4) The Majority Leader of the House of
44 Representatives, or his or her designee.
45 (5) The Majority Leader of the Senate, or his or her
46 designee.
47 (6) The Minority Leader of the House of
48 Representatives, or his or her designee.
49 (7) The Minority Leader of the Senate, or his or her
50 designee.
51 (8) The Chair of the House of Representatives
52 Education Policy Committee, or his or her designee.
53 (9) The Ranking Minority Member of the House of
54 Representatives Education Policy Committee, or his or her
55 designee.
Page 2
designee.
HJR275 INTRODUCED
56 (10) The Chair of the House of Representatives Ways
57 and Means Education Trust Fund Committee, or his or her
58 designee.
59 (11) The Ranking Minority Member of the House of
60 Representatives Ways and Means Education Trust Fund
61 Committee, or his or her designee.
62 (12) The Chair of the Senate Education Policy
63 Committee, or his or her designee.
64 (13) The Ranking Minority Member of the Senate
65 Education Policy Committee, or his or her designee.
66 (14) The Chair of the Senate Finance and Taxation
67 Education Committee, or his or her designee.
68 (15) The Ranking Minority Member of the Senate
69 Finance and Taxation Education Committee, or his or her
70 designee.
71 (b) The appointing authorities shall coordinate their
72 appointments to assure the study commission membership is
73 inclusive and reflects the racial, gender, geographic,
74 urban, rural, and economic diversity of the state.
75 (c) The Chairs of the House of Representatives
76 Education Policy Committee and the Senate Education Policy
77 Committee, or their respective designees, shall serve as
78 co-chairs of the study commission. The first meeting of the
79 study commission shall be held at the call of the co-chairs
80 not later than 30 days after this resolution's passage. The
81 study commission may then meet as necessary to conduct
82 business.
Page 3
business.
HJR275 INTRODUCED
83 (d) The Legislative Services Agency, the Examiners of
84 Public Accounts, the Clerk of the House of Representatives,
85 and the Secretary of the Senate shall provide administrative
86 and other assistance for the work of the study commission as
87 necessary.
88 (e)(1) The Legislative members of the study
89 commission shall be entitled to their legislative
90 compensation, per diem, and travel expenses for each day
91 they attend a meeting of the task force pursuant to Section
92 49 of the Constitution of Alabama of 2022.
93 (2) The nonlegislative members of the study
94 commission shall serve without compensation but may be
95 reimbursed for necessary expenses in attending meetings of
96 the study commission pursuant to the policies of their
97 respective appointing authority.
98 (f) The study commission shall prepare a report of
99 its findings, conclusions, and recommendations to the
100 Legislature not later than November 1, 2026, at which point
101 the study commission shall stand dissolved and discharged of
102 any future duties and liabilities.
103 (g) Pursuant to Section 36-14-17.1, Code of Alabama
104 1975, the study commission shall provide a notice of all
105 meetings, the name of each member serving on the study
106 commission, and a copy of the final report and any other
107 documents produced by the study commission throughout its
108 duration to the Secretary of State.
109 (h) Each appointing authority named herein shall be
110 provided a copy of this resolution upon its passage.
