4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, the Alabama Department of
6 Environmental Management and the Alabama Department of
7 Conservation and Natural Resources jointly administer
8 the Alabama Coastal Area Management Program under
9 delegated federal authority to ensure consistency in
10 balancing conservation, economic, and other interests
11 with regard to coastal area projects.
12 This bill would require a person who annually
13 dredges over 1 million cubic yards of material in a
14 coastal area to cause at least 70 percent of that
15 material to be beneficially used.
16 This bill would also require the Alabama
17 Department of Environmental Management and the Alabama
18 Department of Conservation and Natural Resources to
19 adopt rules to administer this act, including revising
20 the Alabama Coastal Area Management Program as
21 necessary.

32 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
33 Section 1. (a) For purposes of this section, the
34 following terms have the following meanings:
35 (1) BENEFICIALLY USED. The productive and positive use
36 of dredged material, including fish and wildlife habitat
37 development, human recreation, and industrial and commercial
38 uses. The term does not include the deposition of dredged
39 material into public waters unless that deposition is part of
40 a shoreline restoration or marsh creation project.
41 (2) LIVING SHORELINE. The term as defined in Section
42 9-7-13.1, Code of Alabama 1975.
43 (3) MARSH CREATION. The deposition of dredged material
44 or natural or artificial material into public waters to
45 establish a wetland, island, or marsh habitat on that site.
46 (4) PUBLIC WATERS. The term as defined in Section
47 9-11-80, Code of Alabama 1975.
48 (5) SHORELINE RESTORATION. The deposition of dredged
49 material or natural or artificial material directly on or
50 adjacent to an existing shoreline to restore or preserve the
51 shoreline and the deposition of such material south of Dauphin
52 Island or south of the Fort Morgan Peninsula for the
53 restoration of Dauphin Island or Sand Island. This term
54 includes a living shoreline project.
55 (b) This section shall apply only in coastal areas.
56 (c) Any person that dredges more than one million cubic
