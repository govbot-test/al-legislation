4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, the Alabama State Board of
6 Veterinary Medical Examiners is authorized to regulate
7 the practice of veterinary medicine in this state.
8 This bill would prohibit a veterinarian from
9 prescribing or dispensing any medication without a
10 valid veterinarian-client-patient relationship.
11 This bill would provide a maximum amount of time
12 within which a veterinarian may refill a prescription.
13 This bill would provide that a
14 veterinarian-client-patient relationship created with
15 one veterinarian exists among all other veterinarians
16 practicing at the same location.
17 This bill would provide that a
18 veterinarian-client-patient relationship must be
19 reestablished annually to remain valid.
20 This bill would further define what
21 circumstances create a new veterinarian-client-patient
22 relationship and would define certain terms, including
23 "client" and "patient."
24 This bill would also make nonsubstantive,
25 technical revisions to update the existing code
26 language to current style.

Page 12
(a) A VCPR remains with the licensed veterinarian who
337 established the initial VCPR and remains valid with other
338 licensed veterinarians within the practice premises where the
339 written or electronic medical records of the patient are kept.
340 (b)(1) A licensed veterinarian may not prescribe or
341 dispense drugs without a VCPR.
342 (2) A VCPR may not be established for the sole purpose
343 of drug sales.
344 (3) Telemedicine may not be used to establish a VCPR.
345 (c) With a VCPR, a prescription may be refilled for not
346 more than one year from the initial prescription date unless
347 medically prohibited or restricted or prohibited by state or
348 federal law, rule, or regulation.
349 (d) A new VCPR shall be reestablished annually by the
350 date that the previous VCPR was established. If not
351 reestablished annually, a VCPR is not valid.
352 (f)(1) A licensed veterinarian may communicate by means
353 of teletriage with an individual if:
354 a. An emergency occurs outside of a valid VCPR;
355 b. The individual has not had a valid VCPR established
356 in the previous 12 months; or
357 c. The individual contacting the licensed veterinarian
358 is not a client.
359 (2) In teletriage, a diagnosis or treatment may not be
360 rendered. The individual and licensed veterinarian may arrange
361 an in-person visit or the licensed veterinarian may refer the
362 patient and individual to another licensed veterinarian for
363 assessment and treatment.
