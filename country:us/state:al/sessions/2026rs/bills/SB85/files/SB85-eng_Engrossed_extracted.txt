309 Section 2. Section 34-29-77.1 is added to the Code of
310 Alabama 1975, to read as follows:
311 §34-29-77.1
312 (a) A VCPR remains with the licensed veterinarian who
313 established the initial VCPR and remains valid with other
314 licensed veterinarians within the practice premises where the
315 written or electronic medical records of the patient are kept.
316 (b)(1) A licensed veterinarian may not prescribe or
317 dispense drugs without a VCPR.
318 (2) A VCPR may not be established for the sole purpose
319 of drug sales.
320 (3) Telemedicine may not be used to establish a VCPR.
321 (c) With a VCPR, a prescription may be refilled for not
322 more than one year from the initial prescription date unless
323 medically prohibited or restricted or prohibited by state or
324 federal law, rule, or regulation.
325 (d) A new VCPR shall be reestablished annually by the
326 date that the previous VCPR was established. If not
327 reestablished annually, a VCPR is not valid.
328 (f)(1) A licensed veterinarian may communicate by means
329 of teletriage with an individual if:
330 a. An emergency occurs outside of a valid VCPR;
331 b. The individual has not had a valid VCPR established
332 in the previous 12 months; or
333 c. The individual contacting the licensed veterinarian
334 is not a client.
335 (2) In teletriage, a diagnosis or treatment may not be
336 rendered. The individual and licensed veterinarian may arrange
Page 12

rendered. The individual and licensed veterinarian may arrange

337 an in-person visit or the licensed veterinarian may refer the
338 patient and individual to another licensed veterinarian for
339 assessment and treatment.
340 (g) If an emergency occurs within a valid VCPR and the
341 veterinarian who established the original VCPR is not
342 available to examine the patient, the veterinarian may
343 communicate the changes in protocol to the client by means of
344 telemedicine and provide consultation, issue recommendations,
345 or refer the client to another licensed veterinarian.
