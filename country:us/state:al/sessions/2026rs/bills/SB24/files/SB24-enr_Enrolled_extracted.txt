4 Relating to voting; to require the Board of Pardons and
5 Paroles to post procedures and an application form for
6 requesting restoration of voting rights for those individuals
7 who have lost their voting rights by reason of conviction; and
8 to require the Board of Pardons and Paroles to post on its
9 website the Alabama Institutional Serial (AIS) number of
10 parolees and probationers whose voting rights have been
11 restored.
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
13 Section 1. (a) No later than March 1, 2027, the Board
14 of Pardons and Paroles shall develop and make available on the
15 agency's website, in an easily accessible manner, an
16 explanation of how an individual who has lost his or her right
17 to vote by reason of conviction of a crime may have their
18 right to vote restored under Section 15-22-36.1 if the
19 individual meets the criteria set forth in that section. The
20 website shall include a form, along with instructions, which
21 an individual may electronically submit, which shall serve as
22 an application for a Certificate of Eligibility to Register to
23 Vote. The form shall automatically be submitted to the Board
24 of Pardons and Paroles.
25 (b) Upon receipt of an application under subsection
26 (a), the board shall forthwith review the application and make
27 a determination of eligibility in accordance with Section
28 15-22-36.1 and, if appropriate, issue to the applicant a
Page 1
15-22-36.1 and, if appropriate, issue to the applicant a
SB24 Enrolled
29 Certificate of Eligibility to Register to Vote in accordance
30 with Section 15-22-36.1(e).
31 (c) The Board of Pardons and Paroles shall publish on
32 the board's website the Alabama Institutional Serial (AIS)
33 number of each individual who is a parolee or probationer
34 under the jurisdiction of the board whose right to vote has
35 been restored pursuant to Section 15-22-36.1 and if an
36 applicant under subsection (a) included his or her residence
37 on the application form, the county in which the individual
38 resides.
39 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
40 1, 2026.
