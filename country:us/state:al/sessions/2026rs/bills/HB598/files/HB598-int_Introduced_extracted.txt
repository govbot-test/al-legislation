4 SYNOPSIS:
5 This bill creates the Rural Maternal and Infant
6 Health Equity Act to improve birth outcomes in rural
7 communities by expanding access to prenatal, delivery,
8 and postpartum care through midwifery and doula
9 support, rural obstetric workforce development, and
10 culturally responsive community-based services.
11 This bill will also create the Rural Maternal
12 and Infant Health Equity Program Fund.
13
14
15 A BILL
16 TO BE ENTITLED
17 AN ACT
18
19 Relating to maternal and infant health disparities in
20 rural areas; to establish a grant program to support midwife
21 and doula services; to require Medicaid and all insurers to
22 cover midwife and doula services; to establish a grant program
23 to support rural birth centers; and to create the Rural
24 Maternal and Infant Health Equity Program Fund.
25 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
26 Section 1. This act shall be known as the Rural
27 Maternal and Infant Health Equity Act.
28 Section 2. As used in this act, unless otherwise

Section 2:
Section 2. As used in this act, unless otherwise
HB598 INTRODUCED
29 specified or unless the context requires otherwise, the
30 following terms will have the following meanings:
31 (1) DEPARTMENT. The Alabama Department of Public
32 Health.
33 (2) DOULA. A trained, nonclinical birth support
34 professional who provides physical, emotional, and
35 informational assistance.
36 (3) MIDWIFE. A certified nurse-midwife, certified
37 professional midwife, or other licensed midwifery provider
38 licensed by the State Board of Midwifery.
39 (4) RURAL AREA. Any area within this state not included
40 within the boundaries of any incorporated city or town having
41 a population in excess of 25,000 inhabitants, according to the
42 last federal decennial census.
43 Section 3. (a) The department shall support rural
44 access to midwifery and doula services by offering grants to
45 integrate maternity care providers into hospitals and clinics.
46 (b) The State Board of Midwifery shall license and
47 regulate community-based maternity care providers.
48 (c) Cultural competency and evidence-based training
49 shall be required.
50 Section 4. (a) Subject to prior approval or a waiver
51 from the Centers of Medicare and Medicaid Services, the
52 Alabama Medicaid Agency shall provide coverage for midwife and
53 doula services.
54 (b) Every insurer authorized to offer, sell, or deliver
55 insurance in this state shall include coverage for midwife and
56 doula services.

57 Section 5. (a) The department shall establish the
58 Obstetric Workforce Training and Incentive Program to support:
59 (1) Obstetrics and gynecology residencies or rural
60 clinical rotations;
61 (2) Recruitment incentives for obstetric providers
62 locating in rural areas; and
63 (3) Loan repayment for at least two years of rural
64 maternity care service.
65 (b) Priority shall be given to providers trained in
66 team-based and culturally responsive care.
67 Section 6. (a) The department shall fund locally led
68 maternal health partnerships to:
69 (1) Deliver prenatal home visits by nurses or
70 midwives;
71 (2) Complete mobile perinatal clinics; and
72 (3) Complete group prenatal care and peer-led education
73 for at-risk populations.
74 (b) Grantees must demonstrate engagement of target
75 communities in program design.
76 Section 7. (a) The department shall offer capital
77 grants to:
78 (1) Establish new rural birth centers;
79 (2) Expand or modernize obstetric units in rural
80 hospitals; and
81 (3) Support facilities offering continuous labor
82 support and postpartum care.
83 (b) Projects must meet safety and licensure standards.

85 Infant Health Equity Program Fund in the State Treasury.
86 (b) The fund is subject to appropriations by the

89 act. The department may not spend appropriations for the

92 in accordance with Sections 41-4-80 through 41-4-69 and
