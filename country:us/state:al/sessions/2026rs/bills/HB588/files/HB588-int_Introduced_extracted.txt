62 §34-44-1 Short title.
63 This chapter shall be known and may be cited as the
64 Community Health Worker Certification Act.
65 §34-44-2 Definitions.
66 For the purposes of this chapter, the following terms
67 have the following meanings:
68 (1) BOARD. The Community Health Worker Certification
69 Board.
70 (2) CERTIFICATION. Recognition by the board that an
71 individual has met eligibility requirements and demonstrated
72 competency standards established by this chapter and rules
73 adopted pursuant to this chapter.
74 (3) CERTIFIED COMMUNITY HEALTH WORKER. A community
75 health worker who holds certification issued under this
76 chapter.
77 (4) COMMUNITY HEALTH WORKER. A front line public health
78 worker who serves as a liaison, link, and intermediary between
79 health and social services and the community and facilitates
80 access to services and service delivery. A community health
81 worker may also provide outreach, community education,
82 informal counseling, social support, and advocacy.

169 §34-44-4 Core competency skills framework.
170 (a) The board shall adopt the following core competency
171 skills as the minimum competency framework:
172 (1) Communication.
173 (2) Interpersonal skills and relationship building
174 skills.
175 (3) Service coordination and navigation.
176 (4) Capacity building.
177 (5) Advocacy.
178 (6) Presentation and facilitation.
179 (7) Organizational and cultural competency.
180 (8) Public health knowledge.
181 (9) Understanding of health systems and basic diseases.
182 (10) Behavioral health issues and knowledge.
183 (11) Field experience.
184 (b) In adopting, interpreting, and implementing the
185 core competency skills framework, the board shall align
186 competency standards, training program requirements, approved
187 pathways, and assessment criteria with the nationally
188 recognized community health worker competency standards and
189 shall incorporate updates to those standards as they are
190 revised or replaced, through board policy and administrative
191 rule.
192 (c) Nothing in this chapter or the core competency
193 skills framework shall be construed to authorize a community
194 health worker to provide direct care or treatment to any
195 individual or to perform any act or service for which a
196 license issued by a professional licensing board is required.

197 §34-44-5 Training.
198 (a) Community health workers shall be provided with
199 academic-based and community-based training opportunities that
200 lead to the mastery of the community health worker core
201 competency skills framework. Training may be delivered by
202 educational institutions including, but not limited to, public
203 K-12 career technical schools and two-year or four-year
204 institutions of higher education, or through other
205 competency-based, academic-based, community-based
206 organizations, employers, providers, and instructors approved
207 by the board.
208 (b) The board shall establish minimum requirements for
209 community health worker training programs that mirror the
210 nationally adopted curriculum adopted by the Alabama Community
211 Health Worker Coalition and require demonstration of
212 competency through instruction, applied learning, and
213 supervised practice, including field experience.
214 (c) At a minimum, approved training programs shall
215 incorporate the community health worker core competencies
216 national standards as recommended by the Community Health
217 Worker Core Consensus (C3) Project, the National Association
218 of Community Health Workers (NACHW), the Foundations for
219 Community Health Workers, 3rd edition, and other nationally
220 recognized community health workers competency frameworks, and
221 any updated versions or modules published after October 1,
222 2026, as determined by the board.
223 (d) The board shall collaborate with the State
224 Department of Education, the Alabama Community College System,
