10 A BILL
11 TO BE ENTITLED
12 AN ACT
13
14 Relating to state symbols; to amend Section 1-2-16,
15 Code of Alabama 1975; to designate "Stars Fell on Alabama" as
16 the official state song.
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
18 Section 1. Section 1-2-16 of the Code of Alabama 1975,
19 is amended to read as follows:
20 "§1-2-16
21 The poem "Alabama," a gift to the people of the state
22 from Julia S. Tutwiler, together with the original music
23 written for such poem by Edna Gockel-Gussen, which music has
24 been approved and adopted by the Alabama Federation of Music
25 Clubs, is hereby adopted as the state song of Alabama."
26 (a) The Legislature finds and declares both of the
27 following:
28 (1) "Stars Fell on Alabama," with music by Frank
Page 1
(1) "Stars Fell on Alabama," with music by Frank
HB661 INTRODUCED
29 Perkins and lyrics by Mitchell Parish, has long been
30 associated with the State of Alabama, reflecting a unique
31 chapter in the state's cultural history and resonating broadly
32 with Alabamians and visitors alike.
33 (2) Elevating "Stars Fell on Alabama" to the status of
34 the official state song will honor Alabama's musical heritage
35 and strengthen the state's identity across civic, educational,
36 and cultural settings.
37 (b) "Stars Fell on Alabama" is hereby designated and
38 named the official state song of Alabama.
39 (c) Any prior statutory designation of a different
40 official state song is hereby superseded and replaced by the
41 designation in subsection (b).
42 (d) Nothing in this section shall be construed to
43 authorize the reproduction, distribution, or public
44 performance of the musical composition or lyrics of "Stars
45 Fell on Alabama" in violation of applicable intellectual
46 property laws.
47 (e) This section does not limit counties,
48 municipalities, or state agencies from recognizing additional
49 songs or musical works for ceremonial, educational, or tourism
50 purposes consistent with state law."
51 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
52 1, 2026.
