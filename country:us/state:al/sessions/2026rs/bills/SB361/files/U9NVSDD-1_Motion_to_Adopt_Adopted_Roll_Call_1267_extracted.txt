8 Relating to dogs; to provide methods for tethering and
9 confining dogs; to provide certain standards of care for dogs
10 kept outside; to exempt certain persons and activities; to
11 provide criminal penalties for violations; and to authorize
12 agents of political subdivisions to inspect violations.
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
14 Section 1. For purposes of this act, the following
15 terms have the following meanings:
16 (1) ADEQUATE FOOD. Food that is offered in suitable
17 intervals and in sufficient amounts to prevent starvation or
18 significant risk to a dog's health from lack of food.
19 (2) ADEQUATE WATER. Potable water that is offered in
20 suitable intervals and in sufficient amounts to prevent
21 dehydration or significant risk to a dog's health from lack of
22 water.
23 (3) PROPERLY FITTED. When naturally worn by a dog, a
24 collar or harness that:
25 a. Is appropriately sized for the dog based on the
26 dog's measurements and body weight; and
27 b. Does not choke the dog or impede the dog's normal
28 breathing or swallowing.

33 Section 2. (a)(1) A person may not tether a dog to a
34 stationary object, including a tree, pole, stake, or
35 structure, unless the dog is tethered via a trolley system in
36 accordance with all of the following:
37 a. The tether is not unreasonably heavy in relation to
38 the weight of the dog.
39 b. The tether does not allow the dog to leave the
40 property of the owner or person responsible for the dog.
41 c. The tether is connected to only one dog.
42 d. The tether is attached to a properly fitted collar
43 or harness worn by the dog.
44 e. The tether has a swivel on both ends to minimize
45 tangling.
46 f. The tether allows the dog continuous access at all
47 times to adequate food, adequate water, and shelter as
48 described in Section 3.
49 g. The tether does not extend over an object or edge
50 that could result in injury or strangulation of the dog.
51 h. The tether is situated in a manner to prevent
52 entanglement with other objects or animals.
53 (2) A logging chain, choke collar, or pinch collar may
54 not be used to tether a dog.

56 movement by confining the dog within an enclosure unless in

90 Section 3. In addition to any other requirement by law,
91 a person leaving or keeping a dog tethered or confined outside
92 shall provide the dog with access to shelter that complies
93 with all of the following:

98 (2) Has a roof, walls, and a solid floor.
99 (3) Has space for the dog to stand up, turn around, lie
100 down in a normal position, and extend its limbs.
101 (4) Is constructed or manufactured of durable materials
102 and provides adequate ventilation.
103 (5) Is free from accumulated waste, standing water, and
104 debris.
105 (6) Allows the dog unlimited access to the shelter.
