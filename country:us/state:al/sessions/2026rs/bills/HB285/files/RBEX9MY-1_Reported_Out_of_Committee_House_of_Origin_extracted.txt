7 AN ACT

9 Relating to economic and workforce development; to
10 establish the Coal-Impacted Communities Economic and Workforce
11 Development Grant Program to assist local development
12 organizations with economic and workforce development
13 initiatives in coal-impacted communities; to provide for the
14 purposes for which the grant funds may be used; to establish
15 the Coal-Impacted Communities Economic and Workforce
16 Development Grant Program Advisory Committee to review
17 applications and make recommendations; to establish the
18 Renewing Coal-Impacted Communities Act Fund in the State
19 Treasury; to provide for the distribution of revenues received
20 from rent and royalties derived from federal coal lease sales
21 in the state beginning after January 1, 2027; and to provide
22 an effective date.
23 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
24 Section 1. This act shall be known and may be cited as
25 the Renewing Coal-Impacted Communities Act.
26 Section 2. The Legislature finds and declares all the
27 following:
28 (1) The Federal Lands Program regulates coal mining and

29 reclamation operations on federal lands pursuant to the
30 Surface Mining Control and Reclamation Act (SMCRA) and the
31 Mineral Leasing Act of 1920 (MLA).
32 (2) The United States Department of the Interior Office
33 of Natural Resources Revenue (ONRR) collects royalties and
34 other revenues from coal production on federal lands and pays
35 a portion of this revenue to the state where the mineral was
36 extracted.
37 (3) The primary intent of the royalties is to
38 compensate states that host federal mineral extraction
39 activities, including the communities most impacted by mineral
40 extraction. Costs to these communities, include, but are not
41 limited to, infrastructure, increased demand on public
42 services, and workforce-related costs.
43 (4) The McDuffie Coal Terminal at the Port of Mobile
44 serves as the primary export terminal for coal extracted from
45 federal lands in Alabama, handling the majority of coal
46 shipped from mining operations in coal-impacted communities.
47 The movement of coal through the McDuffie Coal Terminal at the
48 Port of Mobile generates significant economic activity,
49 creates jobs in transportation and logistics sectors, and
50 contributes substantial tax revenue to state and local
51 governments. Infrastructure improvements and maintenance at
52 the Port of Mobile are essential to maintaining the
53 competitiveness of Alabama's coal industry, ensuring efficient
54 market access for coal production, and supporting the broader
55 economic ecosystem dependent on coal mining activities.
56 (5) It is the intent of the Legislature, by the passage

57 of this act, to require that all federal funds made available
58 to the state through 30 U.S.C. § 191(a), be expended within
59 coal-impacted communities, the Port of Mobile, and the State
60 General Fund.
61 Section 3. The following words and phrases, whenever
62 used in this act, have the following meanings:
63 (1) COAL-IMPACTED COMMUNITIES. Areas or jurisdictions
64 of the state that meet one or more of the following criteria:
65 a. Areas where coal is currently being mined on federal
66 lands.
67 b. Areas where a significant population of the
68 workforce is engaged in the mining of coal on federal lands.
69 c. Areas that have experienced substantial economic
70 impact due to the decline or cessation of coal mining
71 operations on federal lands.
72 d. Jurisdictions where coal mining on federal lands has
73 historically been a major source of employment or tax revenue.
74 e. For purposes of this definition, Fayette County,
75 Jefferson County, Tuscaloosa County, and Walker County are
76 designated as coal-impacted communities.
77 (2) DEPARTMENT. The Alabama Department of Workforce.
78 (3) FUND. The fund created in Section 6 of this act.
79 (4) LOCAL DEVELOPMENT ORGANIZATION. Any organization
80 that is determined by the board to meet both of the following
81 criteria:
82 a. The organization is an Alabama entity not operating
83 for profit, including, but not limited to, a municipality,
84 county, industrial development board, industrial development

98 Section 4. (a) The Coal-Impacted Communities Economic
99 and Workforce Development Grant Program is established for the
100 purpose of awarding grants to eligible local development
101 organizations for regional economic and workforce development
102 initiatives in coal-impacted communities in the state.
103 (b) The Secretary of the Department of Workforce shall
104 implement and administer the grant program.
105 (c) Grant funds awarded under this program may be used
106 to support coal-impacted communities through:
107 (1) Workforce development and training programs;
108 (2) Quality of place initiatives that enhance community
109 livability and economic competitiveness;
110 (3) Public works, infrastructure, and public services;
111 (4) Capital improvement that support community
112 resilience and economic development; and

115 Section 5. (a) To ensure that coal-impacted communities
116 have meaningful input into the expenditure of grant funds, a
117 Coal-Impacted Communities Economic and Workforce Development
118 Grant Program Advisory Committee is established to review
119 applications and make recommendations to the secretary. Each
120 advisory committee member shall be appointed to a four-year
121 term and shall serve at the pleasure of their respective
122 appointing authority. The advisory committee shall meet at
123 least annually. Additional meetings may be called at the
124 discretion of the secretary.
125 (b) The committee shall consist of the following
126 members:
127 (1) One member appointed by the Governor from a
128 coal-impacted community.
129 (2) One member appointed by the Lieutenant Governor
130 from a coal-impacted community.
131 (3) One member appointed by the President Pro Tempore
132 of the Senate from a coal-impacted community.
133 (4) One member appointed by the Speaker of the House of
134 Representatives from a coal-impacted community.
135 (5) One member appointed by the Fayette County
136 Legislative Delegation.
137 (6) One member appointed by the Jefferson County
138 Legislative Delegation.
139 (7) One member appointed by the Tuscaloosa County

143 (9) The President of the Alabama Mining Association.
144 (c) The appointing authorities shall coordinate their
145 appointments to assure the advisory board membership is
146 inclusive and reflects the racial, gender, geographic, urban,
147 rural, and economic diversity of the state.
148 Section 6. (a) The Renewing Coal-Impacted Communities
149 Act Fund is created within the State Treasury to provide grant
150 funds to local development organizations for the purpose of
151 improving economic and workforce development in coal-impacted
152 communities. The fund shall be administered by the department
153 and shall be comprised of revenues received from rent and
154 royalties derived from federal coal lease sales in the state
155 beginning after January 1, 2027, or allocated by the
156 Legislature from other funds for the purposes of this act.
157 Amounts deposited into the fund shall be budgeted and allotted
158 in accordance with Sections 41-4-80 through 41-4-96 and
159 Sections 41-19-1 through 41-19-12, Code of Alabama 1975. All
160 funds received into the fund shall remain in the fund and not
161 revert or be expended for any other purposes other than those
162 set out in this act.
163 (b) The department may retain up to 10 percent of the
164 total funds allocated for the Coal-Impacted Communities
165 Economic and Workforce Development Grant Program for actual
166 expenses relating to administering the program and conducting
167 financial audits. Members of the board shall serve without
168 compensation but may be reimbursed for expenses incurred for

169 the performance of official board duties, including expenses
170 incurred in attending meetings or otherwise conducting board
171 business. Reimbursement shall be made in accordance with the
172 per diem and travel expense rates authorized for state
173 officers and employees under applicable state law and any
174 rules or policies adopted pursuant thereto.
175 Section 7. The Secretary of the Department of Workforce
176 shall adopt rules as necessary to implement and administer the
177 provisions of this act.
178 Section 8. Notwithstanding any other law to the
179 contrary, beginning January 1, 2027 and ending December 31,
180 2030, the revenues derived from the payment of rent and
181 royalties of federal coal lease sales occurring in the state
182 after January 1, 2027 shall be distributed annually as
183 follows:
184 (1) The first one million dollars ($1,000,000) shall be
185 deposited in the State General Fund.
186 (2) The next five-hundred thousand dollars ($500,000)
187 shall be distributed to the Alabama State Port Authority for
188 the McDuffie Coal Terminal at the Port of Mobile.
189 (3) The next two-hundred fifty thousand dollars
190 ($250,000) shall be distributed to the Alabama Surface Mining
191 Commission.
192 (4) The remaining revenues shall be deposited in the
193 Renewing Coal-Impacted Communities Act Fund and shall be used
194 by the department to award grants to eligible local
195 development organizations pursuant to the provisions of this
196 act.
