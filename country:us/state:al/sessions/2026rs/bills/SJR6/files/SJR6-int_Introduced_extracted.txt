4 SJR___ MOURNING THE DEATH AND CELEBRATING THE LIFE OF FORMER
5 REPRESENTATIVE BRYANT MELTON, JR.
6
7 WHEREAS, it is with profound sorrow and deep regret
8 that we record the passing of The Honorable Bryant Melton,
9 Jr. on December 17, 2025, at the age of 85; and
10 WHEREAS, through his selfless commitment and
11 dedication, Bryant Melton served to better the quality of
12 life in Alabama as an Associate Pastor, Legislator, and
13 civic leader, and he had a measurable positive impact on the
14 lives of its residents; he was a devoted Christian, husband,
15 father, public servant, educator, and veteran; and
16 WHEREAS, Rev. Melton was born on May 9, 1940, in
17 Sprott, Alabama, the seventh of nine children to the late
18 Bryant Melton, Sr. and Bertha Dobynes Melton; he accepted
19 Christ as his Savior at an early age and joined Poplar Grove
20 Baptist Church; and
21 WHEREAS, while attending Lincoln High School, he
22 served as President of the Student Government Association
23 and was President of his Senior Class; following graduation,
24 he began college at Alabama A&M University; however, his
25 studies were interrupted when he was drafted into the United
26 States Army in 1960; he served multiple tours of duty that
27 included a period in Korea; he later retired from the U.S.
28 Army Reserve; his military service was marked by dedication

31 WHEREAS, Bryant married his college sweetheart, the
32 lovely Emma Jean Holmes, on May 5, 1962; he went on to
33 complete his undergraduate studies at Stillman College,
34 graduating with a degree in Chemistry in 1965; in 2000, Rev.
35 Melton was awarded an honorary Doctor of Laws Degree by
36 Alabama A&M University; and
37 WHEREAS, during his early career, Rev. Melton worked
38 in various positions, including as a United States postman,
39 insurance salesman for Protective Insurance, planner with
40 Community Service Programs of West Alabama, quality control
41 specialist with B.F. Goodrich for 25 years, and teacher at
42 Druid High School and Hale County Training School; in 1990,
43 Rev. Melton became employed by Shelton State Community
44 College, where he held various administrative positions
45 until he retired in August 2006; and
46 WHEREAS, Rev. Melton was elected to the Alabama
47 Legislature in 1982, and he was re-elected seven times
48 before retiring in 2006; he was the first Black Legislator
49 elected from Tuscaloosa County since Reconstruction; his
50 legacy includes helping to redesign the structures of
51 Tuscaloosa's city and county governments, as well as the
52 city school board, to be more representative of their
53 populations; he was also the sponsor and co-sponsor of the
54 Mercedes-Benz bills that brought jobs and economic
55 development to Tuscaloosa and West Alabama; and
