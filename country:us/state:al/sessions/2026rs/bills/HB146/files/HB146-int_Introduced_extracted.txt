4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Existing law requires Ivermectin to be dispensed
7 physician.
8 This bill would permit a physician, a physician
9 assistant, or a certified registered nurse practitioner
10 to give a pharmacist a standing order to dispense
11 Ivermectin to individuals without an individual
12 prescription. The standing order would contain a
13 protocol providing the pharmacist with guidelines for
14 providing Ivermectin to individuals who request it.
15 This bill would prohibit health care providers
16 and pharmacists from participating in any program or
17 receiving any benefit that would induce them to
18 encourage patients or the public to use Ivermectin.
19 This bill would also prohibit health care
20 providers qualified to give a standing order and
21 pharmacists who follow a standing order from being
22 subject to disciplinary action from the respective
23 governing licensing board as a result of dispensing
24 Ivermectin.

TO BE ENTITLED
HB146 INTRODUCED
29 AN ACT
30
31 Relating to prescription drugs; to provide for the
32 dispensing of Ivermectin by a pharmacist without an individual
33 prescription pursuant to a standing order issued by a
34 physician, physician assistant, or certified registered nurse
35 practitioner; and to protect physicians, physician assistants,
36 certified registered nurse practitioners, and pharmacists from
37 licensure disciplinary action due to dispensing Ivermectin.
38 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
39 Section 1. (a) For the purposes of this section, the
40 following terms have the following meanings:
41 (1) HEALTH CARE PROVIDER. A physician licensed by the
42 Medical Licensure Commission pursuant to Article 8, Chapter 24
43 of Title 34, Code of Alabama 1975; a physician assistant
44 licensed by the State Board of Medical Examiners pursuant to
45 Article 7, Chapter 24 of Title 34, Code of Alabama 1975; or a
46 certified registered nurse practitioner licensed and certified
47 by the Board of Nursing pursuant to Article 5, Chapter 21 of
48 Title 34, Code of Alabama 1975.
49 (2) STANDING ORDER. A prescription order that is not
50 patient specific.
51 (b) A pharmacist may dispense Ivermectin without a
52 prescription to an individual who is 19 years of age or older,
53 or to an unemancipated individual under 19 years of age upon
54 written consent of the individual's parent or legal guardian,
55 pursuant to a standing order that is signed by a health care
56 provider.

57 (c) The standing order shall include all of the
58 following:
59 (1) A risk assessment protocol for use by the
60 pharmacist to screen an individual who requests Ivermectin and
61 which includes a plan for treating adverse events.
62 (2) A standardized information sheet that provides the
63 following information for the individual who is dispensed
64 Ivermectin:
65 a. Indications and contraindications for the use of
66 Ivermectin.
67 b. Instructions for using Ivermectin.
68 c. A statement on the importance of follow-up care and
69 health care referral information.
70 (3) A form for the pharmacist to record the completion
71 of the risk assessment protocol and the dosage dispensed to
72 the individual.
73 (d) No health care provider who signs a standing order,
74 and no pharmacist who dispenses Ivermectin pursuant to a
75 standing order, shall participate in any program or accept
76 anything of value that induces the health care provider or
77 pharmacist to affirmatively commend or endorse the use of
78 Ivermectin to an individual or the public.
79 (e)(1) The governing licensing board shall not take
80 disciplinary action against a health care provider who signs a
81 standing order for Ivermectin that is based upon a
82 pharmacist's dispensing of Ivermectin pursuant to that
83 standing order.
84 (2) The State Board of Pharmacy shall not take

92 c. The pharmacist's refusal to dispense Ivermectin upon
