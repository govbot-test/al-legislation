6 TO BE ENTITLED
7 AN ACT
8
9 Relating to the Department of Workforce; to establish a
10 talent recruitment grant program administered by the
11 department; to award grant funds to eligible applicants; to
12 provide eligibility standards; and to establish the Talent
13 Recruitment Program Grant Fund.
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
15 Section 1. For the purposes of this act, the following
16 terms have the following meanings:
17 (1) DEPARTMENT. The Alabama Department of Workforce.
18 (2) HOUSEHOLD. A group of one or more individuals who
19 dwell together within the same dwelling.
20 (3) HOUSEHOLD GOAL. The total number of households that
21 a talent recruitment program seeks to successfully incentivize
22 to relocate or commit to relocate from outside this state to a
23 county or municipality in this state.
24 Section 2. (a) Subject to the availability of funds,
25 the department shall:
26 (1) Establish and administer a workforce talent
27 recruitment grant program for the purpose of awarding grants
28 for talent recruitment programs that incentivize households to
Page 1
for talent recruitment programs that incentivize households to

for HB354
29 relocate from outside this state to this state;
30 (2) Award grants to eligible applicants for the purpose
31 of administering the talent recruitment program and the costs
32 associated with incentivizing households to relocate from
33 outside this state to this state; and
34 (3) Disburse 50 percent of the total grant award upon
35 entering into a grant contract and 50 percent of the total
36 grant award upon the recipient reporting to the department
37 that the recipient has successfully met half of the household
38 goal stated in the talent recruitment program plan. If the
39 recipient fails to meet half of its stated household goal, the
40 department shall not disburse the remaining grant amount.
41 (b) Eligible applicants for the talent recruitment
42 program shall include:
43 (1) A municipality or county in this state; and
44 (2) A nonprofit organization that has a mission
45 relating to economic development, workforce and talent
46 development, or community development.
47 (c) Applicants for the talent recruitment program shall
48 do all of the following:
49 (1) Provide the department with a talent recruitment
50 program plan that includes:
51 a. The total estimated cost of the program;
52 b. The individual estimated costs associated with the
53 program's design, administration, marketing, and relocation
54 incentive initiatives;
55 c. The program's plan to assure that new households do
56 not displace households that already live in the county or
Page 2
not displace households that already live in the county or

57 municipality;
58 d. The program's household goal;
59 e. The estimated total grant amount per relocated
60 household;
61 f. The program's estimated state and local tax impact;
62 and
63 g. The program's estimated total economic impact.
64 (2) Demonstrate the ability to contribute at least 20
65 percent of the total talent recruitment program cost. The
66 applicant's contribution may include local investments and
67 in-kind donations. In order for a previous grant recipient to
68 be eligible for additional grant funds, the applicant must
69 have met the household goal stated in the talent recruitment
70 program plan.
71 (d) Each grant recipient must provide semiannual
72 reports to the department with the following information:
73 (1) The total number of household applications.
74 (2) The total number of approved households.
75 (3) The cost per approved household.
76 (4) The annual incomes and occupations of approved
77 households.
78 (5) The economic impact of the talent recruitment
79 program, including state and local tax revenue and new
80 consumer spending.
81 (6) Verification of eligibility for moved and committed
82 to move households.
83 (e) A household is eligible for the talent recruitment
84 program incentives if the household meets all of the following

85 requirements:
86 (1) Resides outside of this state at the time the
87 household applies for talent recruitment program incentives.
88 (2) Has an individual household income equal to or
89 greater than the median household income for the state, as
90 reported in the most recently completed decennial census
91 published by the United States Bureau of the Census.
92 (3) Submits an application to the recipient of a grant
93 to receive talent recruitment program incentives.
94 Section 3. (a) The Talent Recruitment Program Grant
95 Fund is created in the State Treasury.
96 (b) The fund shall consist of all monies paid into the
97 State Treasury to the credit of the fund or by legislative
98 appropriations, grants, gifts, or otherwise.
99 (c) Monies contained in the fund may be expended for
100 grants as provided under this act.
101 (d) At the end of any fiscal year, any unexpended or
102 unencumbered monies contributed to or deposited in the fund
103 from any source shall remain in the fund for the use of the
104 department for the talent recruitment grant program.
105 (e) All monies in the fund shall be budgeted and
106 allotted pursuant to the Budget Management Act in accordance
107 with Article 4 of Chapter 4 and Chapter 19 of Title 41, Code
108 of Alabama 1975, and only in the amount provided by the
109 Legislature in the general appropriations act or other
110 appropriations act.
111 Section 4. This act shall become effective on October
112 1, 2026.
