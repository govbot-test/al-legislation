8 Relating to rural healthcare; to authorize regulated

10 entities; to establish a certification and active supervision
11 framework administered by the state; to articulate a state
12 policy displacing competition where necessary to preserve
13 rural healthcare access and quality; to provide limited
14 immunity from state and federal antitrust laws consistent with
15 the state action doctrine; to prescribe application, review,
16 monitoring, renewal, and enforcement procedures; to limit
17 immunity to approved and supervised conduct; and to provide
18 related protections and safeguards.
19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
20 Section 1. This act may be known and cited as the Rural
21 Health Antitrust Immunity Act.
22 Section 2. The Legislature finds and declares the
23 following:
24 (1) Alabama’s rural communities face persistent
25 disparities in access to care, workforce capacity, health
26 outcomes, and healthcare infrastructure, requiring coordinated
27 and sustained action across state agencies, the Legislature,
28 providers, and community partners.

29 (2) Rural healthcare facilities are closing and losing
30 important specialty services due to declining populations,
31 diminishing health literacy and nutrition awareness,
32 increasing costs, and the inability to recruit and retain
33 qualified physicians and other healthcare professionals.
34 (3) The One Big Beautiful Bill Act, signed into law on
35 July 4, 2025, by President Donald J. Trump, established the
36 Rural Health Transformation Program, which authorizes states
37 to submit plans to strengthen rural communities by improving
38 healthcare access, quality, and outcomes by transforming the
39 healthcare delivery system. On November 5, 2025, the state
40 submitted its plan, known as the Alabama Rural Health
41 Transformation Program, to the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid
42 Services, which includes policy reforms, innovative care
43 models, shared-service infrastructure, and workforce
44 initiatives.
45 (4) On December 18, 2025, Governor Kay Ivey issued
46 Executive Order No. 741 which established the Alabama Rural
47 Health Transformation Advisory Group to coordinate policy
48 development, stakeholder engagement, and intergovernmental
49 collaboration.
50 (5) Collaboration among entities and individuals to
51 expand access to healthcare in rural areas of the state is in
52 the best interest of Alabama citizens.
53 (6) It is the policy of this state to improve
54 healthcare access, healthcare quality, and health outcomes for
55 Alabama citizens who live in one of the state’s rural counties
56 through the Alabama Rural Health Transformation Program’s

57 initiatives, and, where necessary, to substitute regulated

61 healthcare markets, when reasonably necessary to advance the

65 (7) It is the intent of the legislature to exempt from
66 state anti-trust laws, and provide immunity from federal
67 anti-trust laws through the state action doctrine, to entities
68 and individuals carrying out the state’s policy provided in
69 this Act.
70 (8) This Act is intended to satisfy the requirements of
71 the state-action doctrine under federal antitrust law.
