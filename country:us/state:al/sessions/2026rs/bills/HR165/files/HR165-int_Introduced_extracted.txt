5 MCLAREN KELLY, SR.
6
7 WHEREAS, it is with profound sadness that we note the
8 death of Jerry McLaren Kelly, Sr. of Brewton, Alabama, on
9 February 1, 2026, at the age of 89; and
10 WHEREAS, Jerry was born on March 20, 1936, and was a
11 true Brewton man; his education began locally and included a
12 very proud history of playing football and basketball for
13 T.R. Miller High School; he played guard in the 1952 State
14 Basketball Championship in which the team finished
15 undefeated, and he is the only T.R. Miller athlete who was
16 awarded All-State for both basketball and football; Jerry
17 also qualified to try out for the Olympic Trials in diving
18 but was too young to compete; and
19 WHEREAS, he enjoyed his time at The University of
20 Alabama (UA), where he completed a Bachelor of Science in
21 Commerce while remaining an active member of the Sigma Alpha
22 Epsilon Fraternity; Jerry was a founding member of the
23 "Never Should Have Made It Club" at UA and an original
24 member of Tide Pride; and
25 WHEREAS, he honorably served his country as a United
26 States Army 2nd Lieutenant at Fort Bliss in Lawton,
27 Oklahoma, and as 1st Lieutenant at Fort Benning in Columbus,
28 Georgia, during which he served as a paratrooper with the
Page 1
Georgia, during which he served as a paratrooper with the

29 101st Airborne Unit and as a marksman; and
30 WHEREAS, Mr. Kelly was a faithful and devoted
31 Christian; he was an active member of the First Methodist
32 Church of Brewton for over 80 years where he served as usher
33 and on various committees, including being the Chairman of
34 Pastor Parish Relations, Finance Committee, and Church
35 Council; and
36 WHEREAS, he had an expansive banking career that
37 spanned over 65 years, of which he dedicated 58 years to the
38 Bank of Brewton, directly impacting the long legacy of that
39 institution; and
40 WHEREAS, he further served his community as the Chair
41 of the Brewton Chamber of Commerce, Brewton City Schools
42 board member, board member of D.W. McMillan Hospital,
43 Director of the Economic Development Authority, YMCA Board
44 of Directors, member of the Lions Club, Kiwanis Club, and
45 Rotary Club, member of the Alabama Bankers' Association,
46 supporter and sponsor of T.R. Miller High School athletics,
47 and advisor and mentor to numerous other civic and
48 charitable campaigns throughout Brewton; and
49 WHEREAS, he loved traveling the world, hunting, The
50 University of Alabama, and spending time on Pensacola Beach
51 with his family; numbers were his love language, and he was
52 tickled to make bets; his dedication to his family, faith
53 and church, career, and to the world of finance was obvious
54 to all who knew him; and
55 WHEREAS, he was preceded in death by his father,
56 Albert Edward Kelly, Sr.; mother, Helen Roberts Kelly;
