6 WHEREAS, the definition of autism has evolved over
7 time, resulting in an increasingly heterogeneous spectrum of
8 abilities, needs, and life experiences; and
9 WHEREAS, while the entire autism spectrum deserves
10 recognition, understanding, and support, the term profound
11 autism is intended to provide clarity regarding individuals
12 who require 24-hour care from an adult caregiver throughout
13 their lives; and
14 WHEREAS, individuals with profound autism may also
15 have a severe intellectual disability and may have minimal
16 or no functional language, creating distinct and complex
17 support needs; and
18 WHEREAS, due to these unique challenges, individuals
19 with profound autism are frequently excluded from research
20 initiatives, services, and support programs that are not
21 adequately designed to meet their level of need; and
22 WHEREAS, in April 2023, the Centers for Disease
23 Control and Prevention released its first prevalence study
24 on profound autism, concluding that 26.7 percent of children
25 eight years of age with autism in the United States meet the
26 criteria for profound autism, meaning that approximately one
27 in four children with autism are profoundly affected; and
