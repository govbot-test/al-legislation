5 AVENUE IN MONTGOMERY THE KAY IVEY OFFICE BUILDING.
6
7 WHEREAS, Alabama's 54th governor, Kay Ellen Ivey, is
8 the longest continuously serving governor in state history
9 and the longest-serving female governor in American history;
10 and
11 WHEREAS, Governor Ivey grew up in the small town of
12 Camden, in Wilcox County, the only child of Boadman Nettles
13 Ivey and Barbara Elizabeth Ivey, who instilled in her the
14 values of hard work, faith, family, and community; and
15 WHEREAS, after volunteering for Lurleen B. Wallace's
16 successful 1966 gubernatorial campaign and concluding an
17 effective four-year career in student government at her
18 beloved Auburn University, Governor Ivey embarked on a
19 career that began in the classroom as a high school history
20 and English teacher and continued through service as a bank
21 officer, Assistant Director of the Alabama Development
22 Office, Reading Clerk of the Alabama House of
23 Representatives, Government Affairs Director for the Alabama
24 Commission on Higher Education, State Treasurer (2003-2011),
25 and Lieutenant Governor (2011-2017); and
26 WHEREAS, following the resignation of her
27 predecessor, then-Lieutenant Governor Kay Ivey became
28 Governor Kay Ivey when she took the oath of office at 6:01
Page 1
Governor Kay Ivey when she took the oath of office at 6:01
HJR253 INTRODUCED
29 p.m. on April 10, 2017, in the Old Senate Chamber of the
30 State Capitol, leading her to say, "Most governors have
31 three months to prepare; I had three hours"; and
32 WHEREAS, newly installed Governor Ivey not only
33 succeeded at "steadying the ship of state" after years of
34 tumult in Alabama state government but also wracked up an
35 unprecedented series of accomplishments through her quiet
36 but persistent, collaborative, and deliberate leadership;
37 and
38 WHEREAS, as Alabama's economic-development governor,
39 Governor Ivey presided over a state economy that
40 consistently ranked in the top 10 states nationally both for
41 its business climate and for in-migration, as evidenced by
42 the investment of $69 billion (billion with a "B") in new
43 capital investments and the creation of over 100,000 new
44 jobs; and
45 WHEREAS, as Alabama's infrastructure governor,
46 Governor Ivey's signature Rebuild Alabama program led to the
47 investment of more than $2 billion in upgrades to Alabama's
48 roads and bridges, funding some 500 vital road and bridge
49 projects in all 67 Alabama counties, an initiative
50 paralleled by a similar deployment of over $2.5 billion to
51 ensure universal access to high-speed, broadband Internet
52 across Alabama, and which is complemented by her directing
53 over $900 million to support water infrastructure projects
54 throughout the state; and
55 WHEREAS, as Alabama's education governor, Governor
56 Ivey's tenure has seen Alabama skyrocket in national
Page 2
Ivey's tenure has seen Alabama skyrocket in national
HJR253 INTRODUCED
57 rankings; for example, the state has moved from dead last to
58 32nd in fourth-grade math and 34th in fourth-grade reading
59 through the implementation of the Literacy and Numeracy
60 Acts, Governor Ivey's Strong Start, Strong Finish
61 initiative, the statewide expansion of the Dolly Parton
62 Imagination Library, the passage of the state's highest-ever
63 education budgets, including best-in-the-region teacher
64 salaries, and the banning of cell phones in public K–12
65 classrooms, all while authoring and championing the state's
66 adoption of universal school choice through her landmark
67 CHOOSE Act; and
68 WHEREAS, as Alabama's public-safety governor,
69 Governor Ivey has achieved generational successes in the
70 area of public safety, including the construction of new
71 prisons in Elmore and Escambia Counties to replace the
72 state's aging prison infrastructure, the restructuring of
73 the Alabama Board of Pardons and Paroles, a top-to-bottom
74 review of execution procedures, the establishment of a Metro
75 Area Crime Suppression Unit to bolster police in our Capital
76 City, and passage of the 2025 Safe Alabama legislative
77 package, which among other things included enhanced legal
78 protections for law enforcement officers; and
79 WHEREAS, Governor Ivey has also worked quietly but
80 persistently to ensure good government in Alabama, from
81 dissolving unnecessary state task forces, banning the
82 appointment of lobbyists to executive-branch positions, and
83 ending the misuse of local inmate food funds to successfully
84 navigating the COVID-19 pandemic (and some 33 other states

navigating the COVID-19 pandemic (and some 33 other states

85 of emergency), overseeing 10 consecutive budgets without
86 proration, requiring state agencies to maintain their public
87 funds in the State Treasury, improving accountability of
88 state-vehicle use, and enhancing state agencies'
89 responsiveness to public-records requests; and
90 WHEREAS, throughout it all, Governor Ivey has always,
91 always, always reflected Alabama values, from the right to
92 life, protection of children, and promotion of religious
93 liberty to good, old-fashioned hard work, Southern
94 hospitality, common sense, decency, and respect for others;
95 and
96 WHEREAS, there truly is no step too high for our most
97 celebrated and most loved "high-stepper," Governor Kay Ellen
98 Ivey; now therefore,
99 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
100 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That to honor Governor Ivey, who
101 has loved her state and served it so well, the Alabama
102 Building Renovation Finance Authority is strongly encouraged
103 to designate the State of Alabama office building located at
104 501 Washington Avenue in Montgomery the "Kay Ivey Office
105 Building," to sit in parallel to the state office building
106 named for her political inspiration, Lurleen B. Wallace, and
107 to stand as an enduring marker of our admiration and respect
108 for her successful tenure as Alabama's Chief Executive.
109 BE IT FURTHER RESOVLED, That the Alabama Building
110 Renovation Finance Authority take all appropriate steps,
111 consistent with applicable requirements and laws, to
112 effectuate this designation, including by causing Governor
