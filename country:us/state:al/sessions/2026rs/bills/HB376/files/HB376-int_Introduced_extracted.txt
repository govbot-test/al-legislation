4 SYNOPSIS:
5 This bill would create certain requirements for
6 instructional technology used by local boards of
7 education in public school classrooms.
8 This bill would require local boards of
9 education, upon request, to provide parents with
10 certain information about the student's use of and
11 access to instructional technology in the student's
12 classroom.
13 This bill would require the State Board of
14 Education to create a model policy on the use of
15 technology in public schools.
16 This bill would require the adoption and
17 implementation of the model policy by local boards of
18 education.
19 This bill would require the creation and
20 implementation of age-appropriate screen time limits
21 and Internet access limits for each grade level.
22 This bill would also require the State Board of
23 Education to adopt rules.

41 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
42 Section 1. (a) For the purposes of this section, the
43 following terms have the following meanings:
44 (1) INSTRUCTIONAL TECHNOLOGY. Technology that a student
45 uses in connection to curriculum and instructional
46 experiences. The term includes technology such as electronic
47 devices, digital tools, and digital applications.
48 (2) PARENT. The same meaning as defined in Section
49 16-6F-4, Code of Alabama 1975.
50 (3) PUBLIC SCHOOL. The same meaning as defined in
51 Section 16-46B-1, Code of Alabama 1975.
52 (4) SCREEN TIME. Time spent by a student using an
53 electronic device with a screen in a classroom setting. The
54 term does not include when the use of the electronic device
55 involves direct instruction, guidance, or interaction with a
56 teacher, instructor, or other designated educational

58 (b)(1) Instructional technology used in public schools:
59 a. May not be unreasonably used as a substitute for
60 direct, teacher-led instruction;
61 b. Must be designed specifically for instructional use;
62 c. Must be absent of design features that interfere
63 with learning or distract from instruction;
64 d. Must be effective in supporting student learning
65 outcomes;
66 e. Must be safe for students' physical, cognitive, and
67 emotional development;
68 f. Must be supported by evidence or demonstrated
69 educational value appropriate for the grade level;
70 g. Must be intentionally integrated to enhance learning
71 outcomes; and
72 h. Must be compliant with applicable state standards
73 and laws, including privacy and data protection regulations.
74 (2) Local boards of education shall select, deploy, and
75 monitor instructional technology in accordance with the
76 requirements of this subsection, and shall adopt internal
77 monitoring policies and conduct training for faculty and
78 staff, as necessary, to ensure compliance with this section.
79 (3) The State Board of Education shall adopt a method
80 for evaluating and measuring the effectiveness of the
81 requirements for instructional technology described in
82 subdivision (b)(1).
83 (4) Upon request, local boards of education shall
