4 HR____ MOURNING THE DEATH AND CELEBRATING THE LIFE OF
5 BARBARA ANN COLEMAN SHARP.
6
7 WHEREAS, it is with profound sorrow that we note the
8 peaceful passing of Mrs. Barbara Ann Coleman Sharp of
9 Mobile, Alabama, on February 2, 2026, while we also
10 celebrate a life marked by faith, service, and love that
11 ended at the age of 70; and
12 WHEREAS, her departure leaves a void in the lives of
13 her loving family, many friends, and the community where she
14 was highly regarded; and
15 WHEREAS, Barbara, a lifelong resident of Mobile, was
16 born on December 20, 1955, to the blessed union of Cornelius
17 and Milee Coleman; she was educated in the Mobile County
18 Public School System and proudly graduated from Toulminville
19 High School; she went on to earn her Bachelor's Degree at
20 Alabama State University; she also received a Master's
21 Degree in Education from Springhill College, laying the
22 foundation for a lifelong career in service to others; and
23 WHEREAS, upon completing her studies, Barbara
24 dedicated more than 30 faithful years of teaching at John
25 Will Elementary School in Mobile; known for her compassion,
26 patience, and commitment to children and education, she
27 touched countless lives before retiring after three decades
28 of distinguished service; and
