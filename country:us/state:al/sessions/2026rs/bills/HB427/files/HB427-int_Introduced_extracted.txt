4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, residential telephone
6 subscribers may request to be added to the state's
7 database of residential telephone subscribers who
8 object to receiving telephone solicitations.
9 This bill would authorize commercial and
10 cellular telephone subscribers to join the database.
11 Under existing law, telephone solicitors are
12 prohibited from circumventing the use of caller
13 identification systems.
14 This bill would prohibit telephone solicitors
15 from using misleading caller identification
16 information.
17 This bill would prohibit telephone solicitors
18 from making solicitation calls before 8:00 a.m. or
19 after 8:00 p.m.
20 This bill would prohibit telephone solicitors
21 from making more than three solicitation calls to a
22 given person over a 24-hour period.
23 Under existing law, the Public Service
24 Commission may commence a proceeding against a knowing
25 violation or threatened knowing violation of the
26 prohibitions related to telephone solicitations.
27 This bill would authorize the Public Service
28 Commission to commence a proceeding against any
Page 1

Commission to commence a proceeding against any
HB427 INTRODUCED
29 violation or threatened violation of the prohibitions.
30 Under existing law, the Public Service
31 Commission imposes a civil penalty of up to $2,000 for
32 each violation.
33 This bill would authorize the Public Service
34 Commission to impose a civil penalty of up to $20,000
35 for each violation.
36 Under existing law, a person who has received
37 more than one telephone solicitation in violation of
38 the law may seek damages of up to $2,000 for each
39 violation.
40 This bill would authorize a person who has
41 received more than one telephone solicitation in
42 violation of the law to seek up to $20,000 for each
43 violation.
44 This bill would also provide that a violation of
45 the law is a deceptive trade practice.
