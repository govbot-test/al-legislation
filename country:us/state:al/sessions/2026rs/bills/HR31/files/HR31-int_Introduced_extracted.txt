5 AMOS MCDANIEL, SR.
6
7 WHEREAS, it is with profound sorrow that we note the
8 passing of Deacon Amos McDaniel, Sr. of Thomasville,
9 Alabama, on January 7, 2026, while we also celebrate his
10 long, beautiful life that ended at the age of 96; and
11 WHEREAS, his life was a blessing, and he will be
12 lovingly remembered by the countless lives he touched; and
13 WHEREAS, Deacon McDaniel was born on April 19, 1929;
14 he was a longtime resident of Thomasville, where he was
15 widely known and loved by his community; and
16 WHEREAS, he devoted more than 50 years of his life as
17 a dedicated Trustee, Financial Secretary, and a member of
18 the Deacon Ministry for the New Greater First Baptist
19 Church; in fact, he was the last original cornerstone of the
20 New Greater First Baptist Church; and
21 WHEREAS, Deacon McDaniel was preceded in death by his
22 loving wife, Mary; and son Timothy; he leaves to cherish his
23 memory his devoted son, Amos McDaniel, Jr.; many faithful
24 grandchildren and great-grandchildren; and a host of other
25 relatives and friends; and
26 WHEREAS, revered as a man of honor, honesty, and true
27 integrity, Deacon McDaniel will forever be remembered as an
28 exceptional father, grandfather, brother, and friend; his
Page 1
exceptional father, grandfather, brother, and friend; his

29 life was a beautiful reflection of his faith in Christ, his
30 deep love for his family, and the joy he brought into every

32 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE
33 LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, That the death of Deacon Amos
34 McDaniel, Sr. is recorded with deep sadness, and even as we
35 mourn his death and the loss it represents, we also
