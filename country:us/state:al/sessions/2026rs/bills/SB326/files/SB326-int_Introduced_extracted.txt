4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, the Alabama State Law
6 Enforcement Agency (ALEA) is charged with enforcing
7 restrictions on the operation of vessels in the waters
8 of this state.
9 This bill would authorize ALEA, in consultation
10 with other certain regulatory entities, to adopt
11 various rules regulating the anchoring and rafting of
12 vessels. These rules may include enforcement mechanisms
13 such as fees.
14 This bill would also provide that a violation of
15 any of these rules is punishable as a boating
16 violation.

31 Section 1. (a) For purposes of this section, the
32 following terms have the following meanings:
33 (1) ALEA. The Alabama State Law Enforcement Agency.
34 (2) ANCHORING. The temporary securing of a vessel to
35 the bed of a water body solely by resistance of an anchor that
36 is dropped from the vessel which is carried aboard the vessel
37 while underway.
38 (3) EMERGENCY SITUATION. Any situation requiring a
39 vessel to remain anchored to avoid imminent danger to life or
40 property due to mechanical failure of the vessel, severe
41 weather, or other hazard.
42 (4) RAFTING. A group of two or more vessels that are
43 stationary upon a water body and are congregated together,
44 whether anchored, secured to one another, or adrift.
45 (5) VESSEL. Every description of watercraft, barge, or
46 other artificial contrivance capable of being used as a means
47 of transportation on the water.
48 (b) ALEA, in consultation with the Alabama Department
49 of Conservation and Natural Resources, Tennessee Valley
50 Authority, United States Army Corps of Engineers, United
51 States Coast Guard, and any other entity with regulatory
52 authority over a major body of water within the state, shall
53 adopt rules that:
54 (1) Prevent and establish a legal mechanism for the
55 removal of vessels that are long-term, unlawfully, or unsafely
56 anchoring, including vessels occupied by individuals whose
