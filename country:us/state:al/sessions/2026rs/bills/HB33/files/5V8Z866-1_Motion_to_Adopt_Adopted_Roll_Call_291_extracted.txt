4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, the Alabama State Law
6 Enforcement Agency (ALEA) is required to deny the
7 driver license or learner permit for the operation of a
8 motor vehicle to any individual under 19 years of age
9 who does not present certain graduation certificates,
10 or satisfy certain criteria set forth by ALEA at the
11 time of application.
12 This bill would change the age in which ALEA may
13 deny the driver license or learner permit for the
14 operation of motor vehicles for failure to provide
15 certain graduation certificates or satisfy other
16 criteria.
17 This bill would also further provide for
18 approved criteria accepted by ALEA during the driver
19 license and learner permit application process.

28 Alabama 1975, to change the age in which the Alabama State Law

Alabama 1975, to change the age in which the Alabama State Law
29 Enforcement Agency (ALEA) may deny a driver license or learner
30 permit application due to failure to provide or satisfy
31 certain graduation requirements or other criteria; and to
32 further provide for approved criteria accepted by ALEA during

34 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
35 Section 1. Section 16-28-40, as last amended by Act
36 2025-326, 2025 Regular Session, Code of Alabama 1975, is
37 amended to read as follows:
38 "§16-28-40
39 (a) The Alabama State Law Enforcement Agency shall deny
40 a driver license or a learner permit for the operation of a
41 motor vehicle to any individual under 19 17 years of age who
42 does not, at the time of application, present a diploma or
43 other certificate of graduation issued to the individual from
44 a secondary high school of this state or any other state, or
45 documentation that the individual satisfies satisfy one of the
46 following:
47 (1) The individual is enrolled and making satisfactory
48 progress in a nontraditional high school diploma option
49 program or a course leading to a high school diploma or
50 general educational development certificate (GED) from a state
51 approved institution or organization, or has obtained the
52 certificate.
53 (2) The individual is enrolled in a secondary school of
54 this state or any other state and has not at the time of
55 application accumulated disciplinary points while a student in
56 school which would extend the age of eligibility for the
Page 2
school which would extend the age of eligibility for the
57 student to apply for a driver license.
58 (3) The individual is participating in a job training
59 program approved by the State Superintendent of Education.
60 (4) The individual is gainfully and substantially
61 employed.
62 (5) The individual is a parent with the care and
63 custody of a minor or unborn child.
64 (6) A physician certifies that the parents of the
65 individual depend on him or her as their sole source of
66 transportation.
67 (7) The individual is exempted from this requirement
68 due to circumstances beyond his or her control as provided in
69 this chapter.
70 (8) The individual has graduated from a high school or
71 secondary school in this state.
72 (b)(1) The attendance officer or chief attendance
73 administrator, upon request, shall provide documentation of
74 enrollment status and disciplinary points on a form approved
75 by the State Department of Education to any student 15 years
76 of age or older who is properly enrolled in a school under the
77 jurisdiction of the official, for presentation to the Alabama
78 State Law Enforcement Agency, on application for, or renewal
79 or reinstatement of, a driver license or a learner permit to
80 operate a motor vehicle.
81 (2) Upon the request of a student seeking a fee
82 exemption on the basis of his or her homelessness, the staff
83 member designated to act as the local educational agency
84 liaison for homeless children and youth, pursuant to 42 U.S.C.
Page 3
liaison for homeless children and youth, pursuant to 42 U.S.C.
85 § 11432(g)(1)(J)(ii), shall provide documentation that the
86 student is a qualified student, on a form approved by the
87 State Department of Education, for presentation to the Alabama
88 State Law Enforcement Agency, on application for, or renewal,
89 reinstatement, or replacement of a driver license. The term
90 "qualified student" refers to a student who the local
91 educational agency liaison for homeless children and youth
92 verifies as meeting the definition of a "homeless child or
93 youth" as provided by 42 U.S.C. § 11434a(2) and whose
94 enrollment status and disciplinary points have been documented
95 pursuant to subdivision (1).
96 (3) Whenever a student 16 years of age or older
97 withdraws from school, the attendance officer or chief
98 attendance administrator shall notify the Alabama State Law
99 Enforcement Agency of the withdrawal. Withdrawal shall be
100 defined as more than 10 consecutive or 15 days total unexcused
101 absences during a single semester.
102 (c) Within five days of receipt of a notice of
103 withdrawal, the Alabama State Law Enforcement Agency shall
104 send notice to the licensee that his or her driver license or
105 learner permit will be suspended under this article on the
106 30th day following the date the notice was sent unless
107 documentation of compliance with this article is received by
108 the agency before the 30th day.
109 (d) Whenever the withdrawal from school of the student,
110 or the failure of the student to enroll in a course leading to
111 or to obtain a GED or high school diploma, is beyond the
112 control of the student, or is for the purpose of transfer to
Page 4
control of the student, or is for the purpose of transfer to
113 another school as confirmed in writing by the parent or
114 guardian of the student, or is for the purpose of
115 participating in a job training program approved by the State
116 Superintendent of Education, no notice shall be sent by the
117 proper school official to the Alabama State Law Enforcement
118 Agency to suspend the license of the student. If the student
119 is applying for or renewing a driver license or a learner
120 permit, the attendance officer or chief attendance
121 administrator, upon request, shall provide the student with
122 documentation to present to the Alabama State Law Enforcement
123 Agency to exempt the student from this section. The local
124 superintendent of education with the assistance of the county
125 or city school attendance director as the case may be, and any
126 other staff or school personnel, or the appropriate school
127 official of any private secondary school, shall be the sole
128 judge of whether the withdrawal is due to circumstances beyond
129 the control of the individual. Suspension or expulsion from
130 school or imprisonment in a jail or penitentiary is not a
131 circumstance beyond the control of an individual.
132 (e)(1) Any individual over 14 years of age who is
133 convicted of the crime of possession of a pistol on the
134 premises of a public school, or a public school bus, or both,
135 under Section 13A-11-72 shall be denied issuance of a driver
136 license or learner permit for the operation of a motor vehicle
137 for 180 days from the date the individual is eligible and
138 applies for a license or permit for the operation of a motor
139 vehicle. Any adjudication as a juvenile delinquent or youthful
140 offender where the underlying charge is the possession of a
Page 5
offender where the underlying charge is the possession of a
141 pistol on the premises of a public school, or a public school
142 bus, or both, under Section 13A-11-72 shall be considered a
143 conviction under this subsection, and the adjudication of an
144 individual as a juvenile delinquent or youthful offender where
145 the underlying charge is a violation under Section 13A-11-72
146 shall be reported to the Alabama State Law Enforcement Agency.
147 (2) If an individual over 14 years of age possesses a
148 driver license on the date of conviction, the Alabama State
149 Law Enforcement Agency, within five days of receipt of a
150 notice of conviction from the court, shall send notice to the
151 licensee that his or her driver license will be suspended. The
152 notice shall state that the license will be suspended for 180
153 days commencing on the 30th day following the date the notice
154 was sent unless documentation is received by the agency before
155 the 30th day that the individual was not convicted of the
156 crime. Upon the appropriate date, the agency shall suspend the
157 license.
158 (3) Upon the written request of the individual whose
159 license is denied or suspended, the Alabama State Law
160 Enforcement Agency shall afford the individual an opportunity
161 for a hearing in the same manner and under the procedure used
162 for other driver license suspensions. If the suspension or
163 denial of issuance determination is sustained by the Secretary
164 of the Alabama State Law Enforcement Agency or the authorized
165 agent of the secretary, upon such hearing, the individual may
166 file a petition in the appropriate court to review the final
167 order of suspension or denial by the secretary or the
168 authorized agent of the secretary in the same manner and under
Page 6
authorized agent of the secretary in the same manner and under
169 the same conditions as is provided in the case of suspensions
170 and denials.
171 (4) If the conviction is reversed within the 180-day
172 period, the Alabama State Law Enforcement Agency, upon receipt
173 of notice of the reversal from the Administrative Office of
174 Courts, shall reinstate a suspended license and shall accept
175 an application for a license and shall issue the license
176 according to law and regulation.
177 (5) The court shall notify the Alabama State Law
178 Enforcement Agency of the conviction of an individual over 14
179 years of age of a crime involving the possession of a pistol
180 on the premises of a public school or a public school bus, or
181 both, under Section 13A-11-72 and any reversal of the
182 conviction. The Administrative Office of Courts may adopt
183 necessary rules and regulations to implement this notification
184 procedure."
185 Section 2. This act shall become effective on August 1,
186 2026.
