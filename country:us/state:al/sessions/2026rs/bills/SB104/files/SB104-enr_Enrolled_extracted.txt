1 Enrolled, An Act,
2
3
4 Relating to boards and commissions; to require members
5 of boards and commissions that are subject to the Alabama
6 Sunset Law to complete a board governance course.
7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
8 Section 1. (a) For the purposes of this section, the
9 term "board member" means any member of any department,
10 council, board, commission, division, bureau, or other entity
11 subject to the Alabama Sunset Law of 1981, Chapter 20 of Title
12 41, Code of Alabama 1975. The term does not include any
13 cabinet member, statewide elected official, or any member of a
14 county or municipal governing body.
15 (b) All board members serving on October 1, 2026, shall
16 complete the board governance course provided for in
17 subsection (c) on or before March 1, 2027. Any board member
18 who begins a term of office after October 1, 2026, shall
19 complete the board governance course within 150 days after
20 beginning each term of office.
21 (c) The Department of Examiners of Public Accounts
22 shall provide a board governance course of not less than five
23 hours to all board members. The course's curriculum shall be
24 determined by the Chief Examiner of Public Accounts in
25 consultation with the Attorney General and shall include, but
26 not be limited to, all of the following topics as they relate
27 to board membership:
28 (1) The Alabama Open Meetings Act.
Page 1
(1) The Alabama Open Meetings Act.
SB104 Enrolled
29 (2) The role of the Contract Review Permanent
30 Legislative Oversight Committee.
31 (3) Travel, compensation, and per diem.
32 (4) Reoccurring significant issues determined by the
33 Department of Examiners of Public Accounts during audits and
34 reviews conducted pursuant to Section 41-20-9, Code of Alabama
35 1975.
36 (5) General operating procedures and best practices to
37 ensure the effective discharge of the duties of board members
38 in keeping with the highest standards of stewardship and
39 principles of public service.
40 (d) The board governance course shall be available
41 online and may be conducted online or in person. Evidence of
42 each board member's completion of the course shall be recorded
43 and maintained by the Department of Examiners of Public
44 Accounts.
45 (e) At the conclusion of the board governance course,
46 each participating board member shall sign a form developed
47 and provided by the Department of Examiners of Public Accounts
48 affirming all of the following principles:
49 (1) That each decision, action, and vote taken or made
50 as a board member shall be based solely on the needs and
51 interests served by the entity and the general welfare of the
52 public.
53 (2) That no decision, action, or vote shall be taken or
54 made to serve or promote the personal, political, or pecuniary
55 interests of a board member.
56 (3) That the views of all board members of the entity
Page 2
(3) That the views of all board members of the entity
SB104 Enrolled
57 shall be considered before making a decision or taking an
58 action on any measure or proposal before the entity.
59 (4) That the board member shall not make, individually
60 or jointly, any attempt to direct or corrupt the operations of
61 the entity in a manner that is inconsistent with the discharge
62 of the statutory functions and responsibilities of the entity.
63 (5) That the board member shall attend all meetings of
64 the entity unless good cause is shown.
65 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
66 1, 2026.
