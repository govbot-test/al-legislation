5 TO BE ENTITLED
6 AN ACT
7
8
9 Relating to Baldwin County; to amend Section
10 45-2-244.134, Code of Alabama 1975; to remove the requirement
11 that rescue squads in the county must be members of a certain
12 organization to receive a distribution of tobacco tax
13 proceeds.
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
15 Section 1. Section 45-2-244.134, Code of Alabama 1975,
16 is amended to read as follows:
17 "§45-2-244.134
18 The proceeds from the tax authorized, less the amount
19 or percentage of the actual cost of collection as may be
20 agreed upon by the Commissioner of the Department of Revenue
21 and the Baldwin County Commission, shall be distributed to the
22 Baldwin County Commission to be allocated on an annual basis
23 each fiscal year as follows:
24 (1) The first eighty thousand dollars ($80,000) of the
25 funds or portion received pursuant to this subpart shall be
26 equally divided among the rescue squads authorized by the
27 county commission. Each of the recipient rescue squads shall
28 be a Baldwin County recognized quasi-public entity, as defined
Page 1
be a Baldwin County recognized quasi-public entity, as defined
SB349 INTRODUCED
29 in Section 45-2-142.01, and a member in good standing with the
30 Alabama Association of Rescue Squads, Inc. Notwithstanding the
31 foregoing, any rescue squad receiving ad valorem tax funds
32 pursuant to Section 45-2-242 shall be excluded from the
33 distribution in this section regardless of the other
34 requirements being met.
35 (2) The next forty thousand dollars ($40,000) of the
36 funds or portion received pursuant to this subpart shall be
37 distributed to the office of the District Attorney of Baldwin
38 County for the creation and maintenance of a sexual assault
39 nurse examiner program.
40 (3) The remainder of the funds received pursuant to
41 this subpart shall be distributed to the office of the
42 District Attorney of Baldwin County."
43 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
44 1, 2026.
