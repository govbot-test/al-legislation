3 RECOGNIZING FEBRUARY 10, 2026, AS ALPHA PHI ALPHA DAY
4 AT THE ALABAMA STATE CAPITOL.
5 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Incorporated,
6 the first intercollegiate Greek-letter fraternity
7 established for African Americans, was founded at Cornell
8 University in Ithaca, New York, on December 4, 1906, by
9 Henry Arthur Callis, Charles Henry Chapman, Eugene Kinckle
10 Jones, George Biddle Kelley, Nathaniel Allison Murray,
11 Robert Harold Ogle, and Vertner Woodson Tandy, known as the
12 "Jewels"; who recognized the need for a strong bond of
13 brotherhood among African descendants in this country; and
14 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Incorporated,
15 for 119 years has been a beacon of light to the community by
16 standing true to its aims of manly deeds, scholarship, and
17 love for all mankind; and
18 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Incorporated,
19 has produced over 200,000 members that were inducted into
20 706 college and alumni chapters in the United States,
21 Europe, Asia, and the Caribbean; and
22 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Incorporated,
23 continues to make a difference in our society through the
24 fraternity's national programs of civic outreach initiatives
25 which accumulate over one million hours of community
26 service, producing $2.5 million in scholarships, and has
27 registering thousands of American citizens to participate in
28 the voting process; and
