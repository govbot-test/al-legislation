4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, counties and municipalities
6 may participate in various multijurisdiction
7 authorities created by the Legislature.
9 a county or municipality to unilaterally withdraw from
10 certain multijurisdiction authorities, establish a
11 successor entity to an authority, further provide for
12 the distribution or use of any monies previously
13 distributed to the authorities, and increase or
14 decrease the rate of any tax collected in conjunction
15 with the authority.
16 This bill would also require a multijurisdiction
17 authority to remit certain monies to a withdrawing
18 entity.

21 A BILL
22 TO BE ENTITLED
23 AN ACT
24
25 Relating to counties and municipalities; to authorize
26 the governing body of a county or municipality to withdraw
27 from a multijurisdiction authority, establish a successor
28 entity, further provide for the distribution or use of certain
Page 1
entity, further provide for the distribution or use of certain
SB91 INTRODUCED
29 monies previously distributed to an authority, and increase or
30 decrease the rate of any tax collected in conjunction with the
31 authority; and to require a multijurisdiction authority to
32 remit certain monies to a withdrawing entity.
33 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
34 Section 1. (a) For the purposes of this section, the
35 term " multijurisdiction authority" means any entity created
36 by the Legislature authorized to operate in more than one
37 county or municipality whose governing body includes members
38 appointed by the governing body of more than one county or
39 municipality.
40 (b) If a multijurisdiction authority does not have any
41 outstanding debt, the governing body of a county or
42 municipality, by resolution, may unilaterally withdraw from
43 the multijurisdiction authority.
44 (c) Following a withdrawal performed pursuant to
45 subsection (b), the governing body of a county or
46 municipality, by resolution, may do any of the following:
47 (1) Establish an entity that operates within the
48 boundaries of the county or municipality and performs
49 functions similar to the multijurisdiction authority from
50 which the county or municipality withdrew.
51 (2) Further provide for the distribution or use of any
52 monies raised or collected inside the territory administered
53 by the governing body and previously distributed to the
54 multijurisdiction authority by the Legislature.
55 (3) Increase or decrease by up to two percent the rate
56 of any tax collected in the county or municipality in
Page 2
of any tax collected in the county or municipality in

57 conjunction with the multijurisdiction authority from which
58 the county or municipality withdrew.
59 (d) Following a withdrawal performed pursuant to
60 subsection (b), the multijurisdiction authority shall remit to
61 the governing body of the withdrawing entity a portion of the
62 multijurisdiction authority's unencumbered monies that
63 corresponds to the percentage of the multijurisdiction
64 authority's total funding that was raised or collected from
65 the territory of the withdrawing county or municipality.
66 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
67 1, 2026.
