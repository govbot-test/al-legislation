Section 1. Tuskegee University has a unique

29 relationship to the State of Alabama. Due to the unique nature
30 of the circumstances related to the establishment and
31 development of Tuskegee Institute which differs from that of
32 private schools and colleges receiving funds from the state
33 and from the state's public colleges and universities, the
34 institution should be afforded a unique treatment relative to
35 funding from the Education Trust Fund. Tuskegee Institute was
36 established by an act of the Legislature in 1881 and
37 separately incorporated by an act of the Legislature in 1892;
38 the Governor appoints five commissioners who serve as voting
39 members on the Tuskegee University Board; and the State
40 Superintendent of Education serves as an ex officio voting
41 commissioner on the Tuskegee University Board. Tuskegee has
42 received appropriations from the Legislature since 1881. Being
43 a private institution as well as a state related and supported
44 institution, it is deemed that Tuskegee University should be
45 funded accordingly and not as the private schools and colleges
46 nor as the state public colleges and universities are funded.
