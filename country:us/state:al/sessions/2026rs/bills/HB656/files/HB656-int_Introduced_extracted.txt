4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, a city council may
6 establish a board of water and sewer commissioners to
7 administer water and sewer systems for the city.
8 This bill would require that before a board
9 located in a county in which a Class 2 municipality is
10 located may sell or develop real property owned by the
11 board, all local governments with jurisdiction over
12 the real property must approve.
13 This bill would also make nonsubstantive,
14 technical revisions to update the existing code
15 language to current style.

31 Section 1. Section 11-50-343, Code of Alabama 1975, is
32 amended to read as follows:
33 "§11-50-343
34 (a) Each board created under the provisions of this
35 article shall be deemed to be a public agency or
36 instrumentality exercising public and governmental functions
37 to provide for the public health and welfare, and each such
38 board is hereby authorized and empowered may do any of the
39 following:
40 (1) To adopt Adopt bylaws for the regulation of its
41 affairs and the conduct of its business;.
42 (2) To adopt Adopt an official seal and alter the same
43 at its pleasure;.
44 (3) To maintain Maintain an office at such a place or
45 places within the city as it the board may designate;.
46 (4) To sue Sue and be sued in its own name;.
47 (5) To acquire Acquire, purchase, lease as lessee,
48 construct, reconstruct, improve, extend, operate, and maintain
49 any water system or part thereof or any sewer system or part
50 thereof or any combination thereof within or without or partly
51 within and partly without the corporate limits of the city,
52 and to acquire by gift, purchase, or the exercise of the right
53 power of eminent domain lands or rights in land or water
54 rights in connection therewith and to acquire such personal
55 property as it the board may deem necessary in connection with
56 the construction, improvement, extension, enlargement, or

the construction, improvement, extension, enlargement, or

57 operation of any water system or sewer system and to hold and
58 dispose of all real and personal property under its control;,
59 provided, that any proceedings to acquire property by the
60 exercise of the power of eminent domain shall be conducted,
61 and the compensation to be paid shall be ascertained and paid
62 in the manner provided by the laws of the state then
63 applicable which relate to condemnation or the exercise of the
64 power of eminent domain by such the city;.
65 (6) To issue Issue revenue bonds of the board for any
66 of its corporate purposes, such the bonds to be payable solely
67 from revenues, and to refund its bonds, all as provided in
68 this article;.
69 (7) To combine Combine the water system and the sewer
70 system as a single system for the purpose of operation and
71 financing;.
72 (8) To fix Fix and revise from time to time and charge
73 and collect rates, fees, and charges for the use of or for the
74 services and facilities furnished by any system operated by
75 the board;.
76 (9) To exercise Exercise jurisdiction, control, and
77 supervision over any water system or sewer system owned,
78 operated, or maintained by the board and to make adopt and
79 enforce such rules and regulations for the maintenance and
80 operation of any such system as may, in the judgment of the
81 board, may be necessary or desirable for the efficient
82 operation of such the system and for accomplishing the
83 purposes of this article;.
84 (10) To make Make and enter into all contracts and

(10) To make Make and enter into all contracts and

85 agreements as the board may determine which that are necessary
86 or incidental to the performance of its duties and to the
87 execution of its powers under this article, and to employ such
88 consulting and other engineers, superintendents, managers,
89 construction and financial experts, accountants, and
90 attorneys, and such other employees and agents as it the board
91 may determine necessary in its judgment and to fix their
92 compensation;, provided, however, that all such expenses shall
93 be payable solely from funds made available under the
94 provisions of this article;.
95 (11) To enter Enter upon, use, occupy, and dig up, with
96 the consent of the city, any street, alley, road, highway, or
97 other public places necessary to be entered upon, used, or
98 occupied in connection with the acquisition, construction,
99 improvement, maintenance, or operation of any water system or
100 sewer system;.
101 (12) To receive Receive and accept from any federal
102 agency grants for or in aid of the construction of any water
103 system or sewer system or part thereof and to receive and
104 accept aid or contributions from any source of either money,
105 property, labor, or other things of value to be held, used,
106 and applied only for the purposes for which such the grants or
107 contributions may be are made; and.
108 (13) To do Do all acts and things necessary or
109 convenient to carry out the powers expressly granted in this
110 article.
111 (b) All resolutions adopted or promulgated by the board
112 shall, as soon as practicable after their passage, shall be
Page 4
shall, as soon as practicable after their passage, shall be
HB656 INTRODUCED
113 recorded in a book kept for that purpose and be authenticated
114 by the signature of the secretary or other administrative
115 officer authorized to authenticate such resolutions; and all
116 resolutions of a general or permanent nature shall be
117 published in some newspaper of general circulation in the
118 city, but if no such newspaper is published within the limits
119 of the city, such the resolutions may be published by posting
120 three copies thereof in three public places within the limits
121 of the city.
122 (c)(1) Notwithstanding subdivision (a)(5), or any other
123 law to the contrary, before a board, or successor entity, that
124 is located within a county in which a Class 2 municipality is
125 located may sell, convey any interest in, or develop any real
126 property owned by the board, all local governing bodies with
127 jurisdiction over the real property shall approve of the sale,
128 conveyance, or development by resolution of the governing
129 body.
130 (2) Approval or disapproval by a local governing body
131 pursuant to this subsection shall only be rendered after
132 notice and public hearing."
133 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
134 1, 2026.
