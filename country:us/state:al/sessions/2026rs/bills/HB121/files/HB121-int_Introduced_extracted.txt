4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under the Constitution of Alabama of 2022, the
6 Legislature has no power to revive any right or remedy
7 which may have become barred by lapse of time or by
8 statute.
9 This bill would propose an amendment to the
10 Constitution of Alabama of 2022, to allow the
11 Legislature to revive any time-barred civil claim
12 arising from child sexual abuse.
13 This bill would allow the Legislature to create
14 procedures relating to such claims.
15 This bill would also provide legislative
16 findings.

32 PROPOSED AMENDMENT
33 Section 95.1 is added to the Constitution of Alabama of
34 2022, to read as follows:
35 Section 95.1
36 (a) The Legislature makes the following findings:
37 (1) Child sexual abuse is a pervasive and devastating
38 crime that inflicts lifelong harm on survivors and causes
39 lasting damage to families and communities. Studies
40 demonstrate that approximately one in five girls and one in 13
41 boys in the United States experience sexual abuse during
42 childhood.
43 (2) Approximately one-third of victims never disclose
44 their abuse to anyone, ever. The remaining two-thirds
45 typically delay disclosure until adulthood, often decades
46 after the abuse occurred.
47 (3) Delayed disclosure is a well-documented and
48 predictable consequence of childhood sexual abuse.
49 (4) The current legal barriers prevent survivors of
50 child sexual abuse from pursuing civil claims, such as
51 statutes of limitation and claim-presentation deadlines,
52 undermine the pursuit of truth and accountability and deny
53 survivors meaningful access to justice.
54 (5) Accordingly, an amendment to the Constitution of
55 Alabama of 2022, is necessary to authorize the Legislature, by
56 general law, to revive any civil right or remedy arising from

60 (6) This authorization is necessary to:
61 a. Ensure that the laws of Alabama reflect modern
