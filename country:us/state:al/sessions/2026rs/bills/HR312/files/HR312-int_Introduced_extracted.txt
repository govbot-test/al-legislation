5 2, 2026.
6
7 WHEREAS, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated,
8 was founded on January 13, 1913, in Washington, D.C., by 22
9 collegiate women, with its first official act being
10 participation in the Women's Suffrage March; and
11 WHEREAS, Delta Sigma Theta currently has over 1,000
12 collegiate and alumnae chapters located in the United
13 States, Canada, Japan (Tokyo and Okinawa), Germany, the
14 Virgin Islands, Bermuda, the Bahamas, Jamaica, West Africa,
15 Southern Africa, the United Arab Emirates, and the Republic
16 of Korea; and
17 WHEREAS, there are 41 collegiate and alumnae chapters
18 in the State of Alabama with over 3,500 members; and
19 WHEREAS, the members of the Alabama chapters
20 recognize their continued duty and responsibility to public
21 service, which includes not only taking an active interest
22 in and participating in educating the masses regarding
23 issues, but also advocating for policies and policy changes
24 that impact our communities; and
25 WHEREAS, each year, the members of the Alabama
26 chapters convene at the Alabama State Capitol to educate,
27 advocate, and ensure that their collective voices are heard;
28 and

29 WHEREAS, this year's Alabama Delta Days at the State
30 Capitol will take place on April 2, 2026, under the theme:
31 "Solid in Our Sisterhood. Secure in Our Fight"; now
32 therefore,
33 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE
34 LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, That Delta Sigma Theta Sorority,
35 Incorporated, is hereby celebrated and congratulated on its
36 longevity and community service, and we take great pride in
37 recognizing the members' contributions on Delta Sigma Theta
38 Sorority Day on April 2, 2026.
