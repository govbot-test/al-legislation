5 JAMES FOUNDATION'S 20TH ANNUAL "HONORING OUR ELDERS DAY"
6 GALA.
7
8 WHEREAS, with great joy and special pleasure, we
9 honor Mrs. Veronica Curtis-Richie as the special guest at
10 the Rosetta James Foundation's 20th Annual "Honoring Our
11 Elders Day" Gala at the Jackson Center in Huntsville,
12 Alabama, on Saturday, March 14, 2026; and
13 WHEREAS, Mrs. Curtis-Richie is a lifelong resident of
14 Huntsville; she is a graduate of Butler High School and
15 matriculated to Alabama A&M University, where she earned a
16 Bachelor of Science in Special Education with a
17 concentration in Speech Pathology; she later earned a
18 Master's Degree in Learning Disabilities and a certification
19 in School Counseling; and
20 WHEREAS, throughout her 34-year career, Mrs.
21 Curtis-Richie served in Huntsville City Schools as a
22 learning disabilities teacher, drug education instructor,
23 coordinator of drug education, school counselor, and
24 curriculum specialist, reflecting an unwavering dedication
25 to students and families; after retiring in 2010, she served
26 nationally as a Professional Development Trainer with the
27 Military Child Education Coalition, supporting
28 military-connected families across the United States and
Page 1
military-connected families across the United States and

29 Guam; and
30 WHEREAS, Mrs. Curtis-Richie is deeply committed to
31 community service, serving as Treasurer for North Alabama
32 Community United for Action as well as serving on many civic
33 boards and initiatives, and she is a Silver Life Member of
34 the NAACP; she and her husband, Reverend Dr. W. Temple
35 Richie, Jr. are active members of First Missionary Baptist
36 Church, where she serves as a Deaconess and ministry leader;
37 she is a devoted mother and grandmother whose life
38 exemplifies leadership grounded in compassion, advocacy, and
39 faith; and
40 WHEREAS, the mission of the Rosetta James Foundation
41 is to honor the wisdom, service, and dedication of our
42 cherished senior citizens; the "Honoring Our Elders Day"
43 Gala is a heartfelt tribute to community and civic
44 volunteers 70 years of age and above who have dedicated
45 their lives to uplifting others and fostering positive
46 change; these remarkable individuals exemplify the very
47 spirit of compassion, leadership, and service that inspires
48 us all; and
49 WHEREAS, this annual celebration not only shines a
50 spotlight on these distinguished elders but also serves as a
51 platform to invest in the next generation of agents for
52 change; through the generous support of its attendees and
53 donors, the Gala raises vital funds to provide scholarships
54 for deserving students who embody the principles of
55 volunteerism, academic excellence, and civic engagement;
56 these outstanding students will also be recognized during
