3 COMMENDING ALICIA CANNON ON 50 YEARS OF SERVICE TO THE STATE
4 OF ALABAMA AND THE ALABAMA JUDICIAL SYSTEM.
5
6 WHEREAS, it is with the highest admiration and
7 gratitude that we recognize Alicia Cannon for 50 years of
8 faithful, distinguished, and impactful service to the State
9 of Alabama and to the Alabama judicial system; and
10 WHEREAS, Alicia Cannon is a graduate of G.W. Carver
11 High School in Montgomery and later earned her degree from
12 Alabama State University, laying the educational foundation
13 for a lifetime of professional excellence and public
14 service; and
15 WHEREAS, on Friday, February 27, 1976, Alicia Cannon
16 began her career in Alabama's court system as a Clerk and
17 Stenographer with the Department of Court Management,
18 entering state service during the early years of the
19 development of Alabama's modern Unified Judicial System; and
20 WHEREAS, shortly thereafter, she began what would
21 become a long and distinguished tenure in the Finance
22 Division of the Administrative Office of Courts (AOC),
23 serving as an Account Clerk and Purchasing Agent, where her
24 strong work ethic, attention to detail, and sound judgment
25 quickly set her apart; and
26 WHEREAS, through dedication and proven expertise,
27 Alicia Cannon was elevated to Deputy Purchasing Agent, then
28 rose to serve as Purchasing Director for the Administrative
Page 1
rose to serve as Purchasing Director for the Administrative

29 Office of Courts, and ultimately advanced to the leadership
30 role of Assistant Director of the AOC Finance Division; and
31 WHEREAS, throughout her career, Alicia Cannon has
32 demonstrated exceptional stewardship of public funds,
33 earning a reputation for frugality, fairness, and careful
34 oversight in purchasing, saving the State of Alabama
35 significant resources while ensuring that courts across the
36 state received the equipment and support they needed to
37 serve the public effectively; and
38 WHEREAS, she has long been a trusted resource and
39 friend to numerous chief justices and administrative
40 directors of courts and to countless judges, circuit clerks,
41 and court administrators throughout Alabama, generously
42 sharing her knowledge and experience, and helping guide
43 court financial and purchasing practices with
44 professionalism, integrity, and a spirit of service; and
45 WHEREAS, Alicia Cannon has also played a vital role
46 in mentoring and developing staff within AOC, freely sharing
47 her expertise, institutional knowledge, and practical wisdom
48 with generations of employees; and
49 WHEREAS, Alicia Cannon's 50-year (and counting)
50 career stands as a powerful testament to the value of
51 dedicated public servants whose quiet leadership, decades of
52 practical wisdom, and unwavering commitment strengthen
53 Alabama's courts and the administration of justice every
54 day; now therefore,
