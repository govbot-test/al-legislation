3 ESTABLISHING THE CRIME CLEARANCE DATA TASK FORCE.
4
5 WHEREAS, a 2024 Council on Criminal Justice Crime
6 Trends Working Group report identified significant gaps in
7 the nation's crime data which make it difficult for
8 policymakers and practitioners to develop and implement
9 tailored and timely interventions that are used to make
10 communities safer; and
11 WHEREAS, crime clearance rates are an important piece
12 of crime data that predict, albeit imperfectly, the
13 likelihood that a perpetrator will be caught and a victim
14 will have a chance to obtain justice; and
15 WHEREAS, crimes are most commonly cleared through an
16 arrest; clearance rates may be calculated in a jurisdiction
17 by dividing the total number of crimes cleared each year by
18 the total number of crimes committed that year; and
19 WHEREAS, clearance rates have declined dramatically
20 over the last half century, with murder cases only solved
21 approximately half the time now, as opposed to an
22 approximately 90 percent clearance rate in the 1950s;
23 property crimes are solved less than 15 percent of the time;
24 and
25 WHEREAS, low clearance rates send a message to
26 criminals that they have a good chance of escaping justice;
27 low clearance rates do not deter crime; and
28 WHEREAS, more than 3,000 local law enforcement

30 did not report crime data in 2022 for inclusion in the state
31 Uniform Crime Reporting program that informs the Federal
32 Bureau of Investigation's annual crime reports; the lack of

34 lack of resources in many law enforcement agencies, as 21
35 percent of the 18,000 law enforcement agencies in the United
36 States have four or fewer officers, and another 24 percent
37 have only five to nine officers; and
38 WHEREAS, improving clearance rates should be a
39 statewide priority; current data should be studied to
40 determine current clearance rate trends in Alabama and set
41 the framework for timely and accurate reporting to identify
42 continued gaps and possible improvements; now therefore,
43 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
44 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That there is established the
45 Crime Clearance Data Task Force to study crime clearance
46 rates, their impact on deterrence, and data reporting gaps.
