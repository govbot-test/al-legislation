4 HJR_34_ ESTABLISHING THE CRIME CLEARANCE DATA TASK FORCE.
5
6 WHEREAS, a 2024 Council on Criminal Justice Crime
7 Trends Working Group report identified significant gaps in
8 the nation's crime data which make it difficult for
9 policymakers and practitioners to develop and implement
10 tailored and timely interventions that are used to make
11 communities safer; and
12 WHEREAS, crime clearance rates are an important piece
13 of crime data that predict, albeit imperfectly, the
14 likelihood that a perpetrator will be caught and a victim
15 will have a chance to obtain justice; and
16 WHEREAS, crimes are most commonly cleared through an
17 arrest; clearance rates may be calculated in a jurisdiction
18 by dividing the total number of crimes cleared each year by
19 the total number of crimes committed that year; and
20 WHEREAS, clearance rates have declined dramatically
21 over the last half century, with murder cases only solved
22 approximately half the time now, as opposed to an
23 approximately 90 percent clearance rate in the 1950s;
24 property crimes are solved less than 15 percent of the time;
25 and
26 WHEREAS, low clearance rates send a message to
27 criminals that they have a good chance of escaping justice;
28 low clearance rates do not deter crime; and

29 WHEREAS, more than 3,000 local law enforcement
30 agencies, mostly representing less populous jurisdictions,
31 did not report crime data in 2022 for inclusion in the state
32 Uniform Crime Reporting program that informs the Federal
33 Bureau of Investigation's annual crime reports; the lack of
34 reporting and resulting gaps in data may be attributed to
35 lack of resources in many law enforcement agencies, as 21
36 percent of the 18,000 law enforcement agencies in the United
37 States have four or fewer officers, and another 24 percent
38 have only five to nine officers; and
39 WHEREAS, improving clearance rates should be a
40 statewide priority; current data should be studied to
41 determine current clearance rate trends in Alabama and set
42 the framework for timely and accurate reporting to identify
43 continued gaps and possible improvements; now therefore,
44 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
45 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That there is established the
46 Crime Clearance Data Task Force to study crime clearance
47 rates, their impact on deterrence, and data reporting gaps.
