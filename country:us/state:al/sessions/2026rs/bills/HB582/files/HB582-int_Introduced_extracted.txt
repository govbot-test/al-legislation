4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, the Vehicle Protection
6 Product Act regulates vehicle protection product
8 which include vehicle protection devices, systems, or
9 services that are installed on a vehicle, designed to
10 prevent loss or damage to the vehicle, and include a
11 written warranty.
12 This bill would revise the Vehicle Protection
13 Product Act to include component parts of a vehicle
14 within the meaning of vehicle protection product.
15
16
17 A BILL
18 TO BE ENTITLED
19 AN ACT
20
21 Relating to consumer protection; to amend Section
22 8-33-2, Code of Alabama 1975, to provide that the requirements
23 of the Vehicle Protection Product Act apply to component parts
24 of a vehicle.
25 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
26 Section 1. Section 8-33-2, Code of Alabama 1975, is
27 amended to read as follows:
28 "§8-33-2
Page 1
"§8-33-2
HB582 INTRODUCED
29 As used in this chapter, the following words shall have
30 the following meanings:
31 (1) ADMINISTRATOR. A third party other than the
32 warrantor who is designated by the warrantor to be responsible
33 for the administration of vehicle protection product
34 warranties.
35 (2) COMMISSIONER. The Commissioner of the Department of
36 Insurance.
37 (3) COMPONENT PART. Any individual, functional part of
38 a vehicle which, when combined with other parts, constitutes a
39 motor vehicle. The term includes an engine, transmission, and
40 body panel.
41 (3)(4) DEPARTMENT. The Department of Insurance.
42 (4)(5) INCIDENTAL COSTS. Expenses specified in the
43 warranty incurred by the warranty holder related to the
44 failure of the vehicle protection product to perform as
45 provided in the warranty. Incidental costs may include,
46 without limitation, insurance policy deductibles, rental
47 vehicle charges, the difference between the actual value of
48 the stolen vehicle at the time of theft and the cost of a
49 replacement vehicle, sales taxes, registration fees,
50 transaction fees, and mechanical inspection fees.
51 (5)(6) SERVICE CONTRACT. A contract or agreement as
52 defined in subdivision (13) of Section 8-32-2.
53 (6)(7) VEHICLE PROTECTION PRODUCT. A vehicle protection
54 device, system, or service that is all of the following:
55 a. Installed on or applied to a vehicle or a component
56 part of a vehicle.
Page 2
part of a vehicle.
HB582 INTRODUCED
57 b. Is designed to prevent loss or damage to a vehicle
58 or a component part of a vehicle from a specific cause.
59 c. Includes a written warranty.
60 For purposes of this chapter, the term vehicle
61 protection product shall include, without limitation, alarm
62 systems,; body part marking products,; steering locks,; window
63 etch products,; pedal and ignition locks,; fuel and ignition
64 kill switches,; and electronic, radio, and satellite tracking
65 devices. A vehicle protection product is not insurance for any
66 purpose.
67 (7)(8) VEHICLE PROTECTION PRODUCT WARRANTOR or
68 WARRANTOR. A person who is contractually obligated to the
69 warranty holder under the terms of the vehicle protection
70 product warranty agreement. Warrantor does not include an
71 authorized insurer providing a warranty reimbursement
72 insurance policy.
73 (8)(9) VEHICLE PROTECTION PRODUCT WARRANTY or WARRANTY.
74 A written agreement by a warrantor that provides if the
75 vehicle protection product fails to prevent loss or damage to
76 a vehicle or a component part of a vehicle from a specific
77 cause, that the warranty holder shall be paid specified
78 incidental costs by the warrantor as a result of the failure
79 of the vehicle protection product to perform pursuant to the
80 terms of the warranty. Incidental costs may be reimbursed
81 under the provisions of the warranty in either a fixed amount
82 specified in the warranty or sales agreement or by use of a
83 formula itemizing specific incidental costs incurred by the
84 warranty holder.

warranty holder.
HB582 INTRODUCED
85 (9)(10) WARRANTY HOLDER. The person who purchases a
86 vehicle protection product or who is a permitted transferee.
87 (10)(11) WARRANTY REIMBURSEMENT INSURANCE POLICY. A
88 policy of insurance that is issued to the vehicle protection
89 product warrantor to provide reimbursement to the warrantor or
90 to pay on behalf of the warrantor all covered contractual
91 obligations incurred by the warrantor under the terms and
92 conditions of the insured vehicle protection product
93 warranties sold by the warrantor."
94 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
95 1, 2026.
