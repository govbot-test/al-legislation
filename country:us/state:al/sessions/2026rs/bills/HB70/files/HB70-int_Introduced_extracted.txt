4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Prior to the enactment of Act 2017-131,
6 effective April 11, 2017, following a defendant's
7 conviction for a capital offense, the duty of the jury
8 was to review the aggravating and mitigating
9 circumstances associated with the defendant's offense
10 and return to the court an advisory verdict. The
11 sentencing court was required to review the advisory
12 verdict and give it consideration, but the advisory
13 verdict was not binding on the court.
14 Upon the enactment of Act 2017-131, when a jury
15 returns a verdict at sentencing, the trial court must
16 sentence the defendant in accordance with the jury's
17 verdict and may not overrule the jury's verdict.
18 This bill would provide a procedure for a
19 defendant in a capital murder case, who was sentenced
20 pursuant to a judicial override, to be resentenced
21 pursuant to existing law.

29 Relating to sentencing; to add Section 13A-5-46.1 to
30 the Code of Alabama 1975, to provide for resentencing for
31 certain defendants sentenced for capital murder; to repeal
32 Section 13A-5-47.1, Code of Alabama 1975, relating to
33 resentencing for certain defendants.
34 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
35 Section 1. Section 13A-5-46.1 is added to the Code of
36 Alabama 1975, to read as follows:
37 (a) A defendant convicted of a capital offense who was
38 sentenced pursuant to this article prior to June 1, 2026, on
39 petition to the sentencing court, shall be resentenced
40 pursuant to Section 13A-5-46, if the court finds all of the
41 following:
42 (1) The defendant was sentenced pursuant to the
43 provisions of this article prior to June 1, 2026, under the
44 law in effect on April 10, 2017.
45 (2) The jury returned an advisory verdict recommending
46 to the trial court that the imposed penalty be life
47 imprisonment without parole.
48 (3) The sentencing court overruled the jury's advisory
49 verdict and imposed a sentence of death.
50 (b) The petition shall be on a form and in a manner
51 established by the Administrative Office of Courts. Petitions
52 shall be considered authorized motions for modifications of
53 sentence, shall be assigned a unique identifier by the
54 Administrative Office of Courts, and shall not require payment
55 of a filing fee.
56 (c) The petition for resentencing shall be heard by the

57 original sentencing judge or his or her successor, the

73 the statewide automated victim notification system established
74 pursuant to Section 15-22-36.2.
75 (f) The Administrative Office of Courts shall adopt
76 rules to implement and administer this section.
77 Section 2. Section 13A-5-47.1, Code of Alabama 1975,

79 defendants convicted of capital murder, is repealed.
