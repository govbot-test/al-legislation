SB30 Enrolled

4 Relating to civil procedure; to render unenforceable
5 any provision of an agreement that prohibits an individual or
6 entity from disclosing certain acts of sexual abuse or facts
7 related to certain acts of sexual abuse.
8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
9 Section 1. This act shall be known and may be cited as
10 Trey's Law.
11 Section 2. The Legislature finds and declares all of
12 the following:
13 (1) Sexual abuse causes significant and lasting harm to
14 individuals and society as a whole.
15 (2) Sexual abuse survivors often experience long-term
16 physical, psychological, and emotional trauma.
17 (3) The State of Alabama has a compelling interest in
18 preventing sexual abuse, supporting victims, and ensuring that
19 the law does not shield perpetrators or impede public safety.
20 (4) Any agreement that prohibits an individual or
21 entity from disclosing an act of sexual abuse or facts related
22 to an act of sexual abuse is against the public policy of this
23 state.
24 (5) This act is intended to protect survivors' rights
25 to speak freely and to maintain privacy and confidentiality
26 regarding their identities, experiences, and trauma.
27 Section 3. (a) For the purposes of this section, the
28 term "sexual abuse" means any conduct that would constitute a
Page 1
term "sexual abuse" means any conduct that would constitute a
SB30 Enrolled
29 criminal violation of Articles 4, 4A, 4B, 6, 6A, 8, or 11 of
30 Chapter 6 of Title 13A, Code of Alabama 1975, or any of the
31 offenses listed in Section 15-20A-5, Code of Alabama 1975,
32 regardless of whether the conduct has led to a criminal
33 charge, conviction, adjudication, or sentence.
34 (b)(1) Any provision of a nondisclosure agreement,
35 confidentiality agreement, employment agreement, settlement
36 agreement, or any other type of agreement that prohibits an
37 individual or entity from disclosing an act of sexual abuse or
38 facts related to an act of sexual abuse to another individual
39 or entity is void and unenforceable.
40 (2) This subsection shall apply to any agreement
41 entered into, executed, or amended on or after October 1,
42 2026, and shall apply solely to elements of an agreement
43 related to the act of sexual abuse and shall not apply to
44 other aspects of an agreement, including, but not limited to,
45 compensation.
46 Section 4. This act shall become effective on October
47 1, 2026.
