4 Relating to elections; to amend Section 11-43B-4, Code
5 of Alabama 1975; to further provide for qualification fees for
6 municipal elections in certain Class 4 municipalities; to
7 require indigent candidates to submit certain documentation to
8 qualify for fee exemptions.
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
10 Section 1. Section 11-43B-4, Code of Alabama 1975, is
11 amended to read as follows:
12 "§11-43B-4
13 (a) The initial elections provided for herein in this
14 chapter, and all subsequent elections, shall be conducted, the
15 vote canvassed, the results declared, and those elected assume
16 the duties of their offices in the same manner as provided by
17 the general law of the state pertaining to municipal elections
18 for mayor-council forms of government, except as otherwise
19 provided by state statute.
20 (b)(1) The mayor shall be elected by the whole of the
21 electors of the city and shall have been a resident of the
22 city for at least 90 days prior to his or her election.
23 (2) Council members shall be elected by the electors of
24 the district which they represent, and shall have been
25 residents of the district which they represent for at least 90
26 days prior to their election.
27 (c)(1) Any person individual desiring to become a
28 candidate for mayor, or council member, or member of the
Page 1
candidate for mayor, or council member, or member of the
SB165 Enrolled
29 municipal board of education must, at the time of filing a
30 statement of candidacy, shall pay to the city clerk the
31 qualifying fee to be set by ordinance. Notwithstanding Section
32 11-46-2, the qualifying fee for mayoral candidates shall be
33 five hundred dollars ($500), and the qualifying fee for
34 council member and municipal board of education member
35 candidates shall be two hundred fifty dollars ($250).
36 (2) If any person individual desiring to become a
37 candidate for mayor, or council member, or member of the
38 municipal board of education is financially unable to pay the
39 filing fee, then that person individual shall be exempted from
40 the fee upon attesting that the individual has a gross income
41 that does not exceed 130 percent of the federal poverty level
42 and filing with the city clerk a copy of the individual's most
43 recent federal tax return.verified statements from three city
44 electors that said candidate is indigent and not financially
45 capable of paying the fee.
46 (d) Within 30 days after the election, every candidate
47 shall file with the city clerk a sworn statement of all
48 election campaign expenses and of each person individual,
49 firm, or corporation which has contributed funds, itemized for
50 all expenditures and contributions of one hundred dollars
51 ($100.00) or more."
52 Section 2. This act shall become effective immediately.
