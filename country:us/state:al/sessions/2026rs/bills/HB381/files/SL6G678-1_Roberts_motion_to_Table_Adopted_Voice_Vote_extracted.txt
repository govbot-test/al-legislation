4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, the Alabama Department of
6 Public Health regulates the sanitation of food
7 preparation and lodging facilities at campsites, but
8 there are no standards to ensure that camp operators
9 have plans in place to address a range of situations in
10 which campers and staff may be endangered, such as
11 severe storms, flash flooding, or other natural or
12 man-made emergencies.
13 This bill would require camps with residential
14 facilities for campers to obtain an emergency
15 preparedness license from the Alabama Emergency
16 Management Agency as a condition for operation by
17 demonstrating that certain requirements are met. These
18 include requirements for hiring staff members,
19 effective means of communication between staff members
20 and campers in the event of an emergency, flood safety,
21 sheltering, and emergency and evacuation plans, to
22 include coordination with the local emergency
23 management organization.
