4 SJR___ CELEBRATING THE LIFE AND MOURNING THE DEATH OF JUNIOR
5 HAROLD SORRELLS.
6
7 WHEREAS, it is with profound sorrow that we mourn the
8 death, yet celebrate the life, of Junior Harold Sorrells,
9 whose passing on January 15, 2026, has left a void in the
10 lives of his loving family, many friends, and the community
11 where he was deeply respected; and
12 WHEREAS, a devout Christian, devoted family man, and
13 humble servant to others, Harold Sorrells lived a life
14 defined by faith, integrity, and quiet generosity; an
15 accomplished entrepreneur and respected leader in the
16 trucking industry, he was equally known for his unwavering
17 commitment to his church, his community, and for helping
18 others without seeking personal recognition; and
19 WHEREAS, Junior Harold Sorrells was born on May 15,
20 1937, in Covington County, Alabama, to Abe Madison Sorrells
21 and Annie Caroline Sorrells; and
22 WHEREAS, after graduating with honors from Opp High
23 School in 1955, he proudly served in the United States Navy
24 aboard the U.S.S. Yorktown, attaining the rank of Radarman
25 First Class; in July 1959, after he returned home, he
26 purchased his first truck, beginning a successful and
27 enduring career in the trucking industry; and
