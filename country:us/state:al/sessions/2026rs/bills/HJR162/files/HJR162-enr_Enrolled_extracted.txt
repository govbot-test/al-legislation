4 ENCOURAGING THE ALABAMA MEDICAID AGENCY TO EVALUATE THE
5 OVERALL COSTS OF GLP-1 MEDICATIONS AND REPORT ITS FINDINGS.
6
7 WHEREAS, obesity is caused by a complex mix of
8 environmental and biological factors, and it puts
9 individuals at risk for other diseases, such as heart
10 disease and diabetes; and
11 WHEREAS, in 2019, the Centers for Disease Control
12 reported that obesity accounted for nearly $173 billion in
13 medical expenditures annually in the United States;
14 GlobalData estimates that obesity has negatively impacted
15 the State of Alabama's GDP by $7.2 billion and annually
16 impacts the state budget by over $750 million; 2024
17 Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System data estimates
18 the prevalence of obesity in Alabama is estimated at 38.9
19 percent for adults and 15.6 percent for children enrolled in
20 the Women, Infants, and Children program in Alabama; and
21 WHEREAS, the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid
22 Services recently launched two new drug pricing models that
23 could positively impact Medicaid programs; the true impact
24 will be known when the participating manufacturers and
25 medications are announced by Spring 2026, approximately; and
26 WHEREAS, the Alabama Medicaid Agency estimates
27 approximately 110,000 adult and child members may qualify
28 for GLP-1 medications for weight management based on current
Page 1
for GLP-1 medications for weight management based on current
HJR162 Enrolled
29 FDA labeling; it is important to determine whether GLP-1
30 medications could benefit Alabamians; now therefore,
31 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
32 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That the Alabama Medicaid Agency
33 is encouraged to:
34 (1) Determine actual savings that might exist in all
35 facets of the program should coverage of GLP-1 medications
36 be appropriate;
37 (2) Evaluate the overall costs of coverage of GLP-1
38 weight management medications in the context of potential
39 savings from new drug pricing models;
40 (3) Continue to have conversations with providers and
41 stakeholders in an effort to achieve optimal improved health
42 outcomes and cost savings for the State of Alabama should
43 coverage be appropriate; and
44 (4) Report its findings to the Legislature prior to
45 the 2027 Legislative Session.
