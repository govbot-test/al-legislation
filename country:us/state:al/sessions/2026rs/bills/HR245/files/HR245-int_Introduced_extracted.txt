4 HR____ MOURNING THE DEATH AND CELEBRATING THE LIFE OF
5 MARGUERITE JORDAN STONE.
6
7 WHEREAS, with profound sorrow, we note the passing of
8 Marguerite Jordan Stone of Birmingham, Alabama, while we
9 also celebrate her long, accomplished life that ended at the
10 age of 98; and
11 WHEREAS, her departure leaves a void in the lives of
12 her loving family, many friends, and the community where she
13 was highly regarded; and
14 WHEREAS, the second of five children, Marguerite was
15 born to the late William and Beola Lewis Jordan on November
16 15, 1927, in Birmingham; by 12 years of age, both of her
17 parents and her brother were deceased; after her parents'
18 deaths, Marguerite and two sisters were raised by their
19 grandmother, Minnie Lewis Bell; her third sister was raised
20 by a nearby family member, allowing the four sisters to
21 remain close; and
22 WHEREAS, she joined St. Luke A.M.E. Zion Church as a
23 child and worked diligently as a youth; Marguerite was a
24 choir member, chaired several programs, taught Sunday
25 School, and formed the Altar Guild; she was a devout
26 Christian who later affiliated with Sixth Avenue Baptist
27 Church as a member of a missionary circle, Sunday School,
28 and Bible Study; she was honored on two separate occasions
