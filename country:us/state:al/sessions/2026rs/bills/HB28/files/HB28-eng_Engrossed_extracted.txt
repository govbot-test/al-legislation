7 AN ACT
8
9 Relating to income tax; to establish a tax credit for
10 restaurants that recycle oyster shells.
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
12 Section 1. The Legislature finds and declares that
13 oyster shell material is a beneficial natural resource for
14 local water quality, aquatic habitats, and the protection of
15 the coastline of this state. An income tax credit for
16 restaurants that participate in approved oyster recycling
17 programs would incentivize the facilitation of more of this
18 resource to the benefit of the state.
19 Section 2. (a) For purposes of this section, the
20 following terms have the following meanings:
21 (1) DEPARTMENT. The Alabama Department of Revenue.
22 (2) INCOME TAX. The tax imposed by Chapter 18 of Title
23 40, Code of Alabama 1975.
24 (3) OYSTER SHELL RECYCLING PROGRAM. The Oyster Shell
25 Recycling Program or any other oyster shell recycling program
26 or activity designated by rule by the Alabama Department of
27 Revenue as an approved program or activity.
28 (4) OTHER ELIGIBLE PERSON. A state resident who
Page 1
(4) OTHER ELIGIBLE PERSON. A state resident who
HB28 Engrossed
29 possesses a valid license or permit issued pursuant to Section
30 9-12-82, 9-12-87, or 9-12-125, Code of Alabama 1975.
31 (5) RESTAURANT. A facility that prepares and sells food
32 directly to consumers for immediate consumption.
33 (b)(1) A restaurant or other eligible person that
34 donates oyster shell material to an oyster shell recycling
35 program may claim a credit against the state income tax
36 liability as provided by this section. The amount of the
37 credit shall equal the lesser of:
38 a. One dollar ($1) for every 50 pounds of oyster shell
39 material donated to a qualifying oyster shell recycling
40 program or activity during the tax year; or
41 b. Two thousand dollars ($2,000).
42 (2) The total amount of credits granted shall not
43 exceed one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000) per calendar
44 year.
45 (3) The granting of the tax credits authorized by this
46 section shall be on a first-come, first-served basis for the
47 tax year in which the oyster shell recycling occurred.
48 (c) If the amount of the credit exceeds the amount of
49 the taxpayer's tax liability for the taxable year, the excess
50 tax credit amount shall constitute an overpayment as defined
51 in Section 40-18-79, Code of Alabama 1975, and the department
52 shall refund the overpayment from the collections of the taxes
53 imposed.
54 (d) No tax credit provided by this section may be
55 transferred or sold to another taxpayer.
56 (e) The department shall do the following:
Page 2
(e) The department shall do the following:
HB28 Engrossed
57 (1) Provide a standardized format for a certificate to
58 be signed by the restaurant and certified by the Oyster Shell
59 Recycling Program to indicate the amount of oyster shells
60 recycled to substantiate the credit being claimed.
61 (2) Require the taxpayer to provide a copy of the
62 certificate when claiming the tax credit pursuant to this
63 section. Failure to file the required certificate shall result
64 in the automatic denial of the credit.
65 (f) The credit authorized in this section may be
66 claimed by corporations, individuals, and other entities in
67 accordance with the following provisions:
68 (1) An entity taxed as a corporation for Alabama income
69 tax purposes shall claim any credit authorized by this section
70 on its corporation income tax return.
71 (2) An individual shall claim any credit authorized by
72 this section on its income tax return.
73 (3) An entity taxed as a partnership, S-corporation, or
74 electing pass-through entity shall pass through any credit
75 authorized by the section to the partners, members, or
76 shareholders on a pro rata share basis.
77 (g) The department shall adopt rules to implement the
78 provisions of this section. The department shall engage and
79 collaborate with the Commissioner of the Department of
80 Conservation and Natural Resources and may incorporate
81 recommendations of the commissioner in any rules designating
82 oyster shell recycling programs and activities.
83 (h) The tax credit allowed under this section may be
84 claimed beginning with the 2027 tax year and may not be
