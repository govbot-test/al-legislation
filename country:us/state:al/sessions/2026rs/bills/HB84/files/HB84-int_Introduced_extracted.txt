4 SYNOPSIS:
5 This bill would propose an amendment to the
6 Constitution of Alabama of 2022, relating to Covington
7 County, to authorize the Judge of Probate of Covington
8 County to exercise equity jurisdiction concurrent with
9 that of the circuit court in cases originally filed in
10 the Probate Court of Covington County if the judge of
11 probate is licensed to practice law in the State of
12 Alabama.

15 A BILL
16 TO BE ENTITLED
17 AN ACT
18
19 To propose an amendment to the Constitution of Alabama
20 of 2022, relating to Covington County, to authorize the Judge
21 of Probate of Covington County to exercise jurisdiction
22 concurrent with certain circuit court cases if the judge of
23 probate is licensed to practice law in the State of Alabama.
24 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
25 Section 1. The following amendment to the Constitution
26 of Alabama of 2022, is proposed:
27 PROPOSED AMENDMENT
28 The Judge of Probate of Covington County may exercise
Page 1
The Judge of Probate of Covington County may exercise
HB84 INTRODUCED
29 equity jurisdiction concurrent with that of the circuit court
30 in cases originally filed in the Probate Court of Covington
31 County if the judge of probate is licensed to practice law in
32 the State of Alabama. In any case subject to this amendment,
33 the judge of probate shall possess the power and authority of
34 a circuit court judge trying the case and the case shall be
35 treated in all respects in the same manner as a case filed in
36 circuit court. The Alabama Rules of Civil Procedure shall
37 apply in the cases except as otherwise specifically provided
38 by law. This amendment is self-executing, but the Legislature
39 may enact additional laws to implement this amendment if
40 needed.
41 Upon ratification of this constitutional amendment, the
42 Code Commissioner shall number and place this amendment as
43 appropriate in the constitution omitting this instructional
44 paragraph and may make the following nonsubstantive revisions:
45 change capitalization, hierarchy, spelling, and punctuation
46 for purposes of style and uniformity; correct manifest
47 grammatical, clerical, and typographical errors; revise
48 internal or external citations and cross-references; harmonize
49 language; and translate effective dates.
50 END PROPOSED AMENDMENT
51 Section 2. An election upon the proposed amendment
52 shall be held in accordance with Sections 284, 284.01, and 285
53 of the Constitution of Alabama of 2022, and the election laws
54 of this state. The appropriate election official shall assign
55 a ballot number for the proposed constitutional amendment on
56 the election ballot and shall set forth the following
