63 (6) Two representatives of the Alabama Hospital
64 Association, appointed by the association.
65 (7) Two representatives of business, appointed by the
66 Business Council of Alabama.

69 appointed pursuant to subdivisions (a)(6) and (a)(7) shall

88 Montgomery, Alabama, but thereafter shall meet as necessary to

90 (3)(2) A quorum for a meeting of the board shall be
91 five members.
92 (4)(3) The board may meet by electronic means, so long
93 as there is a quorum of participating members.

103 "§40-18-557
104 (a) A taxpayer may claim a tax credit to offset
105 eligible taxes for each qualified donation as provided in this
106 section.
107 (1) For a donor who is a single individual, a head of
108 household, or married, filing a separate return: an amount
109 equal to 100 percent of the qualified donations the taxpayer
110 made during the tax year for which the credit is claimed, not
111 to exceed fifteen thousand dollars ($15,000).
112 (2) For a donor who is married, filing a joint return:

(2) For a donor who is married, filing a joint return:

113 100 percent of the qualified donations the taxpayer made
114 during the tax year for which the credit is claimed, not to
115 exceed thirty thousand dollars ($30,000).
116 (3)a. For a qualified donor that is taxed as an
117 electing pass-through entity under Section 40-18-24.4: 100
118 percent of the qualified donations the taxpayer made during
119 the tax year for which the credit is claimed, not to exceed
120 four hundred fifty thousand dollars ($450,000).
121 b. The tax credit shall be taken by the taxpayer on a
122 pro rata basis according to the percentage of ownership in the
123 entity or the limitations set forth in this section, whichever
124 is less. In the case of a donor that is taxed as a corporation
125 under the Internal Revenue Code: an amount equal to 100
126 percent of the qualified donations the taxpayer made during
127 the tax year for which the credit is claimed, or 75 percent of
128 the corporation's income, excise, or insurance premium tax,
129 not to exceed the amount of the corporation's income, excise,
130 or insurance premium tax liability, whichever is less, with a
131 contribution limit each tax year of five hundred thousand
132 dollars ($500,000).
133 (4)a. A credit for utility taxes may be claimed in an
134 amount equal to 100 percent of total qualified donations
135 during the taxable year for which the credit is claimed. In
136 order to claim a credit against utility tax payments levied in
137 Articles 3 and 4 of Chapter 21 of this title under this
138 subdivision, a taxpayer must have a utility tax direct pay
139 permit from the department pursuant to its requirements. A
140 taxpayer shall provide a copy of the utility tax direct pay
Page 5
taxpayer shall provide a copy of the utility tax direct pay

141 permit to each utility provider from whom the taxpayer
142 receives services. A taxpayer holding a direct pay permit
143 shall notify the department of each qualified donation
144 pursuant to the department's procedures. To the extent credits
145 are available, the department shall reserve credits for a
146 taxpayer and provide written confirmation thereof.
147 b. Upon receipt of a qualified donation, the recipient
148 shall submit to the department certification of the donation
149 received from a taxpayer under this subdivision pursuant to
150 procedures established by the department within 30 business
151 days after receipt of each donation. Upon receipt of the
152 certification from the recipient, the department shall provide
153 written confirmation to the taxpayer within 30 business days
154 that the reserved credits of the taxpayer in the amounts of
155 qualified donations actually made and not yet claimed are
156 eligible to be used on its monthly utility tax direct pay
157 return. A taxpayer may not use a credit against the utility
158 tax until such time as the department provides the
159 notification described in the previous sentence.
160 (b)(1) The total amount of the tax credit for a tax
161 year may not exceed the taxpayer's tax liability or, for
162 financial institutions, the state portion of the taxpayer's
163 financial institution excise tax liability.
164 (2) Any unused tax credit may be carried forward for up
165 to three years following the qualified donation but shall not
166 be transferable.
167 (c)(1) The tax credits may be claimed beginning January
168 1, 2026, for the 2026 tax year.

172 "§40-18-559
173 (a) The department shall adopt rules pursuant to the
174 Alabama Administrative Procedure Act on or before January 1,
175 2026, as necessary to administer and implement this article,
176 inclusive of the responsibility of the board. The rules shall
177 provide for all of the following:
178 (1) That the tax credits will not reduce the
179 distribution for the Alabama Special Mental Health Fund.
180 (2) That the tax credits shall be awarded based on the
181 order in which the credits are requested.
182 (b) The department shall prescribe the method by which
183 the tax credits are to be issued to eligible taxpayers."
184 Section 2. Section 40-18-560, Code of Alabama 1975,
185 relating to the rulemaking authority of the State Treasurer
186 and the Rural Hospital Investment Program Board, is repealed.
187 Section 3. This act shall become effective immediately.
