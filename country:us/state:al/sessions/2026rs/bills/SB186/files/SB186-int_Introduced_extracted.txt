5 Existing law authorizes the issuance of
6 distinctive motor vehicle license plates.
8 distinctive motor vehicle license plate to individuals
9 who live with invisible disabilities and to the primary
10 caregivers of those individuals.

13 A BILL
14 TO BE ENTITLED
15 AN ACT
16
17 Relating to motor vehicle registration and distinctive
18 license plates; to authorize the issuance of a distinctive
19 motor vehicle license plate to individuals who have certain
20 medical conditions and tp the primary caregivers of those
21 individuals; and to provide for the distribution of proceeds
22 from the license plate fee.
23 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
24 Section 1. (a) For purposes of this section, the term
25 "invisible disability" means a physical or mental condition
26 that may interfere with an individual's ability to communicate
27 with a first responder, including:
28 (1) A communication impediment;
Page 1
(1) A communication impediment;

29 (2) Hearing loss;
30 (3) Blindness or a visual impairment;
31 (4) Autism spectrum disorder;
32 (5) Traumatic brain injury;
33 (6) Schizophrenia;
34 (7) Alzheimer's disease or dementia;
35 (8) Post traumatic stress disorder;
36 (9) Epilepsy;
37 (10) A developmental disability;
38 (11) Down syndrome;
39 (12) Diabetes;
40 (13) A heart condition; or
41 (14) A cognitive disability.
42 (b) Notwithstanding Sections 32-6-64, 32-6-65, 32-6-67,
43 and 32-6-68, Code of Alabama 1975, an owner of a motor vehicle
44 who is a resident of Alabama and who has been diagnosed with
45 an invisible disability or an individual who is the primary
46 caretaker of an individual who has been diagnosed with an
47 invisible disability shall be issued a distinctive "Invisible
48 Disability" license plate upon:
49 (1) Application to the judge of probate, license
50 commissioner, or other issuing official;
51 (2) Compliance with motor vehicle registration and
52 licensing laws;
53 (3) Payment of regular fees required by law for license
54 plates for private passenger or pleasure motor vehicles; and
55 (4) Payment of an additional annual fee of forty
56 dollars ($40).

dollars ($40).

57 (c) To receive an "Invisible Disability" license plate,
58 the individual shall provide proof of the diagnosis from a
59 physician licensed to practice medicine in this state.
60 (d) The "Invisible Disability" license plates shall be
61 valid for five years and may then be replaced with either
62 conventional, personalized, or new "Invisible Disability"
63 license plates.
64 (e) Payment of required license fees and taxes for the
65 years during which a new plate is not issued shall be
66 evidenced as provided in Section 32-6-63, Code of Alabama
67 1975.
68 (f) Kulturecity, Incorporated shall be responsible for
69 the design of the distinctive license plate subject to
70 approval by the Commissioner of Revenue and compliance with
71 all laws and rules.
72 (g) Proceeds from the additional revenues generated by
73 the forty dollars ($40) minus the production and
74 administrative costs for the "Invisible Disability" license
75 plates shall be submitted monthly by the Department of Revenue
76 or other appropriate official to Kulturecity, Incorporated.
77 The proceeds shall be used to assist in accomplishing its goal
78 of creating sensory accessibility and inclusion for
79 individuals with invisible disabilities within the state.
80 (h) The Department of Revenue may adopt rules to
81 implement this section.
82 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
83 1, 2026.
