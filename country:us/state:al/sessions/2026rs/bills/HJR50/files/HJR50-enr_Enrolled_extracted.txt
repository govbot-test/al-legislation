HJR50 Enrolled
2
3 COMMENDING TAIWAN FOR ITS RELATIONS WITH THE UNITED STATES
4 AND THE STATE OF ALABAMA.
5
6 WHEREAS, Taiwan and the United States of America are
7 longstanding, close allies who both deeply cherish the
8 common values of democracy, peace, and prosperity; Taiwan
9 continues to pursue integrated diplomacy and serves the best
10 interests of the Indo-Pacific region by deepening ties with
11 other like-minded democratic nations, safeguarding regional
12 security, and utilizing Taiwan's technological and
13 industrial success to promote partnerships on mutual
14 benefits; and
15 WHEREAS, the year 2026 marks both the 250th
16 Anniversary of United States Independence and the 30th
17 Anniversary of Taiwan's first direct presidential election,
18 milestones that honor liberty and the principle that
19 sovereignty resides in the people; this shared celebration
20 reaffirms Taiwan–U.S. commitment to the rule of law, human
21 rights, and a rules-based international order; through close
22 cooperation, Taiwan and the United States strengthen
23 democratic values and contribute to Indo-Pacific security,
24 economic resilience, and global peace and stability for
25 future generations; and
26 WHEREAS, the People's Republic of China repeatedly
27 exerts economic coercion, stages military drills directed
28 toward Taiwan, and distorts UN Resolution 2758 (XXVI)
Page 1
toward Taiwan, and distorts UN Resolution 2758 (XXVI)
HJR50 Enrolled
29 excluding Taiwan's participation from international
30 organizations, which results in global challenges to
31 rules-based international order and aggressive authoritarian
32 expansionism; and
33 WHEREAS, Taiwan is the United States' seventh-largest
34 trading partner, and the United States is Taiwan's
35 second-largest trading partner, with an estimated $157.8
36 billion trade-in-goods value; also, Taiwan was Alabama's
37 17th largest import and 11th largest export market in 2024;
38 the agreement implemented under the United States-Taiwan
39 Initiative on 21st-Century Trade First Agreement
40 Implementation Act includes commitments on anti-corruption,
41 good regulatory practices, customs administration and trade
42 facilitation, and small and medium-sized enterprises; and
43 WHEREAS, in 2022, Alabama joined the U.S. Taiwan
44 Education Initiative by signing a Memorandum of
45 Understanding with Taiwan's Ministry of Education to boost
46 educational cooperation, Mandarin and English language
47 learning, and student and teacher exchanges; the operation
48 of the "Taiwan Center" at Auburn University further
49 emphasizes our common goals; Alabama is the second state to
50 formalize this K-12 and higher educational partnership under
51 the broad U.S.-Taiwan Education Initiative launched in 2020;
52 now therefore,
53 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
54 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That we support continued
55 dialogue under the "U.S.-Taiwan Initiative on 21st-Century
56 Trade" and a mechanism to avoid a U.S.-Taiwan double
Page 2
Trade" and a mechanism to avoid a U.S.-Taiwan double
HJR50 Enrolled
57 taxation, as Taiwanese companies are seeking diversified
58 investment opportunities in the United States within a free
59 and open market; and we encourage the state government to
60 send a trade delegation to Taiwan, and for state executives
61 and officials to further engage with their counterparts in
62 Taiwan to explore, enhance, and deepen substantive
63 relationship on issues of importance to the state.
64 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That in celebrating Taiwan's
65 democracy and reaffirming the United States' commitment to
66 Taiwan, the members of this body are encouraged to join the
67 Taiwan Caucus to explore additional collaboration with
68 Taiwan; to support Taiwan's meaningful participation in
69 international organizations and economic cooperation
70 frameworks, including ICAO, INTERPOL, CPTPP, and IPEF; and
71 to bolster Taiwan's stance in resisting China's oppression
72 and its misrepresentation of United Nations General Assembly
73 Resolution 2758.
74 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the Alabama government
75 is encouraged to sign an Cultural, Educational, and Economic
76 Cooperation Memorandum of Understanding with Taiwan; that
77 the members of the Legislature are encouraged to join the
78 Taiwan Caucus for exploring more collaboration opportunities
79 with Taiwan; and Alabamians are encouraged to take part in
80 activities, experiencing and promoting Taiwan's cultures,
81 cuisine, and tourism.
82 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That a certified copy of this
83 Resolution be prepared and transmitted to Governor Kay Ivey,
84 Secretary of State Wes Allen, the Alabama Congressional

Secretary of State Wes Allen, the Alabama Congressional

85 Delegation, and Mr. Jared Lin, Director-General of the
