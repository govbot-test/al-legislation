7 WHEREAS, Taiwan and the United States of America are
8 longstanding, close allies who both deeply cherish the
9 common values of democracy, peace, and prosperity; Taiwan
10 continues to pursue integrated diplomacy and serves the best
11 interests of the Indo-Pacific region by deepening ties with
12 other like-minded democratic nations, safeguarding regional
13 security, and utilizing Taiwan's technological and
14 industrial success to promote partnerships on mutual
15 benefits; and
16 WHEREAS, the year 2026 marks both the 250th
17 Anniversary of United States Independence and the 30th
18 Anniversary of Taiwan's first direct presidential election,
19 milestones that honor liberty and the principle that
20 sovereignty resides in the people; this shared celebration
21 reaffirms Taiwan–U.S. commitment to the rule of law, human
22 rights, and a rules-based international order; through close
23 cooperation, Taiwan and the United States strengthen
24 democratic values and contribute to Indo-Pacific security,
25 economic resilience, and global peace and stability for
26 future generations; and
27 WHEREAS, the People's Republic of China repeatedly
28 exerts economic coercion, stages military drills directed

34 WHEREAS, Taiwan is the United States' seventh-largest

78 the members of the Legislature are encouraged to join the
79 Taiwan Caucus for exploring more collaboration opportunities
80 with Taiwan; and Alabamians are encouraged to take part in
81 activities, experiencing and promoting Taiwan's cultures,
82 cuisine, and tourism.
