3 ENCOURAGING THE STATE OF ALABAMA TO STUDY NAVIGATIONAL AIDS
4 WITHIN THE WATERWAYS OF MOBILE BAY AND SURROUNDING WATERS.
5
6 WHEREAS, the waters of Baldwin County between Mobile
7 Bay, Weeks Bay, and on to the Magnolia River are frequently
8 traveled by recreational and commercial vessels; and
9 WHEREAS, navigational aids such as buoys and markers
10 assist boaters in identifying safe channels, avoiding
11 hazards, and navigating safely; and
12 WHEREAS, boaters traveling from Mobile Bay through
13 Weeks Bay to the Magnolia River have reported that
14 navigational aids in portions of these waters, particularly
15 from the federally maintained navigation aids in Mobile Bay
16 to the Magnolia River entrance channel, are sparse or
17 nonexistent, creating potential safety concerns; and
18 WHEREAS, Baldwin County's coastal waterways support
19 significant recreational boating activity and tourism,
20 making safe navigation important for the protection of life,
21 property, and local economic activity; and
22 WHEREAS, responsibility for marine patrol enforcement
23 in Alabama waters was transferred in 2015 from the Marine
24 Patrol Division of the Alabama Department of Conservation
25 and Natural Resources to the Marine Patrol Division of the
26 Alabama State Law Enforcement Agency, and Alabama
27 regulations governing public waters have historically
28 referenced the authority of the Director of the Marine

29 Patrol Division to adopt rules relating to boating safety
30 and regulatory markers on the waters of the state; and
31 WHEREAS, the safe navigation of Alabama's coastal
32 waterways is important to the protection of life and
33 property and to the continued enjoyment and responsible use
34 of these waters by residents and visitors alike; and
35 WHEREAS, some coastal states, including Florida and
36 Texas, have utilized state-administered waterway marker
37 programs in certain areas to complement federal navigation
38 aids in waters heavily used by recreational boaters; now
39 therefore,
40 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
41 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That we hereby recognize the
42 potential public safety concern associated with the lack of
43 navigational aids in the waters between Mobile Bay, Weeks
44 Bay, and on to the Magnolia River.
45 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That the Secretary of the
46 Alabama State Law Enforcement Agency, in coordination with
47 the Alabama Department of Conservation and Natural
48 Resources, the United States Coast Guard, the State
49 Department of Transportation, and other appropriate local or
50 regional authorities, is requested to examine navigational
51 aids within these waterways and determine which governmental
52 entity is responsible for their placement and maintenance.
53 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That within six months of the
54 adoption of this resolution, the Secretary of the Alabama
55 State Law Enforcement Agency shall provide a written report
56 to the Legislature identifying the following:
