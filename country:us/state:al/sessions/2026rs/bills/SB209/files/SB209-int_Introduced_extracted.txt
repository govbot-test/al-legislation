4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, any program or curriculum in
7 human reproductive process must emphasize abstinence as
8 the only effective protection against unintended
9 pregnancy and sexually transmitted disease.
10 This bill would require any sex education or
11 human reproductive curriculum or program in a public
12 K-12 school to exclusively teach sexual risk avoidance
13 and encourage abstinence from all sexual activity, and
14 would prohibit any sex education instruction for
15 students in kindergarten through fourth grade.
16 This bill would further provide for the sex
17 education and human reproductive curriculum or program
18 in public K-12 schools, including the provision of
19 information about state laws relating to the financial
20 cost of pregnancy and child care, abortion, and
21 adoption, and instruction about parenting
22 responsibilities.
23 This bill would prohibit any sex education or
24 human reproductive curriculum or program from: (i)
25 providing a referral to or information about how to
26 acquire an abortion or contraception; (ii)
27 misrepresenting the efficacy of or demonstrating the
28 use of contraceptives; and (iii) using images that are
Page 1
use of contraceptives; and (iii) using images that are
SB209 INTRODUCED
29 sexually explicit.
30 This bill would prohibit any local board of
31 education from using the services of any individual or
32 organization that does not endorse sexual risk
33 avoidance or that advocates for or performs abortions.
34 This bill would require each parent or guardian
35 to be provided with notice before sex education or
36 information about the human reproductive process is
37 provided to students, and would provide each parent or
38 guardian with the ability to opt his or her child out
39 of the curriculum or program.
40 This bill would also allow the Attorney General
41 to enforce the requirements of this bill.
