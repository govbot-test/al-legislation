4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, relating to criminal
7 "defendant" includes individuals in a criminal case who
8 have been found not guilty by reason of insanity or not
9 guilty by reason of mental disease or defect.
10 This bill would further define "defendant" to
11 include an individual who the court has determined is
12 incompetent to stand trial and who is not restorable to
13 competency.
14 This bill would provide that the Department of
15 Mental Health shall not provide custody, care, and
16 treatment for longer than the maximum sentence a
17 defendant could receive for the crime for which he or
18 she is convicted, with exceptions for a defendant
19 charged with capital murder or who otherwise continues
20 to meet civil inpatient commitment criteria.
21 This bill would also make nonsubstantive,
22 technical revisions to update the existing code
23 language to current style.

40 The following definitions shall apply to this
41 articleFor the purposes of this article, the following terms
42 have the following meanings:
43 (1) COURT. The court whichthat committed the defendant
44 pursuant to Section 15-16-43.
45 (2) DEFENDANT. A defendant in a criminal case who has
46 been found not guilty by reason of insanity, or not guilty by
47 reason of mental disease or defect, or is determined by the
48 court to be incompetent to proceed to trial or not restorable
49 to competency within a reasonable time, and has been committed
50 to the custody of the Alabama State Department of Mental
51 Health and Mental Retardation or another facility as provided
52 by Section 15-16-43.
53 (3) DEPARTMENT. The State Department of Mental Health
54 and Mental Retardation.
55 (4) DISTRICT ATTORNEY. The district attorney for the

62 (a) If at any time after a defendant has been

65 not cause the defendant to pose a real and present threat of

78 11 and Rule 25 of the Alabama Rules of Criminal Procedure,
