4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, a trustee can only adjust
7 income if the terms of the trust explicitly grant the
8 trustee that authority.
9 This bill would permit trustees to adjust
10 receipts and disbursements between principal and income
11 without requiring the trust terms to authorize the
12 adjustments, aligning current law with the Uniform
13 Principal and Income Act.
14 This bill would also make nonsubstantive,
15 technical revisions to update the existing code
16 language to current style.

21 AN ACT

23 Relating to trusts; to amend Section 19-3A-104, Code of
24 Alabama 1975, to permit trustees to adjust trust receipts and
25 disbursements between principal and income without express
26 authority of the terms of the trust instrument; and to make
27 nonsubstantive, technical revisions to update the existing
28 code language to current style.

43 to comply with Section 19-3A-103(b).

51 (3) The identity and circumstances of the

54 (5) The regularity of income to the trust;
55 (6) The need for preservation and appreciation of

110 (7) If the trustee is not a beneficiary, but the

117 (d) If subsection subdivision (c)(5), subsection

119 trustee and there is more than one trustee, then the

124 (e) A trustee may release the entire power conferred by

129 subsection subdivisions (c)(1) through subsection (c)(7) or if
130 the trustee determines that possessing or exercising the power
131 will or may deprive the trust of a tax benefit or impose a tax
132 burden not described in subsection (c). The release may be
133 permanent or for a specified period, including a period
134 measured by the life of an individual.
135 (f) The trustee or any beneficiary of a trust covered
136 by this chapter (or a trust to be created from a decedent's
137 estate that is covered by this chapter) (1) may seek approval
138 from a court of competent jurisdiction to be governed
139 prospectively by this section, or (2) may at any time
140 affirmatively elect to be governed prospectively by this
