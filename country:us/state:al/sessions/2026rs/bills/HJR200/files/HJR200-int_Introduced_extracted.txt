4 HJR___ HONORING SIGMA GAMMA RHO SORORITY DAY AT THE CAPITOL
5 ON APRIL 2, 2026.
6
7 WHEREAS, Sigma Gamma Rho Sorority, Incorporated, was
8 founded on November 12, 1922, on the campus of Butler
9 University in Indianapolis, Indiana, by seven visionary
10 educators who sought to establish an organization rooted in
11 scholarship, sisterhood, and service; and
12 WHEREAS, Sigma Gamma Rho Sorority, Incorporated, has
13 grown into an international public service organization with
14 chapters across the United States, the Caribbean, Europe,
15 and beyond, impacting countless communities through
16 structured service initiatives and advocacy; and
17 WHEREAS, the sorority's guiding principles of
18 Scholarship, Service, Sisterhood, and Womanhood have shaped
19 generations of women leaders who have excelled in education,
20 public service, business, health care, the arts, government,
21 and civic engagement; and
22 WHEREAS, Sigma Gamma Rho Sorority, Incorporated, has
23 consistently addressed critical social issues through
24 signature programs including, but not limited to, youth
25 development, education advocacy, health equity, economic
26 empowerment, voter engagement, and social justice
27 initiatives; and
28 WHEREAS, chapters within Alabama have demonstrated
Page 1
WHEREAS, chapters within Alabama have demonstrated
HJR200 INTRODUCED
29 exceptional commitment to the residents of this state
30 through mentoring programs, scholarships, community service
31 projects, partnerships with local organizations, and
32 initiatives that promote equity, wellness, and civic
33 responsibility; and
34 WHEREAS, the enduring legacy of Sigma Gamma Rho
35 Sorority, Incorporated, reflects a steadfast dedication to
36 "Greater Service, Greater Progress," uplifting communities
37 and advancing opportunities for individuals and families
38 across this state and the nation; now therefore,
39 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
40 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That on April 2, 2026, we hereby
41 celebrate Sigma Gamma Rho Sorority Day at the Capitol, and
42 we express sincere appreciation for its outstanding
43 contributions, historic legacy, and commitment to the
44 residents of this state through mentoring programs,
45 scholarships, community service projects, partnerships with
46 local organizations, and initiatives that promote equity,
47 wellness, and civic responsibility.
