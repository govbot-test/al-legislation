4 Relating to retired state employees; to amend Section
5 36-27-8.2, as last amended by Act 2025-409, Code of Alabama
6 1975, to further provide for employment authorized for certain
7 retirees of the Employees' Retirement System or Teachers'
8 Retirement System.
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
10 Section 1. Section 36-27-8.2, as last amended by Act
11 2025-409, 2025 Regular Session, Code of Alabama 1975, is
12 amended to read as follows:
13 "§36-27-8.2
14 (a) Any individual who is retired under the Employees'
15 Retirement System may perform duties in any capacity,
16 including as an independent contractor, with any employer
17 participating in the Employees' Retirement System or the
18 Teachers' Retirement System without suspension of his or her
19 retirement allowance provided that: (i) the individual is not
20 employed in a permanent full-time capacity; and (ii) the
21 individual's compensation from the employer in calendar year
22 2016 does not exceed thirty thousand dollars ($30,000).
23 Beginning in calendar year 2017, and each calendar year
24 thereafter, the annual earning limit shall be increased by the
25 same percentage increase as the increase in the Consumer Price
26 Index for all urban consumers as published by the U.S.
27 Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics. Any increase
28 in the annual earning limit shall be rounded to the next

30 amount in excess of the one thousand dollar ($1,000) multiple
31 considered in determining the increase for the following year.
32 Each adjustment shall be based on the increase in the index
33 for the preceding 12-month period ending on September 30 and
34 the increase shall be effective for the following calendar
35 year.
36 (b) Any individual serving as an elected official who
37 has retired from the Employees' Retirement System may serve
38 for his or her full compensation in an elected public office
39 with the state, a county, or an incorporated municipality
40 without suspension of retirement benefits;, provided, that
41 under no circumstances shall such an individual participate in
42 or accrue additional benefits under the Teachers' Retirement
43 System or the Employees' Retirement System for that service,
44 and provided that under no circumstances shall an individual
45 whose retirement is based upon service as an elected official
46 continue in or return to the office and receive both pension
47 benefits and salary. Any provision of this subsection to the
48 contrary notwithstanding, a retiree serving as a local elected
49 superintendent of education shall not receive both pension
50 benefits and salary unless his or her compensation as a local
51 elected superintendent of education does not exceed the annual
52 earning limits provided in subsection (a).
53 (c) The responsibility for compliance with this section
54 is placed upon the employing authority, and each retiree
55 performing duties under this section shall certify to the
56 employer any information required in order to carry out this
