17 TO BE ENTITLED
18 AN ACT
19
20 Relating to the State Employees' Retirement System; to
21 amend Section 36-27-8.2, Code of Alabama 1975, as last amended
22 by Act 2025-409, 2025 Regular Session; to authorize certain
23 retirees to return to work as bus drivers, subject to certain
24 conditions.
25 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
26 Section 1. Section 36-27-8.2, Code of Alabama 1975, as
27 last amended by Act 2025-409, 2025 Regular Session, is amended
28 to read as follows:
Page 1
to read as follows:
HB138 INTRODUCED
29 "§36-27-8.2
30 (a) Any individual who is retired under the Employees'
31 Retirement System may perform duties in any capacity,
32 including as an independent contractor, with any employer
33 participating in the Employees' Retirement System or the
34 Teachers' Retirement System without suspension of his or her
35 retirement allowance provided that: (i) the individual is not
36 employed in a permanent full-time capacity; and (ii) the
37 individual's compensation from the employer in calendar year
38 2016 does not exceed thirty thousand dollars ($30,000).
39 Beginning in calendar year 2017, and each calendar year
40 thereafter, the annual earning limit shall be increased by the
41 same percentage increase as the increase in the Consumer Price
42 Index for all urban consumers as published by the U.S.
43 Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics. Any increase
44 in the annual earning limit shall be rounded to the next
45 lowest multiple of one thousand dollars ($1,000) with any
46 amount in excess of the one thousand dollar ($1,000) multiple
47 considered in determining the increase for the following year.
48 Each adjustment shall be based on the increase in the index
49 for the preceding 12-month period ending on September 30 and
50 the increase shall be effective for the following calendar
51 year.
52 (b) Any individual serving as an elected official who
53 has retired from the Employees' Retirement System may serve
54 for his or her full compensation in an elected public office
55 with the state, a county, or an incorporated municipality
56 without suspension of retirement benefits; provided, that
Page 2
without suspension of retirement benefits; provided, that
HB138 INTRODUCED
57 under no circumstances shall such an individual participate in
58 or accrue additional benefits under the Teachers' Retirement
59 System or the Employees' Retirement System for that service,
60 and provided that under no circumstances shall an individual
61 whose retirement is based upon service as an elected official
62 continue in or return to the office and receive both pension
63 benefits and salary. Any provision of this subsection to the
64 contrary notwithstanding, a retiree serving as a local elected
65 superintendent of education shall not receive both pension
66 benefits and salary unless his or her compensation as a local
67 elected superintendent of education does not exceed the annual
68 earning limits provided in subsection (a).
69 (c) The responsibility for compliance with this section
70 is placed upon the employing authority, and each retiree
71 performing duties under this section shall certify to the
72 employer any information required in order to carry out this
73 section. The retiree shall provide written notice of the
74 postretirement employment under this section to the Employees'
75 Retirement System and employing authority within 30 days after
76 the date the retiree knows or should know that he or she will
77 be performing duties on a full-time or permanent basis or will
78 earn an amount in excess of the annual earning limit under
79 this section.
80 (d)(1) Any individual who is retired under the
81 Employees' Retirement System or the Teachers' Retirement
82 System, has the applicable Alabama Peace Officers' Standards
83 and Training certification, and was classified as either a law
84 enforcement officer, as defined in Section 36-27-59, or a
Page 3
enforcement officer, as defined in Section 36-27-59, or a
HB138 INTRODUCED
85 state policeman, as defined in Section 36-27-1, prior to
86 retirement, may perform duties as a school resource officer,
87 as defined in Section 16-1-44.1, a correctional officer with
88 the Department of Corrections, or a police officer at any
89 state college or university pursuant to Section 16-22-1
90 without suspension of his or her retirement allowance,
91 provided that the compensation earned does not exceed
92 fifty-two thousand dollars ($52,000) per year.
93 (2) Any individual who is retired under the Employees'
94 Retirement System, has the applicable Alabama Peace Officers'
95 Standards and Training certification, and meets any of the
96 following criteria may perform duties as a correctional
97 officer with the Department of Corrections without suspension
98 of his or her retirement allowance, provided that the
99 compensation earned does not exceed fifty-two thousand dollars
100 ($52,000) per year:
101 a. Was classified as a law enforcement officer, as
102 defined in Section 36-27-59, prior to retirement.
103 b. Was classified as a state policeman, as defined in
104 Section 36-27-1, prior to retirement.
105 c. Was classified as a correctional officer, as defined
106 in Section 36-27-59, prior to retirement, and retired pursuant
107 to Section 36-27-16(a)(1)c.
108 (3) Any individual who is retired under the Employees'
109 Retirement System, who has both the applicable Alabama
110 Firefighters' Personnel Standards and Education Commission
111 certification and the applicable State Board of Health
112 certification as a firefighter and an emergency medical
Page 4
certification as a firefighter and an emergency medical
HB138 INTRODUCED
113 services personnel, as defined in Section 36-27-59, prior to
114 retirement, may perform duties as a firemedic without
115 suspension of his or her retirement allowance provided that
116 the compensation earned does not exceed fifty-two thousand
117 dollars ($52,000) per year, commensurate with the work
118 actually performed.
119 (4) This subsection shall have retroactive application
120 to January 1, 2023, and no individual may be employed pursuant
121 to this subsection after December 31, 2030.
122 (e)(1) Any individual who is retired under the
123 Employees' Retirement System or the Teachers' Retirement
124 System may be hired and perform duties as a full-time bus
125 driver without suspension of his or her retirement allowance,
126 provided that: (i) the individual's compensation does not
127 exceed the earnings limit established in subsection (a); and
128 (ii) the individual has drawn his or her retirement benefit
129 for 12 consecutive months.
130 (2) Notwithstanding subdivision (1), any individual who
131 retired under the Employees' Retirement System or the
132 Teachers' Retirement System on or before January 1, 2026, may
133 be hired and perform duties as a full-time bus driver even if
134 the individual has not drawn his or her retirement benefits
135 for 12 consecutive months, provided all other conditions of
136 this subsection are met.
137 (3) No individual may be employed pursuant to this
138 subsection after December 31, 2030."
139 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
140 1, 2026.
