4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, the State Superintendent of
6 Education is responsible for the development and
7 implementation of a school grading system.
8 This bill would provide for the creation and
9 membership of an Accountability Council to review and
10 recommend revisions to the state accountability system.
11 This bill would also require the Alabama
12 Workforce Board, with support from the State Department
13 of Education and the Alabama Commission on Higher
14 Education, to develop a system for collecting,
15 analyzing, and reporting on student performance data
16 associated with completion of college and career
17 readiness indicators.

32 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
33 Section 1. (a) To further the intent of the Legislature
34 to create transparency and ensure all agencies uphold the
35 purposes of this section, on or before July 1, 2026, a
36 standing Accountability Council shall be created. The goal of
37 the Accountability Council is to ensure that the state's
38 educational accountability system supports improved outcomes
39 and long-term success for all Alabama students, with a focus
40 on closing achievement gaps.
41 (b) The Chairs of the House of Representatives and
42 Senate Education Policy Committees shall convene the
43 organizational meeting of the Accountability Council.
44 Thereafter, the Accountability Council shall meet at least
45 annually to accomplish all of the following:
46 (1) Critically review the current state report card
47 provided in Section 16-6C-2, Code of Alabama 1975, and
48 research other state report cards for best practices for
49 improving student achievement.
50 (2) Guide the relevant state agencies and experts to
51 design an annual return on investment study as provided in
52 Section 2.
53 (3) Critically review the annual return on investment
54 study data to determine if college and career readiness
55 indicators are preparing students to be successful after
56 graduation.
