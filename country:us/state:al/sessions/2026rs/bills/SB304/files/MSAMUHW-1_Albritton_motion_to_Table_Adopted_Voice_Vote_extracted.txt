Section 1:
MSAMUHW-1 02/17/2026 GED (L)ma 2026-853 SUB SB304
OFFERED BY SENATOR ELLIOTT
1
2
3
4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Existing law authorizes municipalities to levy a
6 business license on certain businesses operating within
7 their jurisdictions.
8 This bill would provide for the circumstances
9 when a municipality may levy a business license on
10 certain businesses in the building trade located inside
11 the municipality's jurisdiction.
12
13
14 A BILL
15 TO BE ENTITLED
16 AN ACT
17
18 Relating to municipalities; to add Section 11-51-133 to
19 the Code of Alabama 1975, to limit municipalities from levying
20 a business license on certain businesses.
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
22 Section 1. Section 11-51-133 is added to the Code of
23 Alabama 1975, to read as follows:
24 §11-51-133
25 (a) As used in this section, the following terms have
26 the following meanings:
27 (1) CERTIFIED HEATING AND AIR CONDITIONING CONTRACTOR.
28 The same meaning as provided in Section 34-31-18.
Page 1
The same meaning as provided in Section 34-31-18.
29 (2) CERTIFIED REFRIGERATION CONTRACTOR. The same
30 meaning as provided in Section 34-31-18.
31 (3) DUCT AIR TIGHTNESS TESTING CONTRACTOR. The same
32 meaning as provided in Section 34-31-18.
33 (4) ELECTRICAL CONTRACTOR. The same meaning as provided
34 in Section 34-36-3.
35 (5) GENERAL CONTRACTOR. The same meaning as provided in
36 Section 34-8-1, without regard to the monetary thresholds set
37 forth in that section.
38 (6) JOURNEYMAN ELECTRICIAN. The same meaning as
39 provided in Section 34-36-3.
40 (7) MASTER GAS FITTER. The same meaning as provided in
41 Section 34-37-1.
42 (8) MASTER PLUMBER. The same meaning as provided in
43 Section 34-37-1.
44 (9) ONSITE WASTEWATER LICENSEE. A qualified individual
45 holding a license issued by the Alabama Onsite Wastewater
46 Board.
47 (10) PHYSICAL BUSINESS LOCATION. A principal business
48 office, branch office, or other office, warehouse, or
49 permanent fixed place of business. The term does not include a
50 temporary job site, job trailer, equipment, or materials
51 stored on a temporary basis in connection with a construction
52 project or job.
53 (11) RESIDENTIAL HOME BUILDER. The same meaning as
54 provided in Section 34-14A-2, without regard to the monetary
55 thresholds set forth in that section.
56 (12) SUBCONTRACTOR. The same meaning as provided in
Page 2
(12) SUBCONTRACTOR. The same meaning as provided in
57 Section 34-8-1, without regard to the monetary thresholds set
58 forth in that section.
59 (b)(1) A municipality may impose a business license on
60 any of the following businesses who perform work within the
61 municipality but do not maintain a physical business location
62 within the municipality:
63 a. A certified heating and air conditioning contractor.
64 b. A certified refrigeration contractor.
65 c. A duct air tightness testing contractor.
66 d. An electrical contractor.
67 e. A general contractor
68 f. A journeyman electrician.
69 g. A master gas fitter.
70 h. A master plumber.
71 i. An onsite wastewater licensee.
72 j. A residential home builder.
73 k. A subcontractor.
74 (2) The amount of the business license shall be
75 calculated on the basis of a flat fee or the gross receipts
76 derived from the work performed within the municipality.
77 (c) For purpose of calculating the amount of a business
78 license, a municipality shall permit any business listed in
79 subsection (b) to exclude any gross receipts which were used
80 to calculate the amount of a business license paid in another
81 municipality when the business maintains a physical place of
82 business in the municipality where the application for a
83 business license has been filed.
84 (d) A municipality may not require any business listed
Page 3
(d) A municipality may not require any business listed
85 in subsection (b) to obtain a municipal business license to
86 work on a project on behalf of a county government when that
87 project is located on county property or on a
88 county-maintained road, bridge, right-of-way, or easement.
89 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
90 1, 2026.
