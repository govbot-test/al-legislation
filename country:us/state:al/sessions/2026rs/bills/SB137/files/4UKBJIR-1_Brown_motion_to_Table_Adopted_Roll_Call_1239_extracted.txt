9 To create the Alabama Resilience Council as an
10 appointed advisory group within the executive branch of state
11 government, and to provide for the rules, duties, and
12 procedures of the council; to authorize appointment of, and
13 provide for the responsibilities of, a Chief Resilience
14 Officer; to require a statewide resilience plan that includes
15 a statewide comprehensive risk and vulnerability assessment;
16 and to amend Section 27-2-39, Code of Alabama 1975, to revise
17 the distribution of insurance license fees to provide funding
18 for the council.

20 Section 1. The Legislature finds and declares the
21 following:
22 (a) Alabama is susceptible to various natural and
23 manmade disaster events, posing threats to the safety of its
24 citizens, integrity of its infrastructure, prospects for its
25 economic growth, and maintenance and protection of its unique
26 natural resources. These threats also pose significant fiscal
27 and operational risks to functions of the state and local
28 government.

29 (b) For the purposes of this Act, the term “resilience”
30 shall be considered the capacity of individuals, communities,
31 businesses, and systems to anticipate, prepare for, adapt to,
32 and rapidly recover from adverse events. For the purposes of
33 this Act, resilience shall be guided by five principles:
34 individual responsibility; collective continuity and function;
35 factual assessment of risk; integrated resilience efforts; and
36 focused support.
37 (c) The Alabama Resilience Council established by
38 Executive Order No. 736, May 5, 2023, has examined resilience
39 policy needs to address current and future adverse events, and
40 based on its findings, Alabama would benefit from a lasting,
41 comprehensive approach to resilience.
42 (d) Coordinated statewide resilience efforts and
43 development of a statewide resilience plan would be most
44 effective under a single designated Chief Resilience Officer
45 to coordinate activities of the Alabama Resilience Council.

50 coordinating resilience-related activities of state government

87 shall be as follows:
88 (a) Develop strategies to guide statewide resilience
89 efforts by prioritizing objectives reflecting pressing
90 resilience challenges. To this end, the council shall
91 regularly convene government and private-sector stakeholders
92 with different perspectives and interests to facilitate

94 cascading failures. The council shall develop principles to

107 management alternatives by reducing the complexity of risk

119 and maintenance of the state hazard mitigation plan, as
120 contemplated by the federal Disaster Mitigation Act of 2000,
121 42 U.S.C § 5121, et seq., or successor federal statute;
122 provided that the Alabama Emergency Management Agency shall be
123 responsible for administrative maintenance and day-to-day
124 execution of the plan.
125 (g) Identify and pursue funding opportunities to
126 promote resilience in the State of Alabama.

135 the council may establish working groups or subcommittees.

179 develop, maintain, and implement a statewide resilience plan,
180 with the general oversight and input from the council. The
181 plan shall include the following components:
182 (1) Statewide Comprehensive Risk and Vulnerability
183 Assessment. The plan shall evaluate the impacts of extreme
184 weather and disasters; assess current and future risks across
185 various hazards using historical data and future scenarios
186 while factoring in land and water management practices; and
187 examine effects on communities, infrastructure, the economy,
188 and the environment. Additionally, a vulnerability assessment
189 shall identify the social, economic, and environmental systems
190 most at risk. It shall analyze risk exposure, potential costs,
191 and impacts on state assets, government functions, local
192 economies, and critical natural systems.
193 (2) Resilience Actions. The plan shall provide a
194 prioritized list of resilience actions to address
195 vulnerabilities, detailing their benefits, costs, and
196 implementation timeline. Priority shall be given first to
