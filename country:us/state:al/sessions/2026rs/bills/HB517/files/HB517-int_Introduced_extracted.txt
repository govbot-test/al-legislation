4 SYNOPSIS:
5 This bill would establish the Talent Readiness
6 and Industry Needs (TRAIN) Act to authorize businesses
7 to partner with eligible educational institutions to
8 provide career and technical education instruction.
9 This bill would incentivize employers to loan
10 qualified employees to serve as instructors in career
11 and technical education programs by creating a tax
12 credit to offset their income, financial institution
13 excise, and utility tax liability.
14 This bill would establish a workforce teaching
15 certificate and a streamlined process for qualified
16 employees to obtain a certificate to teach a designated
17 career and technical education program at an eligible
18 educational institution.

39 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
40 Section 1. This act shall be known and may be cited as
41 the Talent Readiness and Industry Needs (TRAIN) Act.
42 Section 2. The Legislature finds that Career and
43 Technical Education (CTE) plays a vital role in preparing
44 students for in-demand careers across a range of industries
45 including manufacturing, information technology, construction,
46 and skilled trades. Many school districts across the state
47 face challenges in recruiting and retaining qualified CTE
48 instructors, due to certification barriers, regional
49 shortages, and competition with private-sector wages. The
50 involvement of current industry professionals in the classroom
51 could provide students with direct exposure to up-to-date,
52 real-world knowledge and practices, thereby improving
53 educational outcomes and workforce readiness. The Legislature
54 also finds that public-private partnerships between schools
55 and industry are essential to closing the skills gap and
56 ensuring that education aligns with the evolving needs of the

58 private sector to partner with eligible educational

60 provide for industry professionals to obtain CTE
61 certification. Therefore, it is the intent of the Legislature
62 to establish a targeted income tax credit to incentivize
63 employers to loan qualified employees to serve as instructors
64 in CTE programs at eligible educational institutions in order
65 to promote workforce development and economic growth.
