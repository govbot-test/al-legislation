3 COMMENDING SANDRA THOMPSON FOR HER OUTSTANDING
4 LEADERSHIP AS DIRECTOR OF THE ALABAMA VETERANS MUSEUM AND
5 ARCHIVES.
6
7 WHEREAS, it is with profound appreciation and deepest
8 admiration that we recognize Sandra "Sandy" Thompson for 17
9 years of steadfast service and exemplary leadership as
10 Director of the Alabama Veterans Museum and Archives; and
11 WHEREAS, open in Athens since 2000, the Alabama
12 Veterans Museum and Archives (AVMA) is dedicated to the
13 memory of veterans from Alabama who served in all branches
14 of the United States Armed Forces; and
15 WHEREAS, due to Sandy's calm, innovative, and highly
16 efficient guidance, the past 17 years have been filled with
17 numerous accomplishments at the museum; her positive,
18 persuasive nature has been a tremendous tool in sustaining
19 the AVMA, which is now home to 2,000 artifacts belonging to
20 local veterans both past and present; the collection
21 includes uniforms, medals, weapons, photographs, and books
22 from all major American wars; and
23 WHEREAS, Sandy's job performance has exceeded
24 expectations; the AVMA mainly relies upon donations and
25 daily volunteers, and she is the only paid staff member;
26 under her leadership, the museum continues to succeed and
27 grow, due largely to Sandy's encouragement of others in
28 order to accomplish a mission; and

29 WHEREAS, moving the museum from the Louisville and
30 Nashville Railroad Depot to its current location in the
31 larger Limestone County Events Center was indeed a great
32 challenge; however, Sandy, who is a retired Air Force Master
33 Sergeant with 20 years of service, met the challenge head-on
34 while organizing, planning, and executing the move in a
35 seamless fashion; her leadership style is commendable; and
36 WHEREAS, her ability to interact with elected
37 officials at the city, county, and state levels is another
38 positive attribute; she is well respected within the
39 business and political communities, and she has been very
40 connected in getting the community involved with the museum
41 as a whole; and
42 WHEREAS, under Sandy's direction, the museum has
43 sought to institute a variety of programs and activities to
44 bring in more members for the Alabama Veterans Museum
45 Foundation, raise funds, and increase the number of tourists
46 visiting the museum; some of these new activities include
47 blood drives, an annual fish fry, political rallies,
48 military shows, and participation in a yearly Veterans Day
49 Parade; these efforts also include a variety of successful
50 memorial programs; and
51 WHEREAS, Sandy has also helped initiate two of the
