3 Relating to public safety; to establish the Alabama
4 Work Zone Safety Act as a pilot program to authorize the
5 procurement, installation, and operation of a photographic
6 speed enforcement system in a segment of interstate highway
7 declared to be a work zone; to require reports to the
8 Legislature studying the effectiveness of the pilot program;
9 to establish a process for the issuance of civil traffic
10 citations for violations and the enforcement and appeal of
11 such citations; to establish affirmative defenses to a
12 violation; to repeal these provisions on September 30, 2028;
13 and to create the ALEA Work Zone Safety Fund and the
14 Transportation Work Zone Safety Fund and provide for certain
15 civil penalties to be deposited into the fund.
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
17 Section 1. Sections 1 through 19 of this act shall be
18 known and may be cited as the Alabama Work Zone Safety Act.
19 Section 2. The Legislature finds and declares the
20 following:
21 (1) Highway construction and maintenance creates a high
22 risk environment for highway workers who must work alongside
23 motor vehicles traveling at high rates of speed.
24 (2) Numerous highway workers have been struck and
25 killed in work zones due to motor vehicles traveling at high
26 rates of speed through the work zones.
27 (3) Speed enforcement management within a work zone is
28 one of the most effective measures to improve safety in the
Page 1
one of the most effective measures to improve safety in the
SB341 Enrolled
29 work zone.
30 (4) Speed enforcement is highly labor intensive and can
31 even be hazardous to law enforcement officers given the
32 physical constraints of many work zones.
33 (5) Photographic speed enforcement systems allow law
34 enforcement to successfully manage speed enforcement in a work
35 zone in a safe and cost-effective manner.
36 (6) The adoption and implementation of a photographic
37 speed enforcement system for work zones is necessary to reduce
38 severe crashes, reduce speeding, and improve safe traffic flow
39 in work zones in a state where the patrol resources alone are
40 not capable of consistent enforcement of traffic laws at a
41 level necessary to protect human life.
42 (7) The severe lack of safety of highway workers in
43 work zones is an emergency that necessitates the immediate
44 adoption of a photographic speed enforcement system consistent
45 with the terms of this act.
46 Section 3. (a) The Alabama Work Zone Safety Act is
47 enacted as a pilot program and shall continue through the
48 completion of all work done in the work zone designated by the
49 Department of Transportation, unless continued by act of the
50 Legislature.
51 (b) The Department of Transportation and the Alabama
52 State Law Enforcement Agency (ALEA) shall collaborate to
53 implement the pilot program for one work zone conducted on the
54 interstate and shall finalize a plan for the implementation of
55 the pilot program by October 1, 2026. The plan shall
56 contemplate a gradual rollout of the program and the promotion

57 of driver awareness of the pending use of the program in work
58 zones. The plan shall ensure that the full scale
59 implementation and enforcement of the program shall not occur
60 sooner than November 16, 2026, to ensure that the
61 implementation of the program is phased in. To satisfy this
62 deadline, the department and ALEA may make necessary emergency
63 procurements pursuant to Sections 39-2-2 and 41-16-53, Code of
64 Alabama 1975, and may adopt emergency rules pursuant to
65 Section 41-22-5, Code of Alabama 1975, to govern the use of
66 devices in work zones; ensure adequate notice for drivers, and
67 signage rules, clear protocol for installation and placement

72 Transportation and the Alabama State Law Enforcement Agency

74 the implementation of this pilot program and shall provide
75 data measuring the effectiveness of the pilot program on
76 creating safer work zones.
