4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing court rule, a court may continue
6 a criminal or civil case upon a motion of a party or
7 the court.
8 This bill would require that a criminal or civil
9 case be continued when a party, his or her leading
10 attorney, or a witness satisfying certain criteria is
11 absent because he or she is on active duty in the armed
12 forces.
13 This bill would require in criminal cases that
14 bail be set for certain defendants when the state is
15 granted such a continuance.
16 This bill would also require the Alabama Supreme
17 Court to amend its rules to conform with these
18 requirements.

33 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
34 Section 1. (a)(1) A court, upon or without motion,
35 shall continue a criminal case when any party thereto or his
36 or her leading attorney is absent from court when the case is
37 reached because he or she is on active duty as a member of the
38 National Guard or a reserve or active component of the Armed
39 Forces of the United States.
40 (2) If the leading attorney is absent, his or her
41 client shall make an oath that the client cannot safely go to
42 trial without the attorney or, if a party is absent, his or
43 her leading attorney shall state that the attorney cannot
44 safely go to trial without the party.
45 (3) The case may proceed if the party, in the absence
46 of his or her leading attorney, or the leading attorney, in
47 the absence of the party, announces ready for trial on the
48 call of the case.
49 (b) A court shall continue a criminal case upon a
50 showing by the state or the defendant that a witness is
51 unavailable because the witness is on active duty as a member
52 of the National Guard or as a member of a reserve or active
53 component of the Armed Forces of the United States if all of
54 the following are established by the movant:
55 (1) The witness is material and necessary.
56 (2) The witness is located outside of the state.
