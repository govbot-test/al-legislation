1 Enrolled, An Act,
2 To propose an amendment to the Constitution of Alabama
3 of 2022, relating to Franklin County, to authorize a qualified
4 taxpayer 65 years of age or older to claim a senior property
5 tax exemption for real property owned in the county under
6 certain conditions.
7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
8 Section 1. The following amendment to the Constitution
9 of Alabama of 2022, is proposed:
10 PROPOSED AMENDMENT
11 (a) In Franklin County, an individual 65 years of age
12 or older may claim a senior property tax exemption for ad
13 valorem tax purposes on real property located in the county
14 which is owned by the individual, classified as single-family
15 owner-occupied residential property, and used as the principal
16 place of residence of the individual for not less than five
17 years immediately prior to the tax year for which the
18 individual first claims the senior property tax exemption. The
19 senior property tax exemption shall freeze the assessed value
20 of the property for the year immediately prior to claiming the
21 exemption.
22 (b) The following provisions shall apply to the
23 individual claiming the senior property tax exemption:
24 (1) The taxpayer shall continue to receive the senior
25 property tax exemption as long as the taxpayer continues to
26 use the property as his or her principal place of residence.
27 (2) The individual shall continue to be eligible to
28 claim a homestead exemption and any other exemption authorized

34 added to the assessed value of the property and subject to ad
