5 FESTIVAL.
6
7 WHEREAS, the Ozark Collard Green Festival, founded by
8 Ms. Belinda Thomas, was established to celebrate the
9 cultural significance of collard greens through food,
10 farming, art, wellness, and education, and to serve as a
11 growing platform that uplifts local farmers, artists, and
12 food innovators; and
13 WHEREAS, Alabama's agricultural and cultural
14 identities are deeply rooted in the traditions of greens and
15 root vegetables, and few foods represent the intersection of
16 health, heritage, and community as meaningfully as collard
17 greens; and
18 WHEREAS, rich in vitamins and essential nutrients,
19 collard greens are both a staple of Southern cuisine and a
20 symbol of nourishment, resilience, and shared history across
21 generations; and
22 WHEREAS, from family kitchens and church gatherings
23 to community celebrations and holiday tables, collard greens
24 with potlikker have long served as a unifying cultural
25 experience for Alabamians, and the tradition of dipping
26 cornbread into potlikker reflects the warmth, hospitality,
27 and resourcefulness that define our state; and
28 WHEREAS, the Ozark Collard Green Festival recognizes
