4 COMMENDING THE INAUGURAL OZARK COLLARD GREEN FESTIVAL.
5
6 WHEREAS, the Ozark Collard Green Festival, founded by
7 Ms. Belinda Thomas, was established to celebrate the
8 cultural significance of collard greens through food,
9 farming, art, wellness, and education, and to serve as a
10 growing platform that uplifts local farmers, artists, and
11 food innovators; and
12 WHEREAS, Alabama's agricultural and cultural
13 identities are deeply rooted in the traditions of greens and
14 root vegetables, and few foods represent the intersection of
15 health, heritage, and community as meaningfully as collard
16 greens; and
17 WHEREAS, rich in vitamins and essential nutrients,
18 collard greens are both a staple of Southern cuisine and a
19 symbol of nourishment, resilience, and shared history across
20 generations; and
21 WHEREAS, from family kitchens and church gatherings
22 to community celebrations and holiday tables, collard greens
23 with potlikker have long served as a unifying cultural
24 experience for Alabamians, and the tradition of dipping
25 cornbread into potlikker reflects the warmth, hospitality,
26 and resourcefulness that define our state; and
27 WHEREAS, the Ozark Collard Green Festival recognizes
28 Alabama's significant role in collard green production and
