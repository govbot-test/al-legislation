6 TO BE ENTITLED
7 AN ACT
8
9 Relating to primary elections; to establish the
10 Safeguard Alabama Voter Engagement (SAVE) Act; to require
11 electors to register their party affiliation in order to vote
12 in primary elections and to provide for deadlines to register
13 party affiliation; to amend Sections 17-13-1 and 17-13-7,
14 Code of Alabama 1975, to prohibit electors registered with a
15 political party from voting a different political party ballot
16 at a primary election or primary runoff election; to require
17 the Secretary of State to retain certain voter history
18 information as a public record; to amend Section 17-3-52, Code
19 of Alabama 1975, to require the Secretary of State to revise
20 the voter application form to allow for party registration; to
21 repeal Section 17-13-7.1, Code of Alabama 1975, which
22 prohibits crossover voting between a primary election and
23 subsequent primary runoff election; to amend Section 17-13-47,
24 Code of Alabama 1975, to require political parties to publish
25 qualifications for candidates on the websites of the political
26 parties; and to make nonsubstantive, technical revisions to
27 update the existing code language to current
28 style.

style.

29 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
30 Section 1. (a) This section shall be known and may be
31 cited as the Safeguard Alabama Voter Engagement (SAVE) Act.
32 (b)(1) On and after January 1, 2027, a qualified
33 elector shall register his or her political party affiliation
34 before the elector may vote in any countywide or statewide
35 primary or primary runoff election. A qualified elector may
36 register his or her party affiliation through the Secretary of
37 State's website or with the board of registrars, or at the
38 voting precinct of the elector on the day of any countywide or
39 statewide primary or primary runoff election. An elector who
40 has not registered a political party affiliation shall be
41 considered unaffiliated for purposes of Title 17, Code of
42 Alabama 1975. Notwithstanding Section 17-3-50, Code of Alabama
43 1975, an unaffiliated elector may register a political party
44 affiliation at any time, including during the 14-day period
45 before an election.
46 (2) If an elector chooses to register party affiliation
47 at a voting precinct on primary election day or primary runoff
48 election day, the act of requesting the ballot of a specific
49 political party shall constitute party registration, and no
50 additional action shall be required of the elector.
51 (3) A qualified elector who has already registered his
52 or her political party affiliation may not change his or her
53 political party affiliation to a different political party
54 during the period beginning 60 days prior to a primary
55 election through the day of the corresponding general
56 election.

57 (c) An elector who has registered a political party
58 affiliation may change his or her status to unaffiliated at
59 any time, provided the elector may not subsequently register
60 with a different political party during the blackout period
61 described in subdivision (b)(3).
62 (d) Nothing in this section shall be construed to
63 prohibit any state executive committee of a political party
64 from establishing the qualifications of individuals desiring
65 to become candidates for nomination to offices at a primary
66 election.
67 (e)(1) Beginning on and after July 1, 2027, signs shall
68 be posted at each polling place at a primary election and
69 primary runoff election that contains the following notice:
70 "By voting in this primary election, you have
71 registered your affiliation with a particular political party.
72 State law prohibits you from changing your political party
73 affiliation to a different political party until after the
74 general election. In the future, you may not change your party
75 affiliation within the 60-day period before any primary
76 election."
77 (2) A sign shall be posted in a clearly visible
78 location near the entrance of the polling place, and may be
79 posted in other conspicuous locations within a polling place,
80 as determined by the Secretary of State, by rule.
81 (f) The Secretary of State shall retain the following
82 voter history information for purposes of Section 17-4-38,
83 Code of Alabama 1975 for each voter:
84 (1) The party ballot requested in each primary and

90 (g) Absentee ballot managers shall not be responsible
91 for registering a voter's political party affiliation.
92 Notwithstanding subsection (b), an absentee election manager
93 shall reject an application for an absentee ballot for a
94 primary election unless the absentee election manager
95 affirmatively confirms that the applicant has already
96 registered his or her political party affiliation.
97 (h) The Secretary of State shall adopt rules to
98 implement this section.
