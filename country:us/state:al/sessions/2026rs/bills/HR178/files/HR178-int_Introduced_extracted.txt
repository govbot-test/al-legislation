5 FOUNDATION'S 20TH ANNUAL "HONORING OUR ELDERS DAY" GALA.
6
7 WHEREAS, with great joy and special pleasure, we
8 honor Mrs. Pamela Murray Curry as the special guest at the
9 Rosetta James Foundation's 20th Annual "Honoring Our Elders
10 Day" Gala at the Jackson Center in Huntsville, Alabama, on
11 Saturday, March 14, 2026; and
12 WHEREAS, Mrs. Curry is a native of Dayton, Ohio, who
13 received her Bachelor of Science degree in Social Work from
14 Tennessee State University (TSU) in 1973; she relocated to
15 Huntsville in 1982 and retired from the Huntsville City
16 Schools system in 2007, after many years of professional and
17 dedicated service as a licensed Social Worker; and
18 WHEREAS, Mrs. Curry was married to the late Mr.
19 Charles A. Curry for 46 years; she is the loving mother of
20 two daughters and doting grandmother of two grandchildren;
21 she is also a faithful sister, aunt, and friend, as well as
22 a resolute longtime member of First Missionary Baptist
23 Church, where she has faithfully served as a deaconess,
24 nursery Sunday school teacher, child development center
25 board member, and several other ministries; and
26 WHEREAS, a Golden Life Member of Delta Sigma Theta
27 Sorority, Incorporated, Mrs. Curry diligently serves in the
28 Huntsville Alumnae Chapter, having chaired numerous
Page 1
Huntsville Alumnae Chapter, having chaired numerous
HR178 INTRODUCED
29 committees and community activities; she is also a charter
30 member of the Huntsville Chapter of the TSU Alumni
31 Association and has proudly represented her alma mater as a
32 former member of the President's Honor Circle and Women's
33 Legacy Center board member; she has dutifully served on
34 numerous community boards, including Christmas Charities
35 Year-Round and Harris Home for Children; most recently, she
36 became a charter member of the prestigious Women's Emerald
37 Society of Huntsville Hospital; and
38 WHEREAS, the mission of the Rosetta James Foundation
39 is to honor the wisdom, service, and dedication of our
40 cherished senior citizens; the "Honoring Our Elders Day"
41 Gala is a heartfelt tribute to community and civic
42 volunteers 70 years of age and above who have dedicated
43 their lives to uplifting others and fostering positive
44 change; these remarkable individuals exemplify the very
45 spirit of compassion, leadership, and service that inspires
46 us all; and
47 WHEREAS, this annual celebration not only shines a
48 spotlight on these distinguished elders but also serves as a
49 platform to invest in the next generation of agents for
50 change; through the generous support of its attendees and
51 donors, the Gala raises vital funds to provide scholarships
52 for deserving students who embody the principles of
53 volunteerism, academic excellence, and civic engagement;
54 these outstanding students will also be recognized during
55 the event, connecting their bright futures to the enduring
56 legacies of those who came before them; and

legacies of those who came before them; and

57 WHEREAS, this year's Gala will be held at noon on
58 Saturday, March 14, at the Jackson Center; the extraordinary
59 contributions of our elders will be honored while future
60 leaders will be empowered to continue building a legacy of
61 service and excellence; now therefore,
62 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE
63 LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, That Mrs. Pamela Murray Curry will
64 be highly honored on an unforgettable afternoon filled with
65 inspiration, celebration, and community, and we endeavor to
66 continue uplifting and investing in those who make a
