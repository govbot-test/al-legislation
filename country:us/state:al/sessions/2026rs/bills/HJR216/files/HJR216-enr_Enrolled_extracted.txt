8 WHEREAS, it is with highest commendation that we
9 recognize Alabama State Trooper Joshua Wasden as a recipient
10 of the 2026 Alabama Legislative Meritorious Service Award
11 for Law Enforcement for exemplary courage in the line of
12 duty; and
13 WHEREAS, on August 26, 2025, Trooper Joshua Wasden
14 responded to a call from a motorist in medical distress
15 traveling northbound on I-65 near mile marker 136 in Butler
16 County; the driver, John Wilbur Dotson, Jr. of Fairhope,
17 Alabama, was instructed by the operator to pull over and
18 await emergency responders; Trooper Wasden was in the area
19 at the time and responded to the scene; and
20 WHEREAS, when Trooper Wasden approached the car, he
21 discovered the driver was unresponsive to touch or voice
22 commands; he immediately recognized the life-threatening
23 nature of the situation, returned to his vehicle to retrieve
24 a first aid kit, and then returned to the driver's side door
25 to better assess the patient; and
26 WHEREAS, within minutes Greenville Emergency Medical
27 Services and a Butler County Sheriff's Deputy arrived on the
28 scene; Trooper Wasden informed the ambulance crew of his

scene; Trooper Wasden informed the ambulance crew of his

29 observations and assisted with loading the patient onto a
30 stretcher for transport; rather than returning to service,
31 Trooper Wasden recognized the need for assistance and
32 entered the patient compartment of the ambulance to assist
33 the medics; and
34 WHEREAS, Trooper Wasden immediately assumed the task
35 of providing manual ventilation using a bag-valve mask with
36 supplemental oxygen while the medics focused on other tasks;
37 almost immediately, normal skin color began returning to the
38 patient's face; once the patient was stabilized for
39 transport, he was rushed to Regional Medical Center in
40 Greenville for emergency treatment, then later to a hospital
41 in Mobile for more definitive care; and
42 WHEREAS, following the incident, Trooper Wasden made
43 contact with the patient's spouse, Beth Dotson, to advise
44 her of the situation; it was later determined that the
45 driver was diagnosed with atrial fibrillation and has
46 recovered well under the care of a cardiologist; and
47 WHEREAS, Trooper Wasden went above and beyond the
48 call of duty during this event; he recognized the urgency
49 for lifesaving intervention and the need to assist the
50 ambulance crew as a collaborative effort; without prompt and
51 effective ventilation and cardioversion at the scene, the
52 likelihood of a positive outcome or survival was extremely
