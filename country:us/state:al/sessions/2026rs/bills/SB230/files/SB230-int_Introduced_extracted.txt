4 SYNOPSIS:
5 The Fair Campaign Practices Act provides that
6 campaign contributions may only be spent for certain
7 specified purposes.
8 This bill would authorize campaign funds to be
9 used to pay for security expenses to protect the safety
10 of a candidate, an elected official, or the immediate
11 family or staff of a candidate or elected official.
12 This bill would specify what constitutes a
13 security expense.
14
15
16 A BILL
17 TO BE ENTITLED
18 AN ACT
19
20 Relating to campaign finance; to add Section 17-5-7.3
21 to the Code of Alabama 1975, to authorize campaign funds to be
22 used for certain security measures to protect the safety of a
23 candidate, an elected official, or his or her immediate family
24 or staff.
25 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
26 Section 1. Section 17-5-7.3 is added to the Code of
27 Alabama 1975, to read as follows:

§17-5-7.3
SB230 INTRODUCED
29 (a) In addition to the use of campaign contributions
30 for the purposes listed in Section 17-5-7, campaign
31 contributions may be used to pay, or reimburse the state, for
32 security expenses to protect the safety of a candidate, an
33 elected official, or an immediate family or staff member of a
34 candidate or an elected official. For purposes of this
35 section, "security expenses" include all of the following:
36 (1) Reasonable costs of cybersecurity such as data
37 removal services that find and remove personal information of
38 a candidate, elected official, or immediate family or staff
39 member of the candidate or elected official from data brokers
40 and websites, and other online sources.
41 (2) Reasonable costs of installing and monitoring
42 nonstructural security devices, including home security
43 hardware, locks, alarm systems, motion detectors, and security
44 cameras, provided the security devices are intended to provide
45 security and not to improve or increase the value of the
46 property.
47 (3) Reasonable costs of security personnel for a
48 candidate, elected official, or immediate family or staff
49 member of the candidate or elected official.
50 (b) Any device or other item purchased and installed
51 pursuant to subsection (a) which is permanently attached to
52 the property of a candidate, elected official, or immediate
53 family or staff member of the candidate or elected official is
54 exempt from the liquidation provisions of Section 17-5-7.2.
55 Section 2. This act shall become effective immediately.
