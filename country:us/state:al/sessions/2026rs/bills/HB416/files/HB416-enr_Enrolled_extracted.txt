1 Enrolled, An Act,

3 Relating to Cleburne County; to establish the Cleburne
4 County Economic Development Authority; to provide for the
5 membership of a board of directors; and to provide for the
6 powers and duties of the authority.
7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
8 Section 1. (a) The Cleburne County Economic Development
9 Authority is established to negotiate for the county on all
10 industrial and economic development matters in the county. The
11 board of directors of the authority shall consist of the
12 following members, all of whom must be residents and qualified
13 electors of the county:
14 (1) Two members serving an initial term of two years,
15 one of whom is appointed by the county commissioner
16 representing District 4 and one of whom is appointed by the
17 county commissioner representing District 2.
18 (2) Two members serving an initial term of four years,
19 one of whom is appointed by the county commissioner
20 representing District 1 and one of whom is appointed by the
21 county commissioner representing District 3.
22 (3) One member serving an initial term of six years
23 appointed by the chair of the county commission.
24 (b) Following initial terms, all members shall serve
25 four-year terms that shall commence on January 1. Any vacancy
26 on the board shall be filled by the respective appointing
27 authority for the remainder of the member's term.
28 (c) Members of the board shall serve without

29 compensation but are entitled to reimbursement from funds of
30 the authority for their actual and necessary expenses incurred
31 in the performance of their actual duties.
32 (d) No later than November 1, 2026, the board shall
33 hold an organizational meeting at which time the board shall
34 elect a chair and vice chair by majority vote of the members
35 present and voting at the meeting. Thereafter, the board of
36 directors shall meet at least quarterly and at any other time
37 if two-thirds of the members of the board submit a request to
38 the chair of the board stating a need for a special meeting.
39 (e) At the first board meeting in January of each year,
40 the board of directors, by a majority vote, shall elect a
41 chair, vice chair, secretary, and treasurer who shall hold
42 office until their successors have been elected.
43 Section 2. (a) The board may adopt bylaws to regulate
44 and administer the business of the Cleburne County Economic
45 Development Authority.
46 (b) Members of the board shall appoint a director to
47 serve at the pleasure of the board to carry out the day-to-day
48 operations of the authority. The director shall be subject to
49 the filing requirements under Section 36-25-14, Code of
50 Alabama 1975.
51 Section 3. The authority may employ such personnel as
52 the director, in consultation with the members of the board,
53 deems necessary to carry out the duties and purposes of the
54 authority. Personnel employed by the authority may participate
55 in the Employees' Retirement System of Alabama like other
56 employees of the county and may purchase prior service credit

57 in the Employees' Retirement System for services rendered to

60 Section 4. Members of the board of directors and the
61 director of the authority shall be considered public officials
62 for purposes of Chapter 25 of Title 36, Code of Alabama 1975.
63 Section 5. (a) The authority shall formulate and
64 implement plans to promote and foster the industrial
65 development and economic growth of all areas of Cleburne
66 County and any incorporated municipalities located within the
67 county. To this end, the authority shall be entitled to all of
68 the powers, rights, privileges, exemptions, and immunities
69 provided by the general laws of Alabama to local industrial
70 development authorities, including, but not limited to,
71 abatement or exemption of local taxes, issuance of bonds, and
72 the acquisition, enlargement, improvement, replacement,
73 ownership, leasing, selling, or disposing of properties as the
74 authority deems necessary to promote the creation of jobs and
75 develop commerce and trade within the county and the state.
76 (b)(1) Notwithstanding subsection (a), the authority
77 may not abate or exempt any local tax to any person or other
78 entity unless the governing body of the local entity in which
79 the tax is levied affirmatively approves the granting of the
80 economic incentive.
81 (2) Notwithstanding subdivision (1), the authority may
82 not abate or exempt any local taxes to a board member, the
83 director, or any employee of the authority; to his or her
84 spouse or dependent child; or to any business with which the
