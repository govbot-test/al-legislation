6 WHEREAS, with special pleasure, joyful felicitations
7 are extended to Louise Carr on the occasion of her milestone
8 100th birthday; and
9 WHEREAS, Louise Bice was born on February 26, 1926,
10 to Lonnie and Nina Mae Bice at her family home in Holly
11 Pond, Cullman County, Alabama, during the prohibition era
12 and before the first talking movie was made; popular songs
13 that year include "Valencia" by Paul Whiteman, "Bye Bye,
14 Blackbird" by Gene Austin, and "I'm Sitting on Top of the
15 World" by Al Jolson; and the 1925 Alabama Crimson Tide
16 football team played in the Rose Bowl on January 1, 1926,
17 earning their first national championship; and
18 WHEREAS, Louise Bice married Verbon Ray Carr in 1947
19 at 21 years of age, remaining married until his passing in
20 2000; they were blessed with a son, Harold Carr, and
21 grandchildren, Bradley Carr and Holly Dutton; she is
22 great-grandmother to Jackson Dutton and Addie Dutton; and
23 WHEREAS, Verbon worked in the steel industry for U.S.
24 Steel, American Bridge, and Americo; they lived in
25 Birmingham, Detroit, and Cullman during their marriage
26 before settling in Holly Pond where Louise owned a chicken
27 farm; she also worked for the railroad and Oneida Sewing
28 Plant; and

29 WHEREAS, Louise enjoys crafting, needlepoint,
30 crochet, and puzzles; she is also well-known at the Holly

33 WHEREAS, a centennial birthday is a significant
