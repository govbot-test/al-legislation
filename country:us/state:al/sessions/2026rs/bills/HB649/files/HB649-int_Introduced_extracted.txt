4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, the Alabama Affordable
7 Housing Act, does not authorize a Class 1 municipality
8 to create its own housing trust fund.
9 This bill would authorize a Class 1 municipality
10 to create and administer a housing trust fund and
11 provides for the creation of an advisory board to
12 oversee the administration of the trust fund. This bill
13 would authorize the types of funds the housing trust
14 fund may accept and specifes the types of allowable
15 uses of the funds received.
16
17
18 A BILL
19 TO BE ENTITLED
20 AN ACT
21
22 Relating to Class 1 municipalities; to authorize a
23 Class 1 municipality to create a housing trust fund; to
24 promote the development of affordable housing; to specify how
25 the housing trust may be funded; to establish an advisory
26 board for the housing trust; and to authorize allowable uses
27 for the housing trust funds.

HB649 INTRODUCED
29 Section 1. The provisions of this act shall apply only
30 to a Class 1 municipality that adopts, by ordinance or
31 resolution, provisions creating a housing trust fund in the
32 municipality.
33 Section 2. The Legislature finds and declares that:
34 (a) There is a shortage of adequate shelter for
35 residents of a Class 1 municipality, including the
36 availability of affordable residences or permanent homes with
37 sufficient privacy, space, physical accessibility, security,
38 structural stability, and durability, as well as proper
39 electrical, plumbing, and heating systems.
40 (b) The lack of affordable housing in a Class 1
41 municipality harms the community's ability to develop and
42 maintain a strong and stable economy. Additionally,
43 displacement of families and individuals from affordable
44 housing, as well as the lack of access to it, leads to
45 increased public spending on crime prevention, public health,
46 safety, fire, accident prevention, and other services and
47 facilities.
48 (c) The public's health, safety, and economic interests
49 are best served by providing permanent affordable housing
50 because such housing, among other things, allows residents of
51 a Class 1 municipality to keep jobs, helps children do well in
52 school, and promotes economic growth and prosperity in the
53 community.
54 (d) The establishment of a housing trust fund in a
55 Class 1 municipality is intended to:
56 (1) Provide funding to address the affordable housing
Page 2
(1) Provide funding to address the affordable housing

57 needs of a Class 1 municipality.
58 (2) Help families attain economic stability.
59 (3) Revitalize blighted and distressed neighborhoods by
60 creating safe, decent, and affordable housing.
61 (4) Contribute to economic growth through increased
62 housing production, employment, and tax revenue, thereby
63 benefiting all residents of a Class 1 municipality.
64 (5) Address shortages in providing safe and affordable
65 housing for residents earning at or below one hundred twenty
66 percent of the median family income, many of whom are
67 individuals with disabilities, elderly, victims of domestic
68 violence, veterans, homeless or at risk of homelessness, and
69 living with chronic conditions.
70 (e) Establishing a housing trust fund in a Class 1
71 municipality under this act is essential for the public
72 interest and serves a public purpose for which public funds
73 can be used and private property can be acquired.
74 (f) The purpose of this act is to authorize a Class 1
75 municipality to create and operate a local housing trust fund
76 to promote the development of affordable housing, as defined
77 in this chapter, and to promote fair housing in neighborhoods
78 throughout the Class 1 municipality.
79 Section 3. As used in this act, the following words and
80 phrases have the following meanings:
81 (1) AFFORDABLE HOUSING. Residential housing for rent or
82 sale that is appropriately priced for rent or sale to a person
83 or family whose income does not exceed one hundred twenty
84 percent of the median income for the local area, with

91 the purpose of increasing the production of housing units,
92 rehabilitating existing housing units, and maintaining such
93 housing so that it can be made affordable to individuals and
94 families whose incomes are at or below 120 percent of the
95 median family income.
96 Section 4. A Class 1 municipality is hereby authorized
97 to create and operate a housing trust fund, by ordinance or
98 resolution, for the purposes stated in this act.

121 Section 7. (a) The governing body of a Class 1

123 housing trust fund advisory committee for the purpose of

132 credits for residential and commercial properties and

134 finance.

136 housing, community development, municipal planning, social
137 services, or public health.

139 municipality who either: (i) rents his or her home and lives

147 incorporated by the Class 1 municipality pursuant to Chapter 9

231 Section 9. Money in a housing trust fund created under
232 this act may be used for the following purposes:
233 (1) To provide assistance, by loan, grant, rental
234 subsidy, or otherwise, for the development, rehabilitation, or
235 financing of affordable housing, including inclusionary
236 housing.
237 (2) Predevelopment activities for the acquisition,
238 development, new construction, rehabilitation, or restoration
239 of affordable housing including, but not limited to, fees and
240 expenses for architectural services, engineering services,
241 attorney fees, appraisals, title reports and housing
242 counseling.
243 (3) On-site construction activities involving the
244 building, altering, repairing, improving, or demolishing of
245 any structure or building on real property.
246 (4) Matching other funds from federal, state, or
247 private resources for affordable housing projects.
248 (5) To provide down payment assistance, rental
249 assistance, and home-buyer counseling services to individuals
250 and families whose incomes are at or below one hundred twenty
251 percent of the median family income.
252 (6) Provide technical assistance to nonprofit
Page 9
(6) Provide technical assistance to nonprofit

253 developers of affordable housing.
254 (7) Provide funding for existing housing programs which
255 have been established to assist low income households.
256 (8) Offer capital and operating assistance for the
257 creation of community land trusts that will develop, own, or
258 operate permanently affordable rental housing and help
259 low-income residents achieve homeownership.
260 (9) Offer affordable and fair housing-related services
261 to low-income households to help them find and stay in stable
262 housing, provided that such uses do not exceed 30 percent of
263 the funds allocated to the housing trust fund in a given
264 fiscal year.
265 (10) Cover administrative and planning costs for the
266 operation of the housing trust fund, with such costs not
267 exceeding five percent of the funds allocated in a given
268 fiscal year.
269 (11) Fund development projects that produce or preserve
270 affordable housing units.
271 (12) Providing grants to nonprofit corporations that
272 provide housing assistance such as homeless outreach,
273 re-housing services, case management, and critical home
274 repair.

276 2026.
