5 MONTH IN ALABAMA.

7 WHEREAS, colorectal cancer is the second leading
8 cause of cancer-related deaths among men and women combined
9 in the United States; and
10 WHEREAS, colorectal cancer is one of the most
11 preventable forms of cancer because screening tests can
12 detect and allow for the removal of polyps before they
13 become cancerous; and
14 WHEREAS, screening tests can detect colorectal cancer
15 early, when treatment is most effective; and
16 WHEREAS, the United States Preventive Services Task
17 Force and the American Cancer Society recommend that adults
18 at average risk begin regular colorectal cancer screening at
19 age 45; and
20 WHEREAS, public health authorities estimate that if
21 all eligible adults received recommended screening, as many
22 as 60 percent of colorectal cancer deaths could be
23 prevented; and
24 WHEREAS, when detected early, the five-year survival
25 rate for patients with localized colorectal cancer is
26 approximately 90 percent, yet only 39 percent of cases are
27 diagnosed at this stage, largely due to low screening rates;
28 and

33 WHEREAS, deaths from colorectal cancer occur
34 disproportionately among those who are underserved or

36 WHEREAS, since 2014, colorectal cancer rates have
37 been increasing by two percent per year in people younger

39 WHEREAS, public awareness and education campaigns on
40 colorectal cancer prevention, screening, and symptoms are
41 held annually during the month of March; and
42 WHEREAS, educational efforts help inform the public
43 about prevention methods, screening recommendations, and
44 symptoms for early detection; and
45 WHEREAS, greater awareness of colorectal cancer and

48 WHEREAS, observing Colorectal Cancer Awareness Month
