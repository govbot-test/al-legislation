4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, county commissions may adopt
6 a procurement or purchasing program to simplify
7 purchases that applies only to purchases of tangible
8 personal property.
9 This bill would allow a procurement or
10 purchasing program to apply to purchases of services.
11 Under existing law, county commissions must
12 consider whether a procurement or purchasing program
13 provides cost savings to the county.
14 This bill would allow county commissions to
15 consider whether a procurement or purchasing program
16 provides administrative savings in addition to cost
17 savings.
18 Under existing law, the county chief
19 administrative officer is responsible for overseeing
20 and managing a procurement or purchasing program.
21 This bill would also allow county commissions to
22 designate another individual to oversee and manage a
23 procurement or purchasing program.
