5 Under existing law, retailers of consumable hemp
7 Alabama Alcoholic Beverage Control Board. Existing law
8 authorizes pharmacies that are licensed by the ABC
9 Board to sell topical and sublingual consumable hemp
10 products only.
11 This bill would exempt pharmacies from licensing
12 and oversight by the ABC Board and allow these
13 pharmacies to sell consumable hemp products at retail,
14 provided the pharmacies are permitted by the Alabama
15 Board of Pharmacy and are in compliance with testing,
16 labeling, packaging, and other safety requirements
17 relating to consumable hemp products.
18 This bill would also require the Alabama Board
19 of Pharmacy to enforce these provisions and authorize
20 the board to adopt rules.

34 Code of Alabama 1975, added by Act 2025-385 of the 2025

37 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
38 Section 1. Section 28-12-6 is added to the Code of
39 Alabama 1975, to read as follows:
40 §28-12-6
41 (a) Notwithstanding Sections 28-12-44 and 28-12-45 and
42 any other provision of this chapter that prohibits the sale of
43 a consumable hemp product without a license issued by the
44 board, a pharmacy located in this state that holds a valid
45 permit issued by the Alabama Board of Pharmacy may sell at
46 retail consumable hemp products without obtaining a license
47 from, or being subject to oversight by the Alcoholic Beverage
48 Control Board, provided the pharmacy complies with all of the
49 following:
50 (1) All consumable hemp products must be sold by a
51 licensed pharmacist or by a pharmacy technician or employee
52 who is under the direct supervision and control of a licensed
53 pharmacist.
54 (2) Consumable hemp products may only be sold to
55 individuals 21 years of age or older and the sale must be in
56 compliance with Section 28-12-48.

64 "§28-12-45

88 licensed premises.
89 (c)(b)(1) Consumable hemp products that are beverages
90 may be sold in a retail food store, provided the retail food
91 store obtains a consumable hemp product retailer license from
92 the board and complies with this chapter and rules of the
93 board. Consumable hemp product beverages sold in a retail food
94 store must be kept in an area that is: (i) separated from
95 nonalcoholic beverages or beverages intended for children;
96 (ii) behind glass; and (iii) demarcated by a sign indicating
97 that the beverages contain hemp-derived compounds. This
98 subsection does not prohibit a retailer described in
99 subsection (a) from selling consumable hemp product beverages
100 in that retailer's licensed premises.
101 (2) For purposes of this subsection, "retail food
102 store" means any store commonly known as a supermarket, food
103 store, or grocery store, primarily engaged in the retail sale
104 of a variety of canned goods, frozen foods, nonalcoholic
105 beverages, dry goods, either packaged or in bulk, and fresh
106 produce or meats, and the store dedicates: (i) a minimum of 75
107 percent of the store's selling area to the sale of food items
108 listed in this subdivision; and (ii) at least 14,000 square
109 feet of the store's footprint to the sale of food items listed
110 in this subdivision.
111 (d)(c)(1) A retailer described in subsection (a) shall
112 maintain at its licensed premises a minimum of 500 square feet

117 (2) A retailer must have an employee present in the

119 the premises is open to customers.
120 (3) The purchase of all consumable hemp products must
