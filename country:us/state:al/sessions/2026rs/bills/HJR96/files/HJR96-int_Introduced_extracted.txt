4 HJR___ RECOGNIZING ALABAMA ARMY NATIONAL GUARD STAFF
5 SERGEANT RONALD WAYNE HURST UPON HIS RETIREMENT.
6
7 WHEREAS, it is with profound appreciation and deepest
8 admiration that we recognize Staff Sergeant (SSG) Ronald
9 Wayne Hurst for more than 20 years of faithful military
10 service, first as a member of the United States Navy and
11 more recently as a distinguished noncommissioned officer in
12 the Alabama Army National Guard; and
13 WHEREAS, throughout his noble career, SSG Hurst has
14 demonstrated unwavering dedication to duty, mission
15 accomplishment, and the welfare of sailors, soldiers, and
16 their families; and
17 WHEREAS, his military journey began in the U.S. Navy,
18 as a three-year term from June 1987 to June 1990; Hurst
19 served in the Naval Presidential Ceremonial Honor Guard and
20 was stationed in Washington, D.C., where his duties included
21 funerals in Arlington Cemetery; Hurst also stood guard at
22 the door of then-Department of Defense Secretary Dick
23 Cheney, as well as participated in Presidential galas and
24 inaugurations; and
25 WHEREAS, following a 15-year break in service, SSG
26 Hurst reenlisted in the Alabama Army National Guard in
27 December 2005 as a Horizontal Construction Engineer; he has
28 held key assignments with the 1312th Engineer Detachment,
Page 1
held key assignments with the 1312th Engineer Detachment,

29 the 168th Engineer Company, and the Fort McClellan Training
30 Center; demonstrating exceptional leadership and technical
31 expertise, he steadily progressed through the ranks, earning
32 promotions to Specialist in 2012, Sergeant in 2014, and
33 Staff Sergeant in June 2024; he currently serves as a
34 Horizontal Construction Engineer Instructor at the 3rd
35 Battalion, 200th Regiment; and
36 WHEREAS, SSG Hurst's awards and decorations include
37 the Meritorious Unit Commendation, as well as medals for
38 Joint Service Achievement, Army Achievement (2nd Award),
39 Army Reserve Component Achievement, and the Alabama
40 Commendation Medal, and ribbons for Noncommissioned Officer
41 Professional Development, Army Service, and the Army Reserve
42 Components Overseas Training Ribbon; and
43 WHEREAS, SSG Hurst is scheduled to retire in April
44 2026, completing more than two decades of faithful military
45 service; throughout his career, he has exemplified the
46 highest standards of leadership, professionalism, and
47 personal courage, earning the respect and admiration of
48 officers, noncommissioned officers, and enlisted personnel
49 alike; now therefore,
50 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
51 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That we hereby most highly
52 commend Alabama Army National Guard Staff Sergeant Ronald
53 Wayne Hurst for his exceptional service, outstanding
54 leadership, and lasting contributions to the United States
55 Armed Forces and our nation.
