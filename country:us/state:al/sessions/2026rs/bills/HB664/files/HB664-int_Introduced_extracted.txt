4 SYNOPSIS:
5 This bill would require the Commissioner of
6 Agriculture and Industries to establish the Family Farm
7 Resilience and Revenue Stabilization Program within the
8 Department of Agriculture and Industries to provide
9 bridge gaps and revenue gap payments to eligible family
10 farms during times of economic shock.
11 This bill would establish the Family Farm
12 Resilience Fund within the State Treasury to be used by
13 the commissioner to administer the program.
14 This bill would require the commissioner to
15 provide an annual report to the Governor and
16 Legislature detailing funds distributed under the
17 program and any proposed legislative action.
18 This bill would also require the department to
19 adopt rules to administer this act.

38 Section 1. This act shall be known and may be cited as
39 the Family Farm Resilience and Revenue Stabilization Act.
40 Section 2. For purposes of this act, the following
41 terms have the following meanings:
42 (1) ECONOMIC SHOCK. Any of the following:
43 a. A decline of 20 percent or more in the statewide
44 average price of a primary agricultural commodity within a
45 90-day period;
46 b. Documented trade retaliation or export restriction
47 affecting a primary state commodity;
48 c. A documented increase of 25 percent or more in core
49 agricultural input costs within a six-month period; or
50 d. A state of emergency declared by the Governor by
51 proclamation that materially affects agricultural production.
52 (2) FAMILY FARM. An agricultural operation that:
53 a. Is primarily owned by one or more state residents;
54 b. Is actively managed by the owner or his or her
55 immediate family; and
56 c. Has an annual gross income of not more than one
