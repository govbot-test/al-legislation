4 HJR___ HONORING THE 250TH ANNIVERSARY OF THE DECLARATION OF
5 INDEPENDENCE AND RECOMMITTING TO THE FOUNDING IDEALS OF
6 VIRTUE AND EDUCATION AND COMMENDING FORMER UNITED STATES
7 SECRETARY OF EDUCATION WILLIAM J. BENNETT FOR HIS
8 CONTRIBUTIONS TO ADVANCE THESE IDEALS.
9
10 WHEREAS, the 250th Anniversary of the Declaration of
11 Independence represents an ideal time to reflect and
12 reengage with the critical foundations of our nation; and
13 WHEREAS, our nation's Founding Fathers repeatedly
14 emphasized the necessity of an educated populace, and in
15 particular, a populace educated in the fundamental concepts
16 of virtue; and
17 WHEREAS, the continuation of any republic
18 fundamentally depends upon an educated and virtuous public;
19 and
20 WHEREAS, in too many instances, our schools have
21 strayed from ensuring that students are regularly exposed to
22 the timeless and fundamental concepts of virtue, such as
23 honesty, courage, perseverance, work, and compassion; and
24 WHEREAS, we commend those who have, throughout their
25 careers, worked to advance character and virtue education, a
26 leader among them being former United States Secretary of
27 Education William J. Bennett, author of The Book of Virtues
28 and The Children's Book of Virtues; and

and The Children's Book of Virtues; and

29 WHEREAS, at this critical juncture in our history,
30 the State of Alabama should recommit to infusing robust
31 character and virtue education into our schools; and
32 WHEREAS, reading timeless stories of individuals,
33 both from Alabama and beyond, who exhibit timeless virtues
34 in our schools is an ideal way for children to learn about
35 and grow into virtuous adults; now therefore,
36 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
37 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That the Legislature of Alabama
38 commends all those committed to the endeavor of exposing our
39 children to the critical and timeless characteristics of a
40 virtuous life, including Dr. William J. Bennett, and further
41 encourages all schools to review all aspects of their
42 educational programming and seek to infuse character and
43 virtue education into all aspects of their day.
