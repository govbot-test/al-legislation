9 Relating to the Legislature; to amend Section 29-4-4,
10 Code of Alabama 1975, to provide security for the Legislature
11 in the new Alabama State House, legislative parking deck, and
12 park adjacent to the new Alabama State House and at official
13 legislative events held off-site; to create the crime of
14 unlawful use of unmanned aircraft systems within the Capitol
15 complex; to provide for penalties; to exempt certain persons
16 from the crime of unlawful use of unmanned aircraft systems
17 within the Capitol complex; and to authorize the Alabama State
18 Law Enforcement Agency to take certain actions.
19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
20 Section 1. Section 29-4-4, Code of Alabama 1975, is
21 amended to read as follows:

63 Section 2. (a) For the purposes of this section, the
64 following terms have the following meanings:
65 (1) ALEA. The Alabama State Law Enforcement Agency.
66 (2) CAPITOL COMPLEX. Any of the following locations:
67 a. The Downtown Capitol Area.
68 b. The Alabama Governor's Mansion and all real property
69 situated between Cromwell Street and Finley Avenue and between
70 Court Street and Perry Street in Montgomery, Alabama.
71 c. Any official residence of the Governor of Alabama.
72 d. Any location designated by the secretary where the
73 House of Representatives, the Senate, the Supreme Court of
74 Alabama, the Alabama Court of Civil Appeals, or the Alabama
75 Court of Criminal Appeals convenes, or will convene, to
76 perform official business.
77 e. Any site of the construction or improvement of state
78 property within an area of the Capitol complex.
79 (3) CONDUCTING FLIGHT OPERATIONS. Any of the following
80 actions:
81 a. Manipulating the flight controls of an unmanned
82 aircraft system.
83 b. Acting as a remote pilot in command for a person
84 manipulating the flight controls of an unmanned aircraft

90 between Madison Avenue and High Street and between Lawrence
91 Street and Hilliard Street in Montgomery, Alabama.
92 (5) SECRETARY. The Secretary of ALEA.
93 (6) UNMANNED AIRCRAFT. An aircraft operated without the
94 possibility of direct human intervention from within or on the
95 aircraft.
96 (7) UNMANNED AIRCRAFT SYSTEM or UAS. An unmanned
97 aircraft and its associated elements, including communication
98 links and the components that control the unmanned aircraft,
99 that are required for the safe and efficient operation of the
100 unmanned aircraft in the national airspace system.
101 (8) USE. When used in reference to an unmanned aircraft
102 system, any of the following actions:
103 a. Conducting flight operations for an unmanned
104 aircraft system.
105 b. Launching an unmanned aircraft.
106 c. Landing an unmanned aircraft, or otherwise allowing
107 an unmanned aircraft to make contact with any individual or
108 real or personal property.
109 d. Causing an unmanned aircraft to fly.
110 e. Causing an unmanned aircraft to drop any payload.
111 f. Causing an unmanned aircraft system to deploy or
112 discharge an attached weapon, firearm, explosive, destructive

118 conduct surveillance of, or photograph or otherwise record
119 images of, the Capitol complex for the purpose of committing a
120 criminal offense.
121 (2) A violation of subdivision (1) is a Class C felony.
122 (c)(1) No person shall intentionally or knowingly use a

125 causes a disturbance to the Capitol complex.
126 (2) A violation of subdivision (1) is a Class C felony.
127 (d)(1) No person shall, without written permission from
128 the secretary, intentionally or knowingly use a UAS in the
129 vicinity of any site of the construction or improvement of
130 state property within the Capitol complex.
131 (2) A violation of subdivision (1) is a Class C
132 misdemeanor for a first offense and is a Class B misdemeanor
133 for a second or subsequent offense.
134 (e) None of the following persons shall be subject to
135 the prohibition in subdivision (d)(1):
136 (1) The United States, including any agencies or
137 departments thereof.
138 (2) The State of Alabama, including any agencies or
139 departments thereof.
140 (3) An employee, agent, contractor, or other person

144 (4) A person conducting emergency law enforcement or
145 fire response operations.
146 (5) A person who uses a UAS in accordance with written
147 permission from the secretary, or with a permit issued by the
148 secretary, as established in subsection (g).
149 (f)(1) ALEA may take reasonable and necessary measures
150 against a UAS operating in violation of this section,
151 including all of the following measures:
152 a. Jamming, to the extent consistent with federal law
153 and regulations, including those of the Federal Communications
154 Commission.
155 b. Hacking.
156 c. Physical capture.
157 d. Any other reasonable mitigation measure undertaken
158 to neutralize, intercept, disable, or disrupt a UAS.
159 (2) ALEA may seize a UAS, any part thereof, and any
160 property attached to the aircraft or dropped therefrom if the
161 UAS, any part thereof, or any property attached to the
162 aircraft or dropped therefrom was used in violation of this
163 section.
164 (g)(1) The secretary, by rule, may designate additional
165 locations as within the Capitol complex and may establish
166 procedures for temporarily designating a location as within
167 the Capitol complex.
168 (2) The secretary, in consultation with the Governor,
