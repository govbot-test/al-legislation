4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, a person and facility must
6 be licensed by the Alabama State Board of Prosthetists
7 and Orthotists to provide prosthetic, orthotic, or
8 pedorthic care in the state.
9 This bill would abolish the board and the
10 requirement to be licensed by the board to provide
11 prosthetic, orthotic, or pedorthic care.
12 This bill would require all persons and
13 facilities providing prosthetic, orthotic, or pedorthic
14 care to be certified by a national certifying entity.
15 This bill would provide criminal penalties for
16 violations
17 This bill would require a person to receive a
18 minimum amount of continuing education for
19 certification by a national certifying entity from the
20 Alabama Prosthetics and Orthotics Association.
21 This bill would authorize the Alabama
22 Prosthetics and Orthotics Association to levy fees on
23 certified persons to be deposited into the Alabama
24 Prosthetics and Orthotics Certification Fund.
25 This bill would also establish the Alabama
26 Prosthetics and Orthotics Certification Fund in the
27 State Treasury.
