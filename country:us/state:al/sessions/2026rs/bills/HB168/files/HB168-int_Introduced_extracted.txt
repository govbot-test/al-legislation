4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, loitering is a crime
6 committed when a person unlawfully loiters, remains, or
7 wanders about a specified place for a prohibited
8 purpose. The crime is also committed when a masked
9 person loiters, remains, or congregates in a public
10 place.
11 Existing law specifies that the crime is not
12 committed when the person is participating in a
13 masquerade event or participating in a public parade
14 presentation or an educational, religious, or
15 historical event.
16 This bill would further provide for the
17 exemption by including wearing a surgical or medical
18 mask while participating in a protest, demonstration,
19 or other public assembly, provided the use of the mask
20 is to mitigate the spread of an infectious disease.
21 This bill would also allow local boards of
22 education and public institutions of higher education
23 to adopt and enforce mask policies on their respective
24 campuses.
