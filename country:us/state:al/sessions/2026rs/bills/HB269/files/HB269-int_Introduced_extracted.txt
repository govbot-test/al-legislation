4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, governing authorities of
6 municipalities, counties, and instrumentalities in the
7 state, prior to expending $30,000 or more for any
8 labor, services, work, or for the purchase of
9 materials, equipment, supplies, or other personal
10 property, must enter into the contractual agreement by
11 free and open competitive bidding, on sealed bids, to
12 the lowest responsible bidder.
13 This bill would establish a limited exemption
14 from the competitive bidding requirements for
15 expenditures of less than $15,000 for the repair of
16 vehicles by local awarding authorities in the state.

30 §41-16-52.1
31 (a) For purposes of this section, the term "awarding
32 authority" means any county commission, governing body of a
33 municipality, or an instrumentality of a county or
34 municipality, including a waterworks board, a sewer board, a
35 gas board, and other like utility boards and commissions.
36 (b) All expenditures of funds, made by or on the behalf
37 of any awarding authority, for repair parts and the repair of
38 vehicles shall be made at the option of the awarding authority
39 without regard to this article when the following conditions
40 are met:
41 (1) The vehicle has a gross vehicle weight rating of
42 12,000 pounds or less.
43 (2) The incident of repair does not involve more than
44 fifteen thousand dollars ($15,000).
45 (3) The awarding authority solicits and documents price
46 estimates from at least two different vendors prior to
47 awarding the contract.
48 (c) The exemption provided under this section shall
49 apply to each vehicle repaired, and the exempted expenditure
50 shall not be aggregated with the expenditures of any other
51 incidents of repair.
52 (d)(1) The option provided by subsection (b) may be
53 exercised by the awarding authority by specific reference to
54 this section on any purchase orders and purchase commitments
55 executed by the awarding authority.
56 (2) The option shall not be exercised by any employee,
