Section 1:
PUBLIC SAFETY AND HOMELAND SECURITY SUBSTITUTE TO HB102
OFFERED BY REPRESENTATIVE UNDERWOOD
1
2
3
4 SYNOPSIS:
5 Under existing law, certain distinctive license
6 plates may be retained by the surviving spouse of the
7 original plate recipient.
8 This bill would authorize the surviving spouse
9 of a deceased "Disabled Veteran" license plate
10 recipient to be issued a new "Disabled Veteran Spouse"
11 license plate.
12 This bill would also make nonsubstantive,
13 technical revisions to update the existing code
14 language to current style.
15
16
17
18 A BILL
19 TO BE ENTITLED
20 AN ACT
21
22 Relating to license tags and plates; to amend Section
23 32-6-130, Code of Alabama 1975, to authorize surviving spouses
24 of deceased "Disabled Veteran" license plate recipients to be
25 issued "Disabled Veteran Spouse" license plates; and to make
26 nonsubstantive, technical revisions to update the existing
27 code language to current style.
28 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
Page 1
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
29 Section 1. Section 32-6-130, Code of Alabama 1975, is
30 amended to read as follows:
31 "§32-6-130
32 (a) Any veteran, as defined in Section 31-5-1, who is a
33 resident of this state and who is suffering a physical
34 disability which that requires that any motor vehicle he or
35 she operates be equipped with special mechanical control
36 devices or whose physical disability exceeds 50 percent, upon
37 application accompanied by proof of eligibility to the judge
38 of probate of the county for the special license plates
39 provided herein, as may be prescribed by the State Department
40 of Revenue, and upon payment of five dollars ($5), unless
41 exempted from the payment of such the fee by law, shall be
42 issued license plates, by the judge of probate as provided by
43 law, for private or pleasure motor vehicles, upon which, in.
44 In lieu of the number now prescribed by law shall be inscribed
45 in legible letters the words, "Disabled Veteran," and shall
46 designate the branch of service and an identifying number as
47 prescribed by the State Department of Revenue shall be
48 designated. The plates shall not be transferable. The fee
49 established by this section shall be in lieu of the regular
50 license fee now required for motor vehicles and shall be used
51 to defray the cost of issuing the distinctive license plates.
52 (b) In lieu of applying the registration fee of five
53 dollars ($5) to the disabled veteran license plate category
54 provided for in subsection (a), the registration fee may be
55 applied to any license plate category in which the disabled
56 veteran qualifies to obtain a license plate. Any additional
Page 2
veteran qualifies to obtain a license plate. Any additional
57 fee required for a plate from a distinctive or personalized
58 license plate category shall still be required in order to
59 obtain the plate.
60 (c)(1) There is established a "Disabled Veteran Spouse"
61 license plate to be issued to a surviving spouse of a
62 "Disabled Veteran" license plate recipient pursuant to this
63 subsection.
64 (2) The surviving spouse of a deceased "Disabled
65 Veteran" license plate recipient shall be issued a new
66 "Disabled Veteran Spouse" license plate for one private motor
67 vehicle owned by the surviving spouse for the remainder of the
68 spouse's lifetime or until the spouse's remarriage, upon:
69 a. Returning the "Disabled Veteran" license plate to
70 the judge of probate or other issuing official;
71 b. Providing proof of marriage to the disabled veteran;
72 and
73 c. Paying an issuance fee of five dollars ($5) in lieu
74 of all other regular fees for license plates.
75 (3) This subsection shall not be construed to permit a
76 "Disabled Veteran Spouse" license plate recipient to park in
77 any parking zone designated reserved for handicapped
78 individuals with disabilities."
79 Section 2. This act shall become effective on October
80 1, 2027.
