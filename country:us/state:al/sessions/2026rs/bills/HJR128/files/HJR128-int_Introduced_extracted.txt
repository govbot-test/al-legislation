4 HJR___ HONORING THE 250TH ANNIVERSARY OF THE DECLARATION OF
5 INDEPENDENCE AND RECOMMITTING TO THE FOUNDING IDEALS OF
6 VIRTUE AND EDUCATION.
7
8 WHEREAS, the 250th Anniversary of the Declaration of
9 Independence represents an ideal time to reflect and
10 reengage with the critical foundations of our nation; and
11 WHEREAS, our nation's Founding Fathers repeatedly
12 emphasized the necessity of an educated populace, and in
13 particular, a populace educated in the fundamental concepts
14 of virtue; and
15 WHEREAS, the continuation of any republic
16 fundamentally depends upon an educated and virtuous public;
17 and
18 WHEREAS, in too many instances, our schools have
19 strayed from ensuring that students are regularly exposed to
20 the timeless and fundamental concepts of virtue, such as
21 honesty, courage, perseverance, work, and compassion; and
22 WHEREAS, we commend those who have, throughout their
23 careers, worked to advance character and virtue education, a
24 leader among them being former United States Secretary of
25 Education William J. Bennett, author of The Book of Virtues
26 and The Children's Book of Virtues; and
27 WHEREAS, at this critical juncture in our history,
28 the State of Alabama should recommit to infusing robust
