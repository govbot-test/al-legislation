HB161 Enrolled
1 Enrolled, An Act,
2 Relating to consumer protection; to require an app
3 store provider to take certain actions regarding age
4 verification, parental notification, and data protection; to
5 prohibit an app store provider or developer from taking
6 certain actions that allow minors to access apps without
7 parental consent; to require the Attorney General to adopt
8 rules; and to authorize the Attorney General to bring an
9 action for a violation as a deceptive trade practice.
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
11 Section 1. For the purposes of this act, the following
12 terms have the following meanings:

14 mobile device.

17 16 years of age; (iii) at least 16 years of age but less than
18 18 years of age; or (iv) at least 18 years of age.
19 (3) AGE CATEGORY DATA. Information about a user's age
20 category which is collected by an app store developer and
21 shared with a developer.

24 age groups.

234 Section 11. (a) Any knowing or reckless violation of
235 this act is deemed a deceptive trade practice actionable under
236 Chapter 19 of Title 8 of the Code of Alabama 1975. Provided,
237 however, the Attorney General shall have the exclusive
238 jurisdiction to bring an action pursuant to Chapter 19 of
239 Title 8, Code of Alabama 1975. If the Attorney General has
240 reason to believe that an entity is in violation of this act,
241 the Attorney General may bring an action against the entity
242 for an unfair or deceptive trade practice. In addition to
243 other remedies available under Chapter 19 of Title 8 of the
244 Code of Alabama 1975, the Attorney General may collect a civil
245 penalty of up to seven thousand five hundred dollars ($7,500)
246 per violation, reasonable attorney fees, and court costs.
247 (b) If a violation described in subsection (a) is part
248 of a consistent pattern of knowing or reckless conduct, the
249 Attorney General may seek punitive damages against the entity.
