5 A BILL
6 TO BE ENTITLED
7 AN ACT
8
9 Relating to crimes and offenses; to prohibit the
10 operation of an unmanned aircraft system near a ticketed
11 entertainment event; to provide penalties for violations; and
12 to provide exemptions.
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA:
14 Section 1. (a) For the purposes of this section, the
15 following terms have the following meanings:
16 (1) TICKETED ENTERTAINMENT EVENT. Any music, sporting,
17 or performing arts event held in a location with gated entries
18 or barriers that prevent access to the general public and in
19 which a revocable license that has been issued by an owner,
20 operator, or lessee of the property is required for an
21 individual to attend the event.
22 (2) UNMANNED AIRCRAFT SYSTEM. As defined in Section
23 13A-7-4.3, Code of Alabama 1975.
24 (b)(1) It shall be unlawful to operate an unmanned
25 aircraft system within 400 feet of or above a ticketed
26 entertainment event.
27 (2) A violation of this subsection is punishable by a
28 fine of up to five hundred dollars ($500).
Page 1
fine of up to five hundred dollars ($500).

29 (3) A second or subsequent violation of this subsection
30 is a Class A misdemeanor.
31 (c) Subsection (b) shall not apply to an operator of an
32 unmanned aircraft system in any of the following scenarios:
33 (1) The operator has obtained consent by any person
34 with legal authority over the ticketed entertainment event.
35 (2) The operator is authorized by federal regulations
36 to operate an unmanned aircraft system and is operating the
37 system in a lawful manner consistent with federal regulations.
38 (3) The operator is an employee of the property where
39 the event is being held and is conducting official business.
40 (4) The operator is an employee of a utility service
41 provider and is conducting official business.
42 (5) The operator is an owner of private property who is
43 operating an unmanned aircraft system that he or she owns
44 above the private property, where the operation is consistent
45 with federal regulations and is not done for commercial
46 purposes, to convey or communicate a message, or to record for
47 the purposes of commercial broadcasting, commercial
48 publication, monitoring, or to otherwise interfere with a
49 ticketed entertainment event.
50 (6) The operator is an employee or volunteer with a
51 state agency, law enforcement agency, fire department, or
52 emergency management agency and is conducting official
53 business.
54 (7) The ticketed entertainment event is a middle school
55 or high school sports event.
