7 WHEREAS, specialty public high schools are
8 state-funded high schools that respond to education,
9 cultural, and workforce demands; and
10 WHEREAS, Alabama's specialty public high schools
11 focus on specialized curriculum and include the Alabama
12 School of Fine Arts, the Alabama School of Math and Science,
13 the Alabama School of Cyber Technology and Engineering, and
14 the Alabama School of Healthcare Sciences, slated to open in
15 the fall of 2026; and
16 WHEREAS, these specialty schools develop thousands of
17 leaders in the arts, STEM, technology, and other
18 professions, providing areas of education and hands-on
19 learning in areas including, but not limited to, biology,
20 chemistry, creative writing, cyber technology, dance, math,
21 science, music, engineering, theatre arts, healthcare
22 sciences, visual arts, data science, nursing, biomedical
23 science, and leadership; and
24 WHEREAS, these schools each have a distinctive
25 mission, but share a common goal in preparing students for
26 specific career fields, college, and life, ultimately
27 strengthening Alabama's workforce; and
28 WHEREAS, it is necessary for these unique schools to

31 WHEREAS, collaboration among these specialty public
32 high schools would be beneficial to the schools, students,

34 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH

36 specialty public high schools to form a consortium to
37 encourage collaboration, partnerships, and information
