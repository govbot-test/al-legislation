4 HJR___ COMMENDING REPRESENTATIVE PHILLIP ENSLER ON HIS
5 PROFESSIONAL ACCOMPLISHMENTS IN THE ALABAMA LEGISLATURE.
6
7 WHEREAS, it is with highest admiration and deepest
8 respect that we commend the Honorable Phillip Ensler,
9 currently serving as a Democratic member of the Alabama
10 House of Representatives, representing District 74, for his
11 many accomplishments and dedicated service to the people of
12 the State of Alabama; and
13 WHEREAS, upon his election in 2022, Representative
14 Ensler made history as the first Jewish lawmaker in the
15 Alabama Legislature in over four decades and the first
16 Democrat to flip a House seat in over a decade, bringing a
17 unique and vital perspective to the State House; and
18 WHEREAS, as an undergraduate student at George
19 Washington University in Washington D.C., he served as an
20 intern during the Obama Administration; Representative
21 Ensler first came to Montgomery as a Teach For America corps
22 member, where he taught social studies at the former Lee
23 High School, an experience that forged his lifelong bond
24 with the community and ignited his passion for educational
25 equity; and
26 WHEREAS, in 2012, he started a program called
27 Marching On, in which some of his students and he would fly
28 to Washington, D.C., often the students' first time on an
Page 1
to Washington, D.C., often the students' first time on an
HJR310 INTRODUCED
29 airplane, to meet with leaders, tour colleges, and learn
30 about career opportunities; he has continued leading the
31 program each year since and expanded it to include students
32 from all five of Montgomery's traditional public high
33 schools; and
34 WHEREAS, in order to become an even more effective
35 agent for social change, he earned his law degree from
36 Cardozo School of Law; after passing the Alabama Bar, he
37 served as Policy Counsel for Alabama Appleseed Center for
38 Law and Justice, and later he served as Montgomery Mayor
39 Steven Reed's Senior Policy Advisor; and
40 WHEREAS, recently, it was the honor of a lifetime for
41 Representative Ensler to serve as Claudette Colvin's
42 attorney in her successful attempt to expunge her court
43 record from being the first person arrested for refusing to
44 give up her seat on a Montgomery bus in 1955; and
45 WHEREAS, currently, Representative Ensler serves as
46 Executive Director of the Jewish Federation of Central
47 Alabama, where he develops, implements, and oversees
48 finances, programming, and fundraising; he also leads
49 efforts to combat anti-semitism and build multi-faith
50 coalitions to combat racism and foster acceptance of all
51 people; and
52 WHEREAS, throughout his service in the Alabama
53 Legislature, Representative Ensler has been an advocate for
54 members of the community and has served on the Commerce and
55 Small Business Committee, Financial Services Committee,
56 Insurance Committee, Local Legislation Committee, and the
Page 2
Insurance Committee, Local Legislation Committee, and the
HJR310 INTRODUCED
57 Montgomery County Legislation Committee; and
58 WHEREAS, Representative Ensler has been instrumental
59 in advancing policies that have made a significant impact in
60 the lives of Alabamians, including his work on gun violence
61 prevention through the glock switch ban and securing $2.5
62 million in funding for mental health in public schools in
63 the 2026 Education Trust Fund budget; and
64 WHEREAS, Representative Ensler is widely admired for
65 his involvement in the community and is seen as an asset to
66 the Democratic party; his work has consistently transcended
67 partisan lines, focusing on the human element of policy,
68 from ensuring students have resources to succeed to ensuring
69 families feel safe in their homes; and
70 WHEREAS, Representative Phillip Ensler has served the
71 people of District 74 with a profound sense of duty,
72 informed by a life dedicated to social justice, education,
73 and the rule of law; now therefore,
74 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
75 HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That Representative Phillip
76 Ensler is highly honored and commended for his outstanding
77 record of service to the people of House District 74 and the
78 State of Alabama and for his invaluable contributions over
79 the past four years.
